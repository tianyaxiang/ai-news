# KG-TRACE: A Neuro-Symbolic Framework for Mechanistic Grounding in Antimicrobial Resistance Prediction
# KG-TRACE：用于抗菌药物耐药性预测中机制溯源的神经符号框架

**Abstract:** While WGS-based AMR prediction has reached high accuracy, existing models lack a mechanism to ground neural attributions in established biological pathways. We present KG-TRACE, a novel neuro-symbolic framework that integrates the WHO mutation knowledge graph (KG) as a structured biological constraint on a neural genomic model.

**摘要：** 尽管基于全基因组测序（WGS）的抗菌药物耐药性（AMR）预测已达到很高的准确率，但现有模型缺乏将神经归因（neural attributions）与既定生物学通路相结合的机制。我们提出了 KG-TRACE，这是一个新颖的神经符号框架，它将世界卫生组织（WHO）的突变知识图谱（KG）作为结构化生物约束，整合到神经基因组模型中。

Unlike existing methods that learn statistical patterns in isolation, KG-TRACE fuses genomic features and RotatE-based KG embeddings through a learned epistemic trust gate, dynamically weighting neural evidence against symbolic biological knowledge.

与孤立学习统计模式的现有方法不同，KG-TRACE 通过一个习得的认知信任门（epistemic trust gate），将基因组特征与基于 RotatE 的知识图谱嵌入进行融合，从而在神经证据与符号化生物学知识之间进行动态加权。

Evaluated on the CRyPTIC M. tuberculosis cohort, KG-TRACE achieves an AUROC of 0.9760 for isoniazid, achieving competitive accuracy while its primary value lies in symbolic grounding, not predictive uplift.

在 CRyPTIC 结核分枝杆菌队列上的评估显示，KG-TRACE 在异烟肼预测任务中达到了 0.9760 的 AUROC 值。该模型在实现竞争性准确率的同时，其核心价值在于符号化溯源（symbolic grounding），而非单纯的预测性能提升。

More importantly, we introduce the Biological Grounding Ratio (BGR), a dataset-level metric that quantifies alignment between neural attributions and established biology. Our framework achieves a 92.5% symbolic coverage of isoniazid-resistant predictions and effectively identifies MDR co-occurrence artifacts by issuing laboratory follow-up flags for 'UNCERTAIN' cases.

更重要的是，我们引入了生物学溯源比率（Biological Grounding Ratio, BGR），这是一种数据集层面的指标，用于量化神经归因与既定生物学知识之间的一致性。我们的框架实现了 92.5% 的异烟肼耐药预测符号覆盖率，并通过为“不确定（UNCERTAIN）”病例发布实验室后续跟进标记，有效地识别了多重耐药（MDR）共现伪影。

We demonstrate that neuro-symbolic grounding provides a verifiable audit trail for clinicians, bridging the gap between predictive accuracy and clinical trust.

我们证明了神经符号溯源为临床医生提供了一条可验证的审计追踪路径，弥合了预测准确性与临床信任之间的鸿沟。
