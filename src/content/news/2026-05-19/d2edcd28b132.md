# Minerva-Ego: Spatiotemporal Hints for Egocentric Video Understanding
# Minerva-Ego：用于第一人称视频理解的时空提示

**Abstract:**
**摘要：**

Video reasoning models are a core component of egocentric and embodied agents. However, standard benchmarks for assessing models provide only evaluation of the output (e.g. the answer to a question), without evaluation of intermediate reasoning steps, and most provide answers only in the text domain.
视频推理模型是第一人称（Egocentric）和具身智能体（Embodied agents）的核心组件。然而，目前评估模型的标准基准测试仅提供对输出结果（例如问题的答案）的评估，而缺乏对中间推理步骤的评估，且大多数基准测试仅在文本领域提供答案。

We introduce Minerva-Ego, a benchmark for evaluating complex egocentric visual reasoning. We extend recent high-quality video data sources recorded from egocentric / embodied settings with a set of challenging, multi-step multimodal questions and spatiotemporally-dense human-annotated reasoning traces.
我们推出了 Minerva-Ego，这是一个用于评估复杂第一人称视觉推理的基准测试。我们扩展了近期从第一人称/具身场景中录制的高质量视频数据源，并加入了一系列具有挑战性的多步多模态问题以及时空密集的人工标注推理轨迹。

Benchmarking experiments show that state-of-the-art models still have a large gap to human performance. To investigate this gap in detail, we annotate each reasoning trace in the dataset with the objects of interest required to solve the question, as spatiotemporal mask annotations.
基准测试实验表明，当前最先进的模型与人类表现之间仍存在巨大差距。为了详细研究这一差距，我们为数据集中的每个推理轨迹标注了解决问题所需的感兴趣对象，并以时空掩码（Spatiotemporal mask）的形式进行标注。

Through extensive evaluations, we identify that prompting frontier models with hints of 'where' and 'when' to look yields substantial improvements in performance. Minerva-Ego can be downloaded at this https URL.
通过广泛的评估，我们发现通过提示前沿模型“在哪里”和“什么时候”观察，可以显著提升其性能。Minerva-Ego 可通过此链接下载。
