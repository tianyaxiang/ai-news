# COPRA: Conditional Parameter Adaptation with Reinforcement Learning for Video Anomaly Detection

**COPRA：用于视频异常检测的强化学习条件参数自适应框架**

Vision-language models (VLMs) have shown strong performance in video anomaly detection (VAD) while providing interpretable predictions. However, existing VLM-based VAD methods suffer from a fundamental mismatch between training and inference in both data distribution and model configuration.

视觉-语言模型（VLM）在视频异常检测（VAD）中表现出了强大的性能，并能提供可解释的预测结果。然而，现有的基于 VLM 的 VAD 方法在数据分布和模型配置上，训练与推理之间存在根本性的不匹配。

First, most approaches rely on static post-training adaptation, limiting generalization under distribution shifts such as unseen environments or anomaly types. Second, they train VLMs on sparse frames from long videos, but perform inference on densely sampled short segments, creating inconsistencies between training and testing.

首先，大多数方法依赖于静态的训练后自适应，这限制了模型在面对未见环境或异常类型等分布偏移时的泛化能力。其次，这些方法通常在长视频的稀疏帧上训练 VLM，却在密集采样的短片段上进行推理，从而导致了训练与测试之间的不一致。

To address these limitations, we propose COPRA, a conditional parameter adaptation framework for VLM-based VAD. Instead of fixed prompts or shared parameter updates, COPRA generates input-specific parameter updates to dynamically adapt a frozen VLM for each video segment during both training and inference.

为了解决这些局限性，我们提出了 COPRA，这是一个用于基于 VLM 的 VAD 的条件参数自适应框架。COPRA 不再使用固定的提示词或共享参数更新，而是生成针对特定输入的参数更新，从而在训练和推理过程中为每个视频片段动态调整冻结的 VLM。

Experiments show strong performance on standard VAD benchmarks, consistently outperforming static baselines in both in-domain and cross-domain settings. Moreover, COPRA generalizes beyond VAD to unseen tasks such as multiple-choice Video Question Answering and Dense Captioning. These results highlight COPRA as an effective weight-space generation framework for scalable, adaptive, and context-aware video understanding.

实验表明，该方法在标准 VAD 基准测试中表现优异，在域内和跨域设置下均持续优于静态基准模型。此外，COPRA 的泛化能力不仅限于 VAD，还能扩展到多项选择视频问答和密集字幕生成等未见任务。这些结果凸显了 COPRA 作为一种有效的权重空间生成框架，在可扩展、自适应和上下文感知的视频理解方面的潜力。
