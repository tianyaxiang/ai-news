# LLM Summarizers Skip the Identification Step
# 大语言模型摘要工具跳过了“识别”步骤

A practitioner's argument that meeting summarizers fail in the same way regressions fail when you skip the part where you ask what the data can support.
一位从业者指出：会议摘要工具的失败，与回归分析中跳过“评估数据支撑能力”这一步骤所导致的失败如出一辙。

A meeting summarizer takes a five-minute exchange and returns eight clean sections. Decisions. Action items. Risks. Open questions. Each section reads like it was written by someone who was paying attention.
会议摘要工具接收一段五分钟的对话，并返回八个条理清晰的部分：决策、行动项、风险、待定问题。每一部分读起来都像是出自一位专注的记录者之手。

Read the underlying transcript, though, and you find that two of those sections were inferred from a single ambiguous sentence, one was invented entirely, and three were pattern-matched from the model’s prior on what a meeting summary should contain. Confident, formatted, structurally indistinguishable from a summary of a meeting where those things actually happened.
然而，当你阅读原始转录文本时，会发现其中两个部分仅凭一句模棱两可的话推断而来，一个完全是凭空捏造的，还有三个则是根据模型对“会议摘要应包含什么”的先验知识进行模式匹配生成的。它们看起来自信、格式规范，在结构上与真实发生的会议摘要毫无二致。

This is not a hallucination problem in the usual sense. The model is not making up a fact about the world. It is making up a fact about the meeting. And the failure mode is not visible in the output. It is just confident-sounding text that the reader cannot easily verify against the source.
这并非通常意义上的“幻觉”问题。模型并非在编造关于世界的客观事实，而是在编造关于会议的事实。这种失败模式在输出结果中是不可见的——它只是一段听起来很自信、但读者难以根据原始来源轻松核实的文本。

There is a name for this failure mode in another field, and it is older than language models. It is what happens when you do estimation without identification.
在另一个领域，这种失败模式有一个专门的术语，且其历史比语言模型更为久远。这就是当你进行“估计”却跳过“识别”时所发生的情况。

This article is not a new summarization benchmark. It is an argument for a design pattern that I have not seen treated as the central design constraint in AI engineering literature: treat LLM-generated summaries as structured claims over a source, require each claim to declare its support category, and constrain review stages so they can only weaken unsupported claims rather than make the output smoother.
本文并非要提出一个新的摘要基准测试。我旨在论证一种设计模式，据我所知，它尚未被视为人工智能工程文献中的核心设计约束：将大模型生成的摘要视为基于原始来源的结构化主张，要求每一项主张声明其支撑类别，并限制审查阶段——审查只能削弱那些缺乏支撑的主张，而不是让输出结果看起来更圆滑。

I will walk through what that looks like in practice, what it produces, and where it breaks.
我将详细阐述这种模式在实践中是什么样子，它能产生什么结果，以及它在何处会失效。

### The missing step
### 缺失的步骤

Causal inference is the analytical tradition that formalizes the difference between identifying a quantity and estimating one. Identification is the argument that the data you have can support the claim you want to make. Estimation is the procedure that produces a number once identification is settled.
因果推断是一种分析传统，它正式区分了“识别”一个量与“估计”一个量之间的差异。“识别”是指论证你所拥有的数据能够支撑你想要提出的主张；而“估计”则是指在识别完成后，得出具体数值的过程。

The order is not negotiable. You cannot estimate a treatment effect you have not first argued is identifiable from your observational data, because the resulting number is meaningless. It looks like an effect. It is not an effect.
这个顺序是不容商量的。如果你没有先论证处理效应可以从观测数据中识别出来，你就无法对其进行估计，因为最终得出的数字毫无意义。它看起来像是一种效应，但实际上并非如此。

Practitioners who work in observational settings spend a substantial fraction of their time on identification. They draw causal graphs. They argue about confounders. They distinguish between what the data can support and what the data cannot. The estimation step, when it finally comes, is often the easy part.
在观测环境下工作的从业者会将大量时间花在“识别”上。他们绘制因果图，争论混杂因素，区分数据能支撑什么和不能支撑什么。而最后的“估计”步骤，往往反而是最简单的部分。

Now consider what an LLM summarizer does. It receives a transcript. It produces structured claims about the content of that transcript: decisions made, commitments accepted, risks raised, next steps assigned. Each claim is, in a real sense, an estimate of a latent quantity. The decision was made or it was not. The commitment was accepted or it was not. The summary is asserting a value for each of these quantities.
现在考虑一下大模型摘要工具的工作方式。它接收一份转录文本，并针对其内容生成结构化的主张：做出的决策、接受的承诺、提出的风险、分配的后续步骤。从本质上讲，每一项主张都是对潜在事实的“估计”。决策要么做出了，要么没做出；承诺要么接受了，要么没接受。摘要实际上是在为这些量断言一个值。

There is no identification step. The model does not ask whether the transcript contains enough evidence to support the claim. It produces the claim because the format calls for one. LLM summarization behaves like observational analysis, but it is often deployed without anything resembling an identification step.
这里缺少了“识别”步骤。模型不会询问转录文本中是否包含足够的证据来支撑该主张。它生成主张仅仅是因为格式要求这样做。大模型摘要的表现类似于观测分析，但它在部署时往往缺乏任何类似“识别”的环节。

The AI engineering literature has not been silent on the underlying problem. Hallucination detection, calibrated uncertainty, selective prediction and abstention, RAG grounding, citation verification, factual consistency, and claim verification: each of these is a serious line of work, and each addresses a real layer of the failure.
人工智能工程文献并非对这一根本问题视而不见。幻觉检测、校准不确定性、选择性预测与弃权、RAG（检索增强生成）溯源、引用验证、事实一致性以及主张验证：这些都是严肃的研究方向，每一个都针对失败的某个层面。

What they have in common is that they treat fabrication as a model behavior to be measured, scored, or suppressed after the fact. Identification is a different layer. It does not score the output for trustworthiness. It changes what the model is allowed to assert in the first place by requiring every claim to declare what it is and where it came from.
它们的共同点在于，它们都将“编造”视为一种需要在事后进行测量、评分或抑制的模型行为。“识别”则处于不同的层面。它不对输出的可信度进行评分，而是通过要求每一项主张声明其性质和来源，从源头上改变模型被允许断言的内容。

The two layers are complementary. A pipeline that does identification well still benefits from calibration and grounding work downstream. A pipeline that does only the downstream work is filtering output that should never have been produced in the form it was produced.
这两个层面是互补的。一个做好“识别”的流水线，依然可以从下游的校准和溯源工作中获益。而一个只做下游工作的流水线，实际上是在过滤那些本不该以当前形式生成的输出。

### What identification looks like for a transcript
### 对于转录文本，“识别”是什么样的？

Identification in observational data is a question about what the data can support. Identification for a transcript is the same question, narrowed to a specific source. Given this transcript, what can be observed directly, what can be inferred with stated assumptions, and what cannot be supported at all? That is the whole move.
观测数据中的“识别”是关于数据能支撑什么的问题。对于转录文本，“识别”也是同样的问题，只是范围缩小到了特定的来源。给定这份转录文本，什么是可以直接观察到的？什么是可以通过既定假设推断出来的？什么又是完全无法支撑的？这就是核心所在。

Every claim a summarizer produces should declare which of those three categories it belongs to. Observed claims point to a specific span of the transcript and assert nothing beyond what that span says. Inferred claims declare the assumption being made and the evidence the inference is bridging. Recommendations declare that they are the model’s suggestion, not the participants’ decision.
摘要工具生成的每一项主张都应声明其属于上述三类中的哪一类。“观察类”主张应指向转录文本的特定片段，且断言内容不超过该片段本身；“推断类”主张应声明所做的假设以及推断所依据的证据；“建议类”主张应明确这是模型的建议，而非参与者的决策。

A summarizer that cannot place a claim into one of those categories has no business producing the claim. The right output in that case is not a smoother claim. It is no claim.
如果一个摘要工具无法将某项主张归入这些类别，它就不应该生成该主张。在这种情况下，正确的输出不是让主张看起来更圆滑，而是不生成该主张。

This is uncomfortable for the consumer of summaries, because it means many sections will be empty when the underlying conversation was thin. That discomfort is the point. It is information. It tells the reader that the meeting did not, in fact, produce eight sections of substance, regardless of what the summarizer wanted to write.
这对摘要的使用者来说可能不太舒服，因为这意味着当原始对话内容单薄时，许多部分将是空白的。但这种不适感正是重点所在。这本身就是一种信息——它告诉读者，无论摘要工具想写什么，会议实际上并没有产生八个实质性部分。
