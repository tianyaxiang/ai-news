# Batch or Stream? The Eternal Data Processing Dilemma
# 批处理还是流处理？永恒的数据处理困境

"Should we process our data in batches or in real-time?" It's not batch vs. stream: it's "when does the answer matter?"
“我们应该进行批处理还是实时处理数据？”这并非批处理与流处理的对立，而是“答案何时才具有价值？”的问题。

If you’ve spent any time in the data engineering world, you’ve likely encountered this debate at least once. Maybe twice. Ok, probably a dozen times😉 “Should we process our data in batches or in real-time?” And if you’re anything like me, you’ve noticed that the answer usually starts with: “Well, it depends…”
如果你在数据工程领域待过一段时间，你很可能至少遇到过一次这样的争论。也许两次。好吧，可能已经有十几次了😉。“我们应该进行批处理还是实时处理数据？”如果你和我一样，你会发现答案通常以这句话开头：“嗯，这取决于……”

Which is true. It does depend. But “it depends” is only useful if you actually know what it depends on. And that’s the gap I want to fill with this article. Not another theoretical comparison of batch vs. stream processing (I hope you already know the basics). Instead, I want to give you a practical framework for deciding which approach makes sense for your specific scenario, and then show you how both paths look when implemented in Microsoft Fabric.
这是事实，确实取决于具体情况。但只有当你真正知道它取决于什么时，“取决于具体情况”这句话才有意义。这正是我想通过本文填补的空白。我不想再进行一次关于批处理与流处理的理论对比（希望你已经掌握了基础知识）。相反，我想为你提供一个实用的框架，帮助你决定哪种方法适合你的特定场景，并展示这两种路径在 Microsoft Fabric 中实现时的样子。

It’s not batch vs. stream: it’s “when does the answer matter?” Let me skip dry definitions and jump straight to what actually separates these two approaches: the value of freshness.
这并非批处理与流处理的对立，而是“答案何时才具有价值？”的问题。让我跳过枯燥的定义，直接切入区分这两种方法的关键：数据的时效性价值。

Every piece of data has a shelf life. Not in the sense that it expires and becomes useless, but in the sense that its business value changes over time. A fraudulent credit card transaction detected in 200 milliseconds? Priceless – you just prevented a loss. The same fraud detected 6 hours later in a nightly batch job? Useful for reporting, but the money is already gone.
每一条数据都有保质期。这并不是说它会过期并变得毫无用处，而是指其商业价值会随时间而改变。如果在 200 毫秒内检测到信用卡欺诈交易？那是无价的——你刚刚阻止了一次损失。如果同样的欺诈行为在 6 小时后的夜间批处理作业中才被检测到？这对报告分析有用，但钱已经没了。

On the flip side, a monthly sales report generated from yesterday’s data versus data that’s 3 minutes old? In most organizations, nobody can tell the difference (and probably nobody cares). The business decisions based on that report happen in meetings scheduled days in advance, not in milliseconds after the data arrives.
反过来说，一份基于昨天数据生成的月度销售报告，与一份基于 3 分钟前数据生成的报告相比如何？在大多数组织中，没人能看出区别（而且可能也没人关心）。基于该报告的商业决策是在提前几天安排的会议中做出的，而不是在数据到达后的几毫秒内做出的。

So, the first question isn’t “batch or stream?” The first question is: how quickly does someone (or something) need to act on this data for it to matter? If the answer is “seconds or less”, you’re in streaming territory. If the answer is “hours or days”, batch is likely your friend. And if the answer is “somewhere in between”… Congratulations, you’re in the most interesting (and most common) gray area, which we’ll explore shortly.
因此，第一个问题不是“批处理还是流处理？”，而是：为了让数据产生价值，某人（或某物）需要多快对这些数据采取行动？如果答案是“几秒钟或更短”，那么你处于流处理领域。如果答案是“几小时或几天”，那么批处理很可能是你的首选。如果答案是“介于两者之间”……恭喜你，你处于最有趣（也是最常见）的灰色地带，我们稍后将探讨这一点。

### The trade-offs / 权衡取舍

You know what the most uncomfortable truth about streaming is? It sounds amazing on paper. Who wouldn’t want real-time data? It’s like asking “would you prefer your coffee now or in 6 hours?” But the reality is more nuanced than that. Let’s walk through the trade-offs that actually matter when you’re making this decision.
你知道关于流处理最令人不安的事实是什么吗？它在理论上听起来很棒。谁不想要实时数据呢？这就像问“你想要现在喝咖啡，还是 6 小时后再喝？”但现实情况要复杂得多。让我们来看看在做决定时真正重要的权衡因素。

**Cost / 成本**
I hear you, I hear you: “Nikola, how much more expensive is streaming?” Unfortunately, there’s no single number I can give you, but the pattern is consistent: streaming infrastructure is almost always more expensive than batch processing for the same volume of data. Why? Because streaming requires resources to be always on, listening, processing, and writing continuously. Batch processing, on the other hand, spins up, does its work, and shuts down. You pay for the compute only when the job runs.
我听到了，我听到了：“Nikola，流处理到底贵多少？”遗憾的是，我无法给出一个确切的数字，但规律是一致的：对于相同的数据量，流处理基础设施几乎总是比批处理更昂贵。为什么？因为流处理需要资源始终处于开启状态，持续进行监听、处理和写入。而批处理则是启动、完成工作、然后关闭。你只需为作业运行时的计算付费。

Think of it like a restaurant kitchen. A batch kitchen opens at specific hours – the staff arrives, preps, cooks, cleans up, and goes home. A streaming kitchen is open 24/7 with staff always standing by, ready to cook the moment an order arrives. Even during the quiet hours at 3 AM when nobody’s ordering, someone is still there, waiting. That waiting costs money.
把它想象成餐厅厨房。批处理厨房在特定时间营业——员工到达、准备、烹饪、清理，然后回家。流处理厨房则是 24/7 全天候营业，员工随时待命，订单一到立即烹饪。即使在凌晨 3 点没人点餐的安静时段，依然有人在那里等待。这种等待是需要成本的。

Does this mean streaming is always more expensive? Not necessarily. If your data arrives continuously and you need to process it continuously anyway, the cost difference narrows. But if your data arrives in predictable bursts (daily file drops, hourly API calls), batch processing lets you align your compute spend with those bursts.
这是否意味着流处理总是更贵？不一定。如果你的数据是持续到达的，并且你无论如何都需要持续处理它，那么成本差异就会缩小。但如果你的数据是以可预测的突发方式到达（如每日文件投递、每小时 API 调用），批处理允许你将计算支出与这些突发流量对齐。

**Complexity / 复杂性**
Batch processing is conceptually simpler. You have a defined input, a defined transformation, and a defined output. If something fails, you re-run the job. The data isn’t going anywhere, it’s sitting in a file or a table, patiently waiting.
批处理在概念上更简单。你有明确的输入、明确的转换和明确的输出。如果出现故障，你只需重新运行作业。数据不会丢失，它静静地躺在文件或表中，耐心地等待。

Streaming? Things get trickier. You’re dealing with data that arrives continuously, potentially out of order, potentially with duplicates, and potentially with gaps. What happens when a sensor goes offline for 5 minutes and then dumps all its buffered readings at once? What happens when two events arrive in the wrong order? What happens when the processing engine crashes mid-stream? Do you replay from the beginning? From a checkpoint? How do you ensure exactly-once processing? These are solvable problems, and modern streaming platforms handle most of them well. But these are additional problems that simply don’t exist in batch processing. Complexity isn’t a reason to avoid streaming, it’s simply a reason to make sure you actually need streaming before you commit to it.
流处理呢？情况就复杂多了。你处理的是持续到达的数据，它们可能乱序、可能重复、也可能存在缺失。当传感器离线 5 分钟后一次性倾倒所有缓存读数时会发生什么？当两个事件以错误的顺序到达时会发生什么？当处理引擎在流处理过程中崩溃时会发生什么？你是从头开始重放吗？还是从检查点开始？你如何确保“恰好一次”（exactly-once）处理？这些都是可解决的问题，现代流处理平台也能很好地处理其中大部分。但这些都是批处理中根本不存在的额外问题。复杂性并不是拒绝流处理的理由，它只是提醒你在投入使用前，确保你确实需要流处理。

**Correctness / 正确性**
Batch processing has a natural advantage in correctness, because it operates on complete datasets. When your batch job runs at 2 AM, it has access to all the data from the previous day. Every late-arriving record, every correction, every update, it’s all there. The job can compute aggregates, joins, and transformations against the full picture.
批处理在正确性方面具有天然优势，因为它操作的是完整的数据集。当你的批处理作业在凌晨 2 点运行时，它可以访问前一天的所有数据。每一条迟到的记录、每一次修正、每一次更新，都在那里。作业可以针对全貌进行聚合、连接和转换计算。

Streaming operates on incomplete data by definition. You’re processing records as they arrive, which means your results are always provisional. That daily revenue number you computed at 11:59 PM? A few late-arriving transactions might change it by the time the clock strikes midnight. Windowing strategies and watermarks help manage this, but they add yet another layer of decision-making. Again, this isn’t a reason to avoid streaming. It’s a reason to understand that streaming results and batch results might differ, and your architecture needs to account for that.
流处理从定义上讲就是基于不完整数据运行的。你是在记录到达时进行处理，这意味着你的结果始终是临时的。你在晚上 11:59 计算出的每日收入数字？几笔迟到的交易可能会在午夜钟声敲响时改变它。窗口策略和水印有助于管理这一点，但它们又增加了一层决策难度。同样，这不是拒绝流处理的理由。这只是让你明白，流处理结果和批处理结果可能会有所不同，你的架构需要考虑到这一点。
