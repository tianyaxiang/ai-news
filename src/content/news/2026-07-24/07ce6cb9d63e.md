# SUM: Unified Geometric Surgery on Spatio-Temporal Adaptation Vectors for Federated Class Incremental Learning
# SUM：面向联邦类增量学习的时空适应向量统一几何手术

**Abstract:** Real-world intelligent systems often require both distributed collaboration across data-isolated clients and continual adaptation to evolving tasks. This setting naturally gives rise to Federated Class Incremental Learning (FCIL), which combines Federated Learning (FL) and Continual Learning (CL).

**摘要：** 现实世界的智能系统通常既需要跨数据孤立客户端的分布式协作，也需要对不断演进的任务进行持续适应。这种环境自然产生了联邦类增量学习（FCIL），它结合了联邦学习（FL）和持续学习（CL）。

However, their combination introduces two coupled sources of interference: spatial interference from heterogeneous clients and temporal interference from sequential tasks, jointly leading to Spatial-Temporal Catastrophic Forgetting (ST-CF). Existing approaches typically address spatial and temporal interference with separate mechanisms, often incurring additional client-side computation or communication, while leaving directional interactions among updates during aggregation unregulated.

然而，两者的结合引入了两个耦合的干扰源：来自异构客户端的空间干扰和来自顺序任务的时间干扰，共同导致了时空灾难性遗忘（ST-CF）。现有的方法通常使用独立的机制来解决空间和时间干扰，这往往会产生额外的客户端计算或通信开销，同时在聚合过程中对更新之间的方向性交互缺乏约束。

In this paper, we reinterpret FCIL as a unified multi-task learning problem, where both client and task updates are represented as adaptation vectors in a shared parameter space. Based on this view, we propose Unified Geometric Surgery on Spatio-Temporal Adaptation Vectors (SUM), a purely server-side framework that performs geometric surgery on adaptation vectors during aggregation.

在本文中，我们将 FCIL 重新诠释为一个统一的多任务学习问题，其中客户端和任务的更新都被表示为共享参数空间中的适应向量。基于这一视角，我们提出了时空适应向量统一几何手术（SUM），这是一个纯服务器端的框架，在聚合过程中对适应向量执行几何手术。

Spatial SUM mitigates client-level interference within each round, while causal online temporal SUM removes cross-task interference over time without additional client-side computation, communication, or memory beyond standard federated training.

空间 SUM（Spatial SUM）减轻了每一轮中客户端级别的干扰，而因果在线时间 SUM（causal online temporal SUM）则消除了跨任务的时间干扰，且无需在标准联邦训练之外增加额外的客户端计算、通信或内存开销。

Empirically, SUM achieves up to 22% improvement over prior FCIL methods across diverse vision and language benchmarks while remaining robust to unreliable clients and maintaining computational efficiency.

实验结果表明，SUM 在各种视觉和语言基准测试中比现有的 FCIL 方法提升了高达 22% 的性能，同时对不可靠的客户端保持稳健，并维持了计算效率。
