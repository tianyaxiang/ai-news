# When Data Science Makes Us Sad: The Story of an Overbooked Flight
# 当数据科学让我们感到难过：关于超售航班的故事

I recently came across a post on social media about a passenger bumped from an airplane because of overbooking. If you’re a frequent flyer, you may come across a similar situation. If you’re unlucky enough, it might even be you. This might sound like a mistake but I can assure you it isn’t. We live in a world where such operational mistakes, especially in the airline industry, do not happen unless it’s on purpose. So this is not a mistake but a risk taken by the airline to maximize profit. In more fancy terms, this is a statistically predictable trade-off and it is based on data science (or pure math). This was a decision based on probability and math to potentially gain some extra millions.

最近我在社交媒体上看到一篇文章，讲述了一名乘客因航班超售而被赶下飞机的故事。如果你经常乘飞机，可能也遇到过类似的情况。如果不走运的话，那个人甚至可能是你。这听起来像是一个错误，但我可以向你保证，事实并非如此。我们生活在一个这样的世界里：除非是故意的，否则航空公司等行业不会出现这种运营失误。因此，这并非失误，而是航空公司为了实现利润最大化而承担的风险。用更高级的术语来说，这是一种统计学上可预测的权衡，它基于数据科学（或纯数学）。这是一个基于概率和数学的决策，旨在潜在地赚取额外的数百万美元。

In this article, we will get to the bottom of this and learn a lot of valuable concepts around probability, distributions, and expected value (highly important subjects for data scientists, analysts, or anyone interested in analytics).

在本文中，我们将深入探讨这一问题，并学习有关概率、分布和期望值的许多宝贵概念（这些对于数据科学家、分析师或任何对分析感兴趣的人来说都是非常重要的课题）。

### DS Airlines Selling Extra Tickets
### DS 航空公司超售机票

I’d like to call this company DS Airlines. They sell 304 seats for a flight with a capacity of 300 seats. The probability of a passenger showing up for a flight is 95% based on historical flight data. We will try to answer the following questions: What is the probability that this flight is overbooked? It happens if at least 301 passengers show up for the flight. What is the expected value of the number of overbooked passengers? What is the airline risking when selling more tickets than available seats?

我想把这家公司称为 DS 航空公司。他们为一架载客量为 300 人的航班售出了 304 张机票。根据历史飞行数据，乘客登机的概率为 95%。我们将尝试回答以下问题：该航班超售的概率是多少？（即至少有 301 名乘客登机的情况）。超售乘客数量的期望值是多少？航空公司在售出超过可用座位的机票时承担了什么风险？

We start by making the assumption that passengers are independent from each other. One passenger’s choice of showing up for the flight has zero impact on other passengers’ choices. Then, we can say that the probability of any passenger showing up is 0.95 (i.e. 95%). This assumption does not always hold because there might be families or groups flying together. If one person in a family shows up, then other members of the family are much more likely to show up than 95%. However, when we get into such details and conditions, the math becomes a nightmare with complex conditional probabilities. Besides, we may not always know who are flying together (e.g. members of a group can purchase tickets separately). With the assumption of independent passengers, we can say that the number of passengers who show up for the flight follows binomial distribution.

我们首先假设乘客之间是相互独立的。一名乘客是否登机的选择对其他乘客的选择没有影响。那么，我们可以说任何一名乘客登机的概率都是 0.95（即 95%）。这一假设并不总是成立，因为可能存在家庭或团体一起飞行的情况。如果家庭中的一人登机，那么其他家庭成员登机的可能性远高于 95%。然而，当我们深入研究这些细节和条件时，数学计算会因为复杂的条件概率而变得像噩梦一样。此外，我们可能并不总是知道谁是一起飞行的（例如，团体成员可以分开购买机票）。在假设乘客独立的前提下，我们可以说登机的乘客数量遵循二项分布。

### Binomial Distribution
### 二项分布

Binomial distribution is a probability model used for counting successes in a series of repeated, identical, and independent events. The outcome of these events must be binary (i.e. 1 or 0, show up or do not show up for the flight). Hence, 304 passengers showing up for the flight can be modeled using a binomial distribution of a series of 304 independent events where the probability of an event is 95%. For a scenario to be qualified for a binomial distribution, it needs to satisfy the following conditions:
1. Binary outcome: Success (show up for the flight) or failure (does not show up).
2. Independent events: A person showing up for the flight has zero impact on other passengers.
3. Same probability of success: It’s 95% for all the passengers.
4. Fixed number of events: This is the number of tickets sold for this flight, which is 304.

二项分布是一种概率模型，用于计算一系列重复、相同且独立的事件中的成功次数。这些事件的结果必须是二元的（即 1 或 0，登机或不登机）。因此，304 名乘客登机的情况可以使用一系列 304 个独立事件的二项分布来建模，其中每个事件的概率为 95%。要使一个场景符合二项分布，它需要满足以下条件：
1. 二元结果：成功（登机）或失败（不登机）。
2. 独立事件：一个人是否登机对其他乘客没有影响。
3. 相同的成功概率：所有乘客均为 95%。
4. 固定的事件数量：即该航班售出的机票数量，为 304 张。

Binomial distribution helps us answer the following question: If I repeat an experiment multiple times, what is the probability of getting exactly k successful outcomes?

二项分布有助于我们回答以下问题：如果我重复进行多次实验，获得恰好 k 次成功结果的概率是多少？

### Probability of Overbooking
### 超售概率

Our flight will be overbooked if more than 300 passengers show up so we need to calculate the probability of k > 300 successful outcomes. With binomial distribution, the probability of k number of successful outcomes is: n is the total number of events, which is 304; k is the number of successes; p is the probability of success event, which is 0.95. (1-p) is the probability of failure event (not showing up), which is 1 – 0.95 = 0.05. This formula relies on the independence of events. In probability theory, if A and B are independent, the probability of both events happening is their individual probabilities multiplied together.

如果超过 300 名乘客登机，我们的航班就会超售，因此我们需要计算 k > 300 次成功结果的概率。在二项分布中，k 次成功结果的概率公式为：n 是事件总数，即 304；k 是成功次数；p 是成功事件的概率，即 0.95。(1-p) 是失败事件（不登机）的概率，即 1 – 0.95 = 0.05。该公式依赖于事件的独立性。在概率论中，如果 A 和 B 是独立的，那么两个事件同时发生的概率就是它们各自概率的乘积。

To calculate the probability of k=301 passengers showing up, we multiply 301 0.95 and 5 0.05 together. Then, we multiply it with the combinator at the beginning. It reads as “n choose k”, which counts for how many different ways we select 301 people out of 304. The following drawing makes it a little easier to grasp the logic behind the formula of the probability with binomial distribution. To simplify things, it illustrates the probability of 4 passengers showing to a flight with 5 tickets sold: The first row assumes passenger 5 does not show up, the second row assumes passenger 2 does not show up. Since these events are independent and identical, the probability of these events are the same. There are 5 combinations of 1 passenger not showing up (i.e. 5 chooses 4 is 5). Hence, the probability of 4 passengers showing up for the flight is 5 times the probability of 1 passenger not showing up.

为了计算 k=301 名乘客登机的概率，我们将 301 个 0.95 和 5 个 0.05 相乘。然后，将其与开头的组合数相乘。它读作“n 选 k”，计算的是从 304 人中选出 301 人的不同方式数量。下面的图示可以让你更容易理解二项分布概率公式背后的逻辑。为了简化，它展示了售出 5 张机票的航班有 4 名乘客登机的概率：第一行假设第 5 名乘客未登机，第二行假设第 2 名乘客未登机。由于这些事件是独立且相同的，因此这些事件的概率是相同的。有 5 种组合方式导致 1 名乘客未登机（即 5 选 4 等于 5）。因此，4 名乘客登机的概率是 1 名乘客未登机概率的 5 倍。

So the answer to our first question (What is the probability that this flight is overbooked?) can be calculated as P(X > 300) which is the sum of P(X=301) + P(X=302) + P(X=303) + P(X=304). The numbers at the beginning come from combinations: We can select 301 passengers from a group of 304 passengers in 4,636,304 different ways. So the final result is: The exact probability that the flight will be overbooked is 0.000139, which translates to roughly 0.014% (or about a 1-in-7,200 chance).

因此，我们第一个问题的答案（该航班超售的概率是多少？）可以通过计算 P(X > 300) 得出，即 P(X=301) + P(X=302) + P(X=303) + P(X=304) 的总和。开头的数字来自组合数：我们可以用 4,636,304 种不同的方式从 304 名乘客中选出 301 名。最终结果是：航班超售的精确概率为 0.000139，换算下来大约是 0.014%（或者大约 7,200 分之一的几率）。

We’ve found the probability that this flight will be overbooked. As we can see in the calculations above, the flight is overbooked if 301, 302, 303, or 304 passengers show up (i.e. 1, 2, 3, or 4 overbooked passengers).
