# Google just had its first negative cash flow quarter due to massive AI spending
# 谷歌因巨额人工智能支出，首次出现季度现金流为负

Google has reported its financial results for the second quarter of 2026, and as usual, the search giant raked in an unfathomable amount of money. Google saw total revenue of $119.8 billion, beating analyst expectations by a comfortable margin. Despite that, the company’s stock has taken a hit. Along with all that revenue, Google has announced a further increase in its AI-fueled capital expenditures (or capex). The company is actually spending so much on AI infrastructure that it has negative cash flow for the first time.

谷歌公布了 2026 年第二季度的财务业绩，这家搜索巨头一如既往地赚取了惊人的收入。谷歌总营收达到 1198 亿美元，轻松超过了分析师的预期。尽管如此，该公司的股价还是受到了冲击。在公布营收的同时，谷歌还宣布将进一步增加其由人工智能驱动的资本支出（capex）。事实上，该公司在人工智能基础设施上的投入巨大，导致其首次出现了现金流为负的情况。

Search was the largest chunk of Google’s income, accounting for $63.3 billion. Google Cloud pulled in $24.8 billion, a significant 23.8 percent increase from the first quarter. This shows there is massive demand for Google’s AI services. Google also earned $12.9 billion from its subscriptions, platforms, and devices portfolio, as well as $11.1 billion from YouTube ads. The company managed to goose that last one by more than 12 percent since last quarter as it made YouTube ads even longer.

搜索业务是谷歌最大的收入来源，贡献了 633 亿美元。谷歌云业务收入为 248 亿美元，较第一季度大幅增长了 23.8%。这表明市场对谷歌人工智能服务的需求巨大。此外，谷歌从订阅、平台和设备组合中获得了 129 亿美元的收入，从 YouTube 广告中获得了 111 亿美元的收入。由于延长了 YouTube 广告的时长，该公司成功使后者的收入较上季度增长了超过 12%。

A significant chunk of Google’s revenue comes from investments. When you subtract those non-cash earnings, Google’s operating cash flow for Q2 2026 was about $39.1 billion. That’s not the most the company has ever seen, but it’s a healthy 40 percent increase from Q2 2025. The problem is that Google’s spending has also gone up—a lot. Before this latest round of financial updates, Google told investors it was expecting $180 billion to $190 billion in capital expenditures for 2026. Like other AI-obsessed tech behemoths, Google is burning cash on building and running the data centers powering its AI models.

谷歌很大一部分收入来自投资。扣除这些非现金收益后，谷歌 2026 年第二季度的运营现金流约为 391 亿美元。虽然这并非该公司历史最高水平，但与 2025 年第二季度相比，仍有 40% 的健康增长。问题在于，谷歌的支出也大幅增加。在此次财务更新之前，谷歌曾告诉投资者，预计 2026 年的资本支出将在 1800 亿至 1900 亿美元之间。像其他痴迷于人工智能的科技巨头一样，谷歌正在烧钱建设和运营为其人工智能模型提供算力的数据中心。

These numbers were already well above the $91 billion Google spent in 2025. The company now says it’s planning to spend as much as $205 billion on infrastructure in 2026. As a result of its increasing AI demands, Google reports it spent $44.9 billion expanding its AI footprint in the second quarter, and you don’t need an accounting degree to know which number is larger. With $39.1 billion in cash income, this spending left Google with -$5.8 billion free cash flow.

这些数字已经远高于谷歌 2025 年 910 亿美元的支出。该公司现在表示，计划在 2026 年投入高达 2050 亿美元用于基础设施建设。由于人工智能需求不断增加，谷歌报告称其在第二季度花费了 449 亿美元来扩大其人工智能版图。无需会计学位也能看出哪个数字更大：在 391 亿美元现金收入的情况下，这笔支出导致谷歌的自由现金流为 -58 亿美元。

To be clear, Google is still profitable—wildly so. It’s also sitting on a war chest of more than $100 billion. But free cash flow is an important metric that goes to the overall health of a business. This is the actual money a company takes in to fund its operations without selling investments or taking out new loans. So it’s notable that Google’s free cash flow has dipped into negative territory for the first time since going public. Google’s stock price took a hit overnight on the news, dropping about 4.5 percent. It has continued to trend downward today.

需要明确的是，谷歌仍然盈利，而且盈利能力极强。它还拥有超过 1000 亿美元的现金储备。但自由现金流是衡量企业整体健康状况的重要指标。这是公司在不通过出售投资或申请新贷款的情况下，为运营提供资金的实际收入。因此，谷歌自上市以来自由现金流首次跌入负值区域，这一点值得注意。受此消息影响，谷歌股价隔夜下跌约 4.5%，今日继续呈下行趋势。

Onward and upward Google’s leadership is signaling to investors that this state of affairs is the new normal. The company points out with much fanfare that its capex spending currently is about six times higher than the $22 billion it spent in 2022 before the AI boom. Google expects spending to be even higher next year, too. Investors have started to question the scale of AI spending, which is expected to top $700 billion industry-wide this year. Tech firms used to be reliable stocks, reporting high profits and juicy margins, but the race to build AI data centers and train new models has changed the landscape.

谷歌领导层向投资者发出信号，称这种状况是“新常态”。该公司大张旗鼓地指出，其目前的资本支出约为 2022 年人工智能热潮前 220 亿美元支出的六倍。谷歌预计明年的支出还会更高。投资者已经开始质疑人工智能支出的规模，预计今年全行业的人工智能支出将超过 7000 亿美元。科技公司过去曾是可靠的股票，拥有高额利润和丰厚的利润率，但建设人工智能数据中心和训练新模型的竞赛已经改变了这一格局。

Google is in a better position than most, with a robust ad business and cloud services that pull in plenty of cash. It just wasn’t quite enough cash to offset its spending this time. It also designs its own AI chips, and the latest Tensor 8i and 8t are supposed to be more efficient for AI data centers. Where things go from here is uncertain. People are clearly more sensitive to the high cost and lack of profit in AI, so Google’s stock price may be in for a minor correction. Things will probably even out for the company if it can continue to compete with other major AI players.

谷歌的处境比大多数公司都要好，其强大的广告业务和云服务带来了充足的现金流。只是这一次的现金流还不足以抵消其支出。该公司还设计了自己的 AI 芯片，最新的 Tensor 8i 和 8t 被认为对 AI 数据中心更有效率。未来局势尚不明朗。人们显然对人工智能的高成本和缺乏利润更加敏感，因此谷歌的股价可能会经历小幅回调。如果谷歌能继续与其他主要人工智能参与者竞争，其状况可能会趋于平稳。

Google recently delayed the release of its flagship Gemini 3.5 Pro model, which it says is still in testing with a small number of partners. Reports have suggested that Google is not seeing the kind of gains it needs to remain competitive with the likes of GPT 5.6 and Claude Mythos. Google has also been hit with a wave of resignations among its top AI researchers. The next few months may be pivotal for Google’s AI efforts.

谷歌最近推迟了其旗舰模型 Gemini 3.5 Pro 的发布，称该模型目前仍处于与少数合作伙伴的测试阶段。有报道称，谷歌尚未获得在 GPT 5.6 和 Claude Mythos 等竞争对手面前保持竞争力所需的收益。谷歌还遭遇了一波顶级人工智能研究人员的辞职潮。未来几个月对谷歌的人工智能业务来说可能至关重要。
