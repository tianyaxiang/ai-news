# Lessons Learned After 8.5 Years of ML
# 机器学习 8.5 年后的经验教训

Machine Learning Lessons Learned After 8.5 Years of ML: Patience, Optimism, Discipline, Projects, Teams. Pascal Janetzky, Jul 23, 2026.
机器学习 8.5 年后的经验教训：耐心、乐观、自律、项目与团队。作者：Pascal Janetzky，2026 年 7 月 23 日。

It is now already more than eight years that I have spent with machine learning. The more I get to know this field the more I am astounded by how diverse it is. When I started my studies of machine learning, the only thing I back then knew about it was the classic machine learning techniques, such as KNN or clustering algorithms. Now, with more than eight years of further experience, I think that the field is so broad that no single person can understand all of this.
我投身机器学习领域已经超过八年了。我对这个领域了解得越多，就越为它的多样性所震撼。当我刚开始学习机器学习时，我所知道的仅仅是 KNN 或聚类算法等经典技术。如今，经过八年多的深入实践，我认为这个领域已经极其广阔，没有任何一个人能够完全掌握它。

However, I think that there are still some lessons that are applicable regardless of the field one actually is doing machine learning research or machine learning practices in. Frequently, I take the opportunity of another half year of progress in my machine learning journey to step back for a moment and look at the previous years. What have I learned? Which lessons seem to persist? In this edition of my lessons learned articles I look back at the previous eight years and try to distill lessons that are persisting in coming up over and over again. This time I found that making progress in machine learning, or any field, really, often comes down to the following five things. They are patience, discipline, optimism, good projects, and good teams. I will give more details about these in the remainder of this article.
然而，我认为无论是在机器学习研究还是工程实践中，总有一些经验教训是通用的。我经常利用机器学习之旅中每半年一次的进展节点，停下来回顾过去。我学到了什么？哪些经验是持久的？在这一期的经验总结文章中，我回顾了过去八年，试图提炼出那些反复出现的、经久不衰的教训。这一次我发现，在机器学习或任何领域取得进步，往往归结为以下五点：耐心、自律、乐观、好的项目和好的团队。我将在本文的后续部分详细阐述这些内容。

### Patience / 耐心

Nobody is born an expert, in no field. If one is not born with genius-grade capacity — and even then –, being patient will work wonders. I frequently like to compare the progress in machine learning with the progress one makes in sportive activities. Often times as a beginner you will fail very much, very often in the beginning; in the early days and months of the new activity. Take learning the handstand, for example. Until you have mastered the balance to hold your weight overhead, and built the strength in the first place, it takes quite a while. And learning to do so you will also encounter a lot of failures. Probably you will see no progress at all in the early days.
没有人天生就是专家。如果你没有天才般的资质——即便有——耐心也会创造奇迹。我经常喜欢把机器学习的进步与体育运动的进步相提并论。作为初学者，你往往会在起步阶段频繁失败；在接触新事物的最初几天或几个月里尤其如此。以学习倒立为例，在你掌握平衡以支撑体重并建立起基础力量之前，需要相当长的时间。在这个过程中，你会遇到很多失败，可能在初期根本看不到任何进展。

With machine learning, it is the same. It will take a couple of iterations on a project until it is good enough. Along the way, your work will be rejected and criticed, until it is deemed good enough. This happened to me as well, of course. A paper of mine has been rejected four times over two years until I finally got it accepted at an A* conference. During these times, I had to resubmit the paper over and over again, and redo the story, the experiment, almost everything. I would be lying would I say that I was always positive. No, from time to time I would have been more than happy to drop the paper and focus on other things. But — patience. I focused on doing what I could do at that moments, and that was redoing and resubmitting. And then waiting. Now it is accepted, but had I not been patient enough, then it would certainly NOT be published now.
机器学习也是如此。一个项目需要经过多次迭代才能达到足够好的水平。在此过程中，你的工作会被拒绝、被批评，直到它被认为足够优秀。当然，我也经历过这种情况。我的一篇论文在两年内被拒绝了四次，直到最后才被 A* 类会议录用。在那段时间里，我不得不反复修改论文，重写故事线、重做实验，几乎重做了一切。如果说我一直保持积极心态，那是在撒谎。不，我时常想放弃这篇论文去关注其他事情。但是——耐心。我专注于当时能做的事情，那就是重做和重新提交，然后等待。现在它被录用了，但如果我当时不够耐心，它现在绝对不会发表。

### Optimism / 乐观

This brings me to the next lesson. You need to cultivate healthy optimism (or, healthy ignorance). When working on a project, such as a research paper or a deployment, you will inevitably face obstacles. Persisting is necessary, but you need more than to just persist. I think that you need to be silently optimistic about your work. Others can say their things, and you can listen, but in the end it is your work. As long as you trust the progress, you are generally fine. Or, to be more precise: as long as you trust the process for the majority of times, then you are fine.
这引出了下一个教训。你需要培养健康的乐观精神（或者说，健康的“无知”）。在进行项目（如研究论文或系统部署）时，你不可避免地会遇到障碍。坚持是必要的，但仅仅坚持是不够的。我认为你需要对自己的工作保持一种默默的乐观。别人可以发表他们的看法，你可以听取，但归根结底这是你的工作。只要你相信进展，通常就没问题。或者更准确地说：只要你在大多数时候相信这个过程，你就会没问题。

### Discipline / 自律

Again, this neatly leads to the next lesson underlying my past years: discipline. In our daily lives, there surely are things that are more engaging at the moment. Well, sitting down to read another paper is interesting, but checking Twitter or YouTube is more engaging. But, that will not bring you forward, or even actively pull you back. To make progress, you need to ignore deflections and daily distractions. You will need the quality that has been praised in all disciplines across most of humanity: discipline. When learning for an exam, you do so, regardless of the circumstances. When writing a lab report, you do so, regarless of your peers going partying. When drafting a thesis, you do so regardless of others going on vacation. Having nearly written a report does not count. Neither does a nearly written thesis. Only if you can focus and be disciplined about it, you can move forward. My “secret” to this is to put the important things first, day after day. I try to have an 80% adherence to this schedule to make it realistically workable. I found it to work quite well.
这自然引出了过去几年我学到的下一个教训：自律。在日常生活中，肯定有当下更吸引人的事情。坐下来读一篇论文很有趣，但刷 Twitter 或 YouTube 显然更“吸引人”。然而，那不会让你进步，甚至会拖你的后腿。为了取得进步，你需要忽略干扰和日常琐事。你需要具备人类各领域都推崇的品质：自律。备考时，无论环境如何，你都要坚持学习；写实验报告时，无论同伴是否去聚会，你都要坚持完成；写论文时，无论别人是否去度假，你都要坚持撰写。报告写了一半不算数，论文写了一半也不算数。只有专注并保持自律，你才能前进。我对此的“秘诀”是日复一日地把重要的事情放在首位。我尝试保持 80% 的执行率，以确保计划切实可行。我发现这非常有效。

### Projects / 项目

This is a lesson specific to people working towards a (doctoral) thesis: you need a sufficiently good project to work on. It might not matter too much if you are intelligent enough. What might matter more is that your topic is sufficiently niche/new AND stable enough so that others care about it, and that you can utilize your strengths on it. Some years ago, I had a colleague who studied emergent capabilities of LLMs. This is quite an interesting topic, and I came across it at ICLR 2024 in Vienna, where I saw some posters on it. My colleague was quite skilled in his transformer model knowledge and programming skills. However, the field was TOO new, too unstable. The models were progressing too fast, and he could not get a grip on the topic. In the end, it burned him out and he switched to other things. (He’s doing fine now!)
这是专门针对攻读（博士）学位的人的教训：你需要一个足够好的项目。如果你足够聪明，这可能不是最重要的。更重要的是，你的课题必须足够细分/新颖，同时又足够稳定，这样别人才会关注，并且你能发挥自己的优势。几年前，我有一位同事研究大语言模型的涌现能力。这是一个非常有趣的话题，我在维也纳举行的 ICLR 2024 会议上看到过相关海报。我那位同事在 Transformer 模型知识和编程技能方面非常出色。然而，这个领域太新、太不稳定了。模型发展太快，他无法把握住这个课题。最终，他精疲力竭并转向了其他领域。（他现在过得很好！）

### Teams / 团队

I reserved the most important lesson for the end. It often matters more who you work with than what you work at. (If you tick both, meaning you have a good group AND your work is cool, then you are especially lucky). If you are good at what you do, but your environment does not let you put this to use, then you are in the wrong place. If you cannot make mistakes, then you are in the wrong place. If you fear showing up, then...
我把最重要的教训留到了最后。与你共事的人往往比你从事的工作更重要。（如果你两者兼得，即拥有一个优秀的团队且工作内容很酷，那你就是特别幸运的）。如果你擅长自己的工作，但环境让你无法发挥，那么你就在错误的地方。如果你不能犯错，那么你就在错误的地方。如果你害怕露面，那么……
