# Secure-by-Disguise: A Systematic Evaluation of Image Disguising for Confidential Medical Image Modeling

**Title:** Secure-by-Disguise: A Systematic Evaluation of Image Disguising for Confidential Medical Image Modeling
**标题：** Secure-by-Disguise：针对机密医学图像建模中图像伪装技术的系统性评估

---

**Abstract:** Cloud-based deep learning enables large-scale medical image analysis but raises significant privacy concerns when sensitive patient images are outsourced for model development. Image disguising has recently emerged as a promising privacy-enhancing technology (PET) that transforms images into visually unintelligible representations while preserving information for downstream learning.

**摘要：** 基于云的深度学习技术实现了大规模医学图像分析，但当敏感的患者图像被外包用于模型开发时，引发了重大的隐私担忧。图像伪装技术作为一种新兴且具有前景的隐私增强技术（PET），能够将图像转换为视觉上无法识别的表示形式，同时保留用于下游学习的关键信息。

---

We established a unified framework to evaluate representative methods, DisguisedNets and NeuraCrypt, across four datasets involving classification and semantic segmentation tasks. Our analysis assessed predictive utility, efficiency, and robustness against reconstruction attacks.

我们建立了一个统一的框架，在涉及分类和语义分割任务的四个数据集上，对代表性方法 DisguisedNets 和 NeuraCrypt 进行了评估。我们的分析评估了这些方法在预测效用、效率以及抵御重构攻击的鲁棒性方面的表现。

---

Results showed that image disguising performance varies significantly between tasks; while methods preserved utility for medical image classification, they caused substantial degradation in dense semantic segmentation. Specifically, Randomized Multidimensional Transformation (RMT) offered the optimal balance of performance and security, whereas AES-based disguising severely impacted utility.

结果表明，图像伪装技术在不同任务间的表现差异显著；虽然这些方法在医学图像分类任务中保持了良好的效用，但在密集语义分割任务中却导致了明显的性能下降。具体而言，随机多维变换（RMT）在性能和安全性之间提供了最佳平衡，而基于 AES 的伪装技术则严重影响了模型效用。

---

Furthermore, regression-based reconstruction attacks effective on natural images proved considerably less successful on realistic medical images. These findings provide a systematic assessment of PET suitability for confidential medical AI applications.

此外，在自然图像上有效的基于回归的重构攻击，在真实医学图像上的成功率显著降低。这些发现为评估隐私增强技术（PET）在机密医学人工智能应用中的适用性提供了系统性的参考。
