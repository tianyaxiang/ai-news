# Sticky Routing: Training MoE Models for Memory-Efficient Inference
# Sticky Routing：训练用于内存高效推理的 MoE 模型

Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) models activate only a sparse subset of experts per token, yet consecutive tokens frequently activate different experts -- causing constant weight swapping between slow storage and fast memory on edge devices.
混合专家（MoE）模型在处理每个 token 时仅激活一小部分专家，但连续的 token 往往会激活不同的专家，这导致边缘设备在慢速存储和快速内存之间频繁进行权重交换。

Existing remedies are either system-level (caching heuristics) or post-hoc (router fine-tuning), leaving the root cause unchanged during pretraining.
现有的补救措施要么属于系统层面（如缓存启发式算法），要么属于事后处理（如路由微调），这些方法在预训练阶段并未改变问题的根本原因。

We propose StickyMoE, a differentiable routing consistency loss that penalises abrupt expert switches between adjacent tokens, encouraging the router to maintain the same expert assignment across semantically coherent spans.
我们提出了 StickyMoE，这是一种可微分的路由一致性损失函数。它通过惩罚相邻 token 之间突兀的专家切换，鼓励路由器在语义连贯的片段中保持相同的专家分配。

StickyMoE requires no architectural changes, adds a single hyperparameter lambda, and unlike post-hoc methods, allows expert representations and routing decisions to co-adapt from the first training step.
StickyMoE 不需要对架构进行任何更改，仅增加了一个超参数 lambda。与事后处理方法不同，它允许专家表示和路由决策从训练的第一步就开始协同适应。

Experiments on small-scale MoE language models show that StickyMoE reduces the expert switch rate by up to 60% with less than 4% perplexity degradation, Pareto-dominating post-hoc fine-tuning on the quality-locality frontier. Routing temporal locality is most efficiently instilled at training time.
在小规模 MoE 语言模型上的实验表明，StickyMoE 在困惑度（perplexity）下降不到 4% 的情况下，将专家切换率降低了高达 60%，在质量与局部性（quality-locality）的权衡前沿上，其表现优于事后微调方法。事实证明，在训练阶段注入路由的时间局部性是最有效的。
