# iLENS: Interpretable LLM-Guided Mixture-of-Experts for Neuroimaging Survival Analysis
# iLENS：用于神经影像生存分析的可解释大模型引导专家混合模型

**Abstract:** Alzheimer's Disease (AD) is a complex neurodegenerative disorder that continues to impact millions of people worldwide. Predicting AD conversion during the prodromal stage remains critical for disease understanding and patient care. 

**摘要：** 阿尔茨海默病（AD）是一种复杂的神经退行性疾病，持续影响着全球数百万人。在疾病的前驱期预测 AD 的转化对于疾病理解和患者护理至关重要。

As such, survival models are widely used for AD risk prediction, yet they are typically static predictors with limited interpretability and no capacity for natural language reasoning. 

因此，生存模型被广泛用于 AD 风险预测，但它们通常是静态预测器，可解释性有限，且不具备自然语言推理能力。

In this work, we propose iLENS, an interpretable large language model (LLM) guided framework based on mixture-of-experts (MoE) for survival prediction in AD conversion. 

在这项工作中，我们提出了 iLENS，这是一个基于专家混合模型（MoE）的可解释大语言模型（LLM）引导框架，用于 AD 转化中的生存预测。

Our approach uses LLM to synthesize structured neuroimaging measurements and unstructured information to guide expert routing. 

我们的方法利用大语言模型来综合结构化的神经影像测量数据和非结构化信息，从而引导专家路由。

Our framework demonstrates competitive predictive performance and capability in patient subtyping. 

我们的框架展示了具有竞争力的预测性能以及在患者亚型分析方面的能力。

Furthermore, our framework provides transparent, biologically grounded rationales for its routing decisions, bridging the gap between high-performance survival analysis and interpretable clinical decision support.

此外，我们的框架为其路由决策提供了透明且具有生物学依据的解释，弥合了高性能生存分析与可解释临床决策支持之间的鸿沟。
