# Mixture of Probes: Learning from Privileged Modalities in Multimodal LLMs Through Probing

**Abstract:**

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are typically designed under the assumption that all modalities available during training will also be accessible at inference. However, many real-world settings violate this assumption, requiring models to operate under a privileged modality setting, where auxiliary modalities are available only during training.
多模态大语言模型（MLLMs）的设计通常基于一个假设：训练时可用的所有模态在推理时也同样可用。然而，许多现实场景违背了这一假设，要求模型在“特权模态”（privileged modality）设置下运行，即辅助模态仅在训练阶段可用。

While these modalities contain valuable information, existing MLLMs largely fail to leverage them effectively, as they treat modalities as interchangeable inputs rather than sources of complementary supervision. We propose Mixture of Probes (MoP), a novel framework that disentangles modality-specific and modality-general signals within the MLLM, allowing the model to preserve modality-dependent structure while learning transferable representations across modalities.
尽管这些模态包含有价值的信息，但现有的 MLLMs 在有效利用它们方面大多表现不佳，因为它们将模态视为可互换的输入，而非互补的监督来源。我们提出了“探针混合”（Mixture of Probes, MoP），这是一个新颖的框架，旨在解耦 MLLM 内部的模态特定信号与模态通用信号，使模型在保留模态依赖结构的同时，学习跨模态的可迁移表示。

At its core, MoP achieves this through a structured probing mechanism that extracts and organizes information from intermediate representations of a shared modality encoder, rather than relying only on final-layer alignment as done in existing MLLMs. To support this disentanglement, we further introduce MoP Cross-modal Training (MoP-X), a training strategy for MoP centered around a probe disentanglement loss that prevents probe collapse and encourages cross-modal learning.
MoP 的核心在于通过一种结构化的探针机制，从共享模态编码器的中间表示中提取并组织信息，而不是像现有 MLLMs 那样仅依赖最终层的对齐。为了支持这种解耦，我们进一步引入了 MoP 跨模态训练（MoP-X），这是一种围绕探针解耦损失（probe disentanglement loss）的训练策略，旨在防止探针崩溃并促进跨模态学习。

We evaluate MoP across two domains spanning eight tasks and four modalities under a comprehensive evaluation protocol tailored to the privileged modality setting, where each modality is independently treated as the sole input at inference time. MoP consistently outperforms strong MLLM baselines, achieving up to 65% relative improvement, demonstrating that auxiliary modalities, even when unavailable at inference, can provide substantial gains when effectively leveraged during training.
我们在两个领域、八项任务和四种模态下对 MoP 进行了评估，采用了专为特权模态设置量身定制的综合评估协议，即在推理时将每种模态独立视为唯一输入。MoP 在各项指标上均持续优于强大的 MLLM 基线模型，实现了高达 65% 的相对性能提升。这证明了辅助模态即使在推理时不可用，只要在训练阶段得到有效利用，也能提供显著的增益。
