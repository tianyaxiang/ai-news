# GRAFT: Grafted Reference Audio for Fine-grained Pronunciation in Zero-shot Text-to-Speech
# GRAFT：用于零样本语音合成中细粒度发音的嫁接参考音频技术

**Abstract:** We present GRAFT, a per-word pronunciation conditioning mechanism for text-to-speech neural codec language modeling. Existing systems reach high intelligibility and naturalness but inherit the ambiguity of text and mispronounce rare proper nouns, loanwords and technical terms. Even phoneme-conditioned models offer no direct acoustic handle for per-word pronunciation.

**摘要：** 我们提出了 GRAFT，这是一种用于神经编解码语音合成语言模型的逐词发音调节机制。现有的系统虽然达到了很高的可懂度和自然度，但仍受限于文本的歧义性，且在处理罕见专有名词、外来词和技术术语时容易出现发音错误。即使是基于音素调节的模型，也无法为逐词发音提供直接的声学控制手段。

GRAFT controls the pronunciation of a chosen word from a short spoken sample of it, encoded with the model's own speech tokenizer and bound to the word's position in the prompt. Voice conversion during training-data construction disentangles the hint speaker from the target speaker, so the hint may come from any voice while the output stays in the target voice.

GRAFT 通过一段简短的语音样本来控制特定词汇的发音，该样本使用模型自身的语音分词器进行编码，并与提示词中的位置绑定。在训练数据构建过程中，通过语音转换技术将提示音说话人与目标说话人解耦，因此提示音可以来自任何声音，而输出则保持目标说话人的音色。

In a blind English listening study, human raters rank GRAFT first by a clear margin, judging its rendering of the difficult word closest to a reference recording of that word. On a five-language objective benchmark, GRAFT reduces target-word phoneme error rate by 22-39% over the identical text-only backbone and outperforms competitive open-source zero-shot systems, both phoneme- and text-conditioned, on target-word pronunciation, while preserving speaker similarity and naturalness.

在一项盲听英语测试中，人类评估员以明显的优势将 GRAFT 排在首位，认为其对难词的渲染最接近该词的参考录音。在五种语言的客观基准测试中，与相同的纯文本基准模型相比，GRAFT 将目标词的音素错误率降低了 22-39%。同时，在保持说话人相似度和自然度的前提下，其在目标词发音方面的表现优于目前主流的开源零样本系统（包括基于音素和基于文本调节的系统）。
