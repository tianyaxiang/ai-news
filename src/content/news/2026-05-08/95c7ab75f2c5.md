# Anatomy of a failure: When, how, and why deep vision fails in scientific domains
# 失败的剖析：深度视觉在科学领域何时、如何以及为何会失效

**Abstract:** Mirroring its ubiquity in popular media and all human activities, the use of deep learning (DL) is rapidly growing in scientific imaging modalities. However, unlike everyday RGB pictures, pixels encode precise physicochemical properties in scientific imaging across potentially thousands of channels. While DL is well validated on human-centric RGB perceptual tasks, its effectiveness for scientific imaging remains uncertain.

**摘要：** 深度学习（DL）在科学成像领域的应用正迅速增长，这反映了它在流行媒体和人类各项活动中的普遍性。然而，与日常的 RGB 图片不同，科学成像中的像素在数千个通道中编码了精确的物理化学属性。虽然深度学习在以人为中心的 RGB 感知任务中已得到充分验证，但其在科学成像中的有效性仍不确定。

Here, we show that the naive application of DL frameworks to scientific images can lead to critical failures. We evaluate the use of DL for pathology, comparing RGB images of stained tissue with the quantitative and information-rich biochemical signatures of infrared (IR) imaging. Despite this informational advantage, DL models trained on IR data paradoxically underperform.

在此，我们展示了将深度学习框架简单地应用于科学图像可能会导致严重的失败。我们评估了深度学习在病理学中的应用，对比了染色组织的 RGB 图像与红外（IR）成像中定量且信息丰富的生化特征。尽管具有这种信息优势，但在红外数据上训练的深度学习模型却反常地表现不佳。

We investigate this discrepancy to find that IR data priors interact poorly with the simplicity bias of DL, causing models to collapse to one-dimensional predictions. This constitutes a catastrophic DL failure because the model's representational capacity remains largely unused, while furthermore raising AI safety concerns and undermining the advantages of such scientific modalities.

我们调查了这种差异，发现红外数据先验与深度学习的“简单性偏差”（simplicity bias）相互作用不良，导致模型坍缩为一维预测。这构成了深度学习的灾难性失败，因为模型的表征能力在很大程度上未被利用，同时还引发了人工智能安全问题，并削弱了此类科学成像方式的优势。

Notably, this problem persists even with state-of-the-art DL robustification strategies, which are primarily designed and validated for RGB imagery and thus inherit the same prior-bias mismatch. This work establishes a framework for understanding the limitations of generic DL in science and advocates for the study of modality-specific failure modes to guide the development of specialized, safe AI algorithms.

值得注意的是，即使使用最先进的深度学习鲁棒化策略，这个问题依然存在，因为这些策略主要是为 RGB 图像设计和验证的，因此继承了相同的先验与偏差不匹配问题。这项工作建立了一个理解通用深度学习在科学领域局限性的框架，并提倡研究特定模态的失效模式，以指导专业、安全的 AI 算法的开发。
