# Transformation Categorization Based on Group Decomposition Theory Using Parameter Division
# 基于群分解理论与参数划分的变换分类

**Abstract:** Representation learning seeks meaningful sensory representations without supervision and can model aspects of human development. Although many neural networks empirically learn useful features, a principled account of what makes a representation "good" remains elusive.
**摘要：** 表征学习旨在无需监督的情况下寻求有意义的感知表征，并能模拟人类发展的某些方面。尽管许多神经网络在经验上能够学习到有用的特征，但对于什么样的表征才算“好”表征，目前仍缺乏原则性的解释。

We study unsupervised categorization of transformations between pairs of inputs under algebraic constraints. Classical disentanglement favors mutually independent factors and fails when factors are coupled. Our prior Galois-theoretic approach decomposes a group via normal subgroups by learning a product of two transformations with one factor constrained to a normal subgroup, covering both commutative and non-commutative cases.
我们研究了在代数约束下，输入对之间变换的无监督分类问题。经典的解耦方法倾向于相互独立的因子，但在因子耦合时往往失效。我们之前的伽罗瓦理论方法通过学习两个变换的乘积来分解群（通过正规子群），其中一个因子被约束在正规子群内，涵盖了交换和非交换两种情况。

That method, however, relied on auxiliary assumptions (e.g., motion and isometry restrictions) not required by decomposition theory, and ablations did not separate theory-based from auxiliary effects. We propose parameter division for a single transformation: we split its parameter into components, impose homomorphism constraints mapping the full transformation to one component, and identify the normal subgroup as the set of transformations when that component is fixed to the identity.
然而，该方法依赖于分解理论本身并不需要的辅助假设（例如运动和等距限制），且消融实验未能将基于理论的效果与辅助效果区分开来。我们提出了一种针对单一变换的参数划分方法：我们将变换参数拆分为多个分量，施加同态约束将完整变换映射到一个分量上，并将该分量固定为恒等元时的一组变换识别为正规子群。

This formulation drops the previous auxiliary assumptions and applies more broadly. We evaluate on image pairs involving rotation, translation, and scale; ablations show that group-decomposition constraints drive appropriate categorization.
这一公式化方法摒弃了之前的辅助假设，具有更广泛的适用性。我们在涉及旋转、平移和缩放的图像对上进行了评估；消融实验表明，群分解约束能够驱动准确的分类。
