# I Rewrote a Real Data Workflow in Polars. Pandas Didn’t Stand a Chance.
# 我用 Polars 重写了一个真实的数据工作流，Pandas 毫无胜算。

**From 61 seconds to 0.20 seconds — and the mental model shift I didn't expect**
**从 61 秒缩短至 0.20 秒——以及我意想不到的思维模式转变**

I’ll be honest — I wasn’t actively looking for Polars. I’ve been on a bit of a Pandas optimization journey lately. First, I wrote about why you should stop writing loops in Pandas and think in columns instead. Then I went deeper to profiling real workflows, fixing vectorization mistakes, and ended up cutting a 61-second pipeline down to 0.33 seconds using nothing but better Pandas and NumPy. That one surprised even me. So I was in a good place with Pandas. I felt like I finally understood how to use it properly.
老实说，我并没有刻意去寻找 Polars。最近我一直在进行 Pandas 的优化之旅。起初，我写了一篇文章，探讨为什么你应该停止在 Pandas 中编写循环，转而以列（column）的思维去思考。随后，我深入分析了真实的工作流，修复了向量化错误，最终仅通过优化 Pandas 和 NumPy 的用法，就将一个 61 秒的流水线缩短到了 0.33 秒。连我自己都感到惊讶。因此，我对 Pandas 的掌握感觉良好，觉得自己终于真正理解了如何正确使用它。

Then someone dropped a comment on one of my posts. Something along the lines of: “Have you tried Polars? It’s built for exactly this kind of thing.” I’d seen the name floating around in the data community. There was buzz around it — something about speed, about a completely different way of thinking about data pipelines. But I’d never actually touched it. That comment was enough to push me over the edge. So I did what I always do. I got curious, I installed it, and I rewrote the exact same workflow from my last article, the one I’d already optimized in Pandas, in a tool I’d never used before. What I found surprised me. Not just the speed numbers, but what Polars quietly teaches you about how data pipelines actually work.
后来，有人在我的文章下留言，大意是：“你试过 Polars 吗？它就是为这种场景而生的。” 我在数据社区里经常听到这个名字，大家都在讨论它——关于它的速度，以及它处理数据流水线时那种截然不同的思维方式。但我从未真正接触过它。那条评论成了促使我尝试的契机。于是，我做了我一贯的做法：我产生了好奇心，安装了它，并用这个我从未用过的工具，重写了上一篇文章中那个我已经优化过的 Pandas 工作流。结果让我大吃一惊。不仅是速度上的提升，更在于 Polars 悄然教会了我数据流水线究竟是如何运作的。

### Isn’t Pandas Enough?
### Pandas 还不够吗？

Fair question. In my last article, I took a slow Pandas pipeline and optimized it down to 0.33 seconds. Vectorized operations, correct data types, no unnecessary copies. The results were honestly better than I expected. So why are we even talking about Polars? Here’s the thing. Everything I did in that article was me doing the optimization manually. I had to know which operations were slow, why they were slow, and how to fix them. Polars does a lot of that thinking for you automatically, before it even runs your code.
这是一个好问题。在上一篇文章中，我将一个缓慢的 Pandas 流水线优化到了 0.33 秒。通过向量化操作、正确的数据类型以及避免不必要的拷贝，结果确实超出了我的预期。那么，为什么我们还要谈论 Polars 呢？关键在于，我在那篇文章中所做的一切都是手动优化。我必须清楚哪些操作慢、为什么慢，以及如何修复它们。而 Polars 在运行代码之前，就会自动为你完成大部分此类思考。

On top of that, Polars is built on a completely different foundation than Pandas. It uses all your CPU cores by default. It manages memory differently. And it introduces a way of writing data pipelines that, once it clicks, changes how you think about the whole process. Optimized Pandas is impressive. But it still has a ceiling. This article is about what’s on the other side of it.
此外，Polars 的构建基础与 Pandas 完全不同。它默认使用你所有的 CPU 核心，内存管理方式也不同。它引入了一种编写数据流水线的新方式，一旦你掌握了它，就会改变你对整个处理过程的看法。优化后的 Pandas 确实令人印象深刻，但它依然有上限。本文要探讨的，就是突破这个上限后的世界。

### Installing Polars and First Impressions
### 安装 Polars 与初步印象

Getting started was straightforward. If you’re on Google Colab like me, one line is all you need:
上手非常简单。如果你像我一样使用 Google Colab，只需一行代码：
`!pip install polars`

Done. No environment headaches, no dependency conflicts. That alone was a good start. But then I opened the Polars documentation and immediately noticed something. The syntax looked familiar enough — DataFrames, columns, filtering — but the way you’re supposed to think about operations felt different. In Pandas, you work with your data step by step. You do something, store the result, do something else. In Polars, you describe what you want as a single expression, and Polars figures out how to execute it. I didn’t fully understand that yet.
搞定。没有环境配置的烦恼，也没有依赖冲突。单凭这一点，就是一个好的开始。但当我打开 Polars 文档时，立刻注意到了一些东西。语法看起来很熟悉——DataFrame、列、过滤——但你思考操作的方式感觉不同。在 Pandas 中，你是按步骤处理数据的：做一件事，存储结果，再做下一件事。而在 Polars 中，你将需求描述为一个单一的表达式，然后由 Polars 决定如何执行它。我当时还没完全理解这一点。
