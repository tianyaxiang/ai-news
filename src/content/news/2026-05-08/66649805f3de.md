# Disentangled Learning Improves Implicit Neural Representations for Medical Reconstruction
# 解耦学习提升医学重建中的隐式神经表示

**Abstract:** Implicit neural representations (INRs) have emerged as a powerful paradigm for medical imaging via physics-informed unsupervised learning. Classical INRs optimize an entire network from scratch for each subject, leading to inefficient training and suboptimal imaging quality. Recent initialization-based approaches attempt to inject population priors into pre-trained networks, yet they rely on high-quality images and often suffer from catastrophic forgetting during fine-tuning.

**摘要：** 隐式神经表示（INRs）已成为通过物理信息无监督学习进行医学成像的一种强大范式。传统的 INR 方法需要为每个受试者从头开始优化整个网络，这导致了训练效率低下和成像质量欠佳的问题。近期基于初始化的方法尝试将群体先验注入预训练网络，但它们依赖于高质量图像，且在微调过程中往往会遭遇灾难性遗忘。

We present DisINR, a novel INR framework that explicitly disentangles shared and subject-specific representations. DisINR introduces a shared encoder-decoder pair and subject-specific encoders, whose features are jointly decoded for image reconstruction. By integrating differentiable forward models, it pre-trains the shared modules directly from limited raw measurements, removing the need for pre-acquired high-quality images.

我们提出了 DisINR，这是一种新型的 INR 框架，它明确地将共享表示与受试者特定表示进行了分离。DisINR 引入了一对共享的编码器-解码器以及受试者特定的编码器，其特征被联合解码以进行图像重建。通过集成可微正向模型，该框架可以直接从有限的原始测量数据中预训练共享模块，从而无需预先获取高质量图像。

During test-time adaptation, only the subject-specific encoder is optimized, while the shared pair remains frozen, effectively preserving learned priors. Extensive evaluations on three representative medical imaging tasks show that DisINR significantly outperforms state-of-the-art INRs in both reconstruction accuracy and efficiency.

在测试时适应阶段，仅优化受试者特定的编码器，而共享模块保持冻结，从而有效地保留了已学习的先验知识。在三个代表性医学成像任务上的广泛评估表明，DisINR 在重建精度和效率方面均显著优于现有的最先进 INR 方法。
