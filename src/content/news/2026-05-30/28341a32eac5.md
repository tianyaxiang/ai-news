# Five Questions About Chronos-2, the Time Series Foundation Model
# 关于时间序列基础模型 Chronos-2 的五个问题

**Part 1: A practitioner's walkthrough of univariate, multivariate, covariate-informed, and cold-start forecasting.**
**第一部分：从业者指南——涵盖单变量、多变量、协变量辅助及冷启动预测。**

Foundation models are now mainstream. We first saw them in language, then vision, and now also in video and speech. The recipe by now is familiar: first, pretrain a big neural net on large enough data, then apply the model to downstream tasks without any per-task adaptation.
基础模型现已成为主流。我们首先在语言领域见证了它们，随后是视觉，现在又扩展到了视频和语音领域。其核心配方已广为人知：首先，在足够大的数据集上预训练一个大型神经网络，然后将该模型直接应用于下游任务，而无需进行任何针对特定任务的调整。

For many industrial applications, time series is a crucial modality. We frequently need to do forecasting, anomaly detection, and classification by using different kinds of recording data. The current practice is usually to build dedicated models for one specific problem at hand. That can work, but it involves quite some “reinventing the wheel”, and may deliver suboptimal performance if the dataset for the current problem is small.
对于许多工业应用而言，时间序列是一种至关重要的模态。我们经常需要利用各种记录数据进行预测、异常检测和分类。目前的做法通常是针对手头的特定问题构建专用模型。这种方法虽然可行，但涉及大量的“重复造轮子”，且如果当前问题的相关数据集较小，其性能往往不尽如人意。

Naturally, we’d like to ask: can we apply the same recipe here, that is, pretrain a large time-series foundation model and use it for any downstream tasks, out of the box? That’s the bet behind time series foundation models, or TSFMs. In fact, a lot of work has already gone down this path, and we now see a zoo of such models, to name a few: TimesFM from Google, MOIRAI from Salesforce, Lag-Llama, TimeGPT, and the Chronos family from AWS.
自然地，我们不禁要问：我们能否在这里应用同样的配方，即预训练一个大型时间序列基础模型，并将其直接用于任何下游任务？这正是时间序列基础模型（TSFM）背后的赌注。事实上，许多研究已经沿着这条路径展开，我们现在看到了各种各样的此类模型，例如：谷歌的 TimesFM、Salesforce 的 MOIRAI、Lag-Llama、TimeGPT 以及亚马逊 AWS 的 Chronos 系列。

In this post, we look at Chronos-2 [1], the latest model in the Chronos line, released in October 2025. We’ll walk through five questions one might ask when encountering the model for the first time: What is a time series foundation model, and how does it change the analytics workflow? Why would a foundation model even work for time series? What is Chronos-2, specifically? What new things can we actually do with Chronos-2? Where does zero-shot stop being enough? For question 4, we’ll get hands-on with a case study on a synthetic building electricity demand dataset.
在本文中，我们将探讨 Chronos 系列的最新模型——于 2025 年 10 月发布的 Chronos-2 [1]。我们将通过五个初次接触该模型时可能会问的问题来深入了解：什么是时间序列基础模型，它如何改变分析工作流？为什么基础模型对时间序列有效？Chronos-2 具体是什么？我们能用 Chronos-2 做哪些新事情？零样本（zero-shot）预测在什么情况下不再适用？针对第四个问题，我们将通过一个合成建筑电力需求数据集的案例研究进行实操演示。

### 1. What is a time series foundation model, and how does it change the analytics workflow?
### 1. 什么是时间序列基础模型，它如何改变分析工作流？

As implied by its name, a TSFM is a single neural network pretrained on a large, diverse collection of time series. Its promise is the same as LLMs for text, i.e., instead of training a fresh model every time a new forecasting problem comes up, you load one pretrained model and ask it to forecast. That’s a big shift to the workflow.
顾名思义，TSFM 是一个在庞大且多样化的时间序列集合上预训练而成的单一神经网络。它的愿景与文本领域的 LLM 相同，即：不再为每一个新的预测问题重新训练模型，而是加载一个预训练好的模型并直接进行预测。这对工作流来说是一个巨大的转变。

Let’s say we’d like to do week-ahead energy demand forecasts for buildings. If we follow the traditional workflow, we’d start with preparing the data, followed by picking forecasting models, think of ARIMA/gradient-boosted trees/LSTM, TCN, N-BEATS, and then spending most of the project time on training, hyperparameter tuning, and validation. The output is a model that (hopefully) solves this one problem on this one dataset. Six months later, a new forecasting task arrives, and the cycle restarts almost from scratch.
假设我们要对建筑进行未来一周的能源需求预测。如果遵循传统工作流，我们首先要准备数据，接着选择预测模型（如 ARIMA、梯度提升树、LSTM、TCN 或 N-BEATS），然后将大部分项目时间花在训练、超参数调优和验证上。最终产出一个（希望）能解决该特定数据集上特定问题的模型。六个月后，当新的预测任务到来时，整个周期几乎又要从头开始。

Now with TSFM, most of what I described above is compressed into a single inference call. The workflow now becomes: Take the historical series (if available, also related covariates, we will discuss that later), input to the pretrained TSFM, set the desired forecast horizon, then run the TSFM inference and get back a forecast. What’s also nice about it is that you won’t just get a point forecast, but typically with predictive quantiles to quantify uncertainties.
现在有了 TSFM，上述大部分流程都被压缩成了一次推理调用。工作流变成了：获取历史序列（如果可用，还包括相关协变量，稍后讨论），输入到预训练的 TSFM 中，设置所需的预测范围，然后运行 TSFM 推理并获得预测结果。它的另一个优点是，你不仅能得到点预测，通常还能获得用于量化不确定性的预测分位数。

So what does this imply? Well, the first thing is that the cost of just trying out a forecast drops a lot. If it works, great. If not, you’ve learned something useful in just ten minutes. Then, cold-start is no longer a big issue. In the past, you might have had to stop a project simply because “we don’t really have enough data yet.” With a pretrained model, that “little data” might already be sufficient to deliver something meaningful. The model has already seen a lot of demand/traffic/sensor-like patterns. It’s bringing prior knowledge that your tiny dataset can’t fully represent.
这意味着什么呢？首先，尝试进行预测的成本大幅降低。如果成功了，那很好；如果失败了，你也在十分钟内学到了有用的东西。其次，冷启动不再是一个大问题。过去，你可能仅仅因为“数据不足”而不得不终止项目。而有了预训练模型，那“少量数据”可能已经足以提供有意义的结果。模型已经见过大量需求、流量或传感器类的模式，它带来了你的微小数据集无法完全体现的先验知识。

Finally, who can do this changes too. It used to take an ML expert to do proper forecasting. A TSFM, of course, doesn’t make any of that knowledge obsolete, but it does mean a domain expert with some Python knowledge can get a credible forecast without years of ML background. None of this is free, though. You’re now depending on somebody else’s model. Inference gets more expensive. For your domains, zero-shot probably won’t be good enough. And careful evaluation and validation become even more important.
最后，谁能做这件事也发生了变化。过去，进行专业的预测需要机器学习专家。当然，TSFM 并不会让那些专业知识过时，但它确实意味着具备一定 Python 知识的领域专家，无需多年的机器学习背景也能获得可靠的预测结果。然而，这一切并非没有代价。你现在依赖于他人的模型，推理成本变得更高。对于你的特定领域，零样本预测可能还不够好，因此仔细的评估和验证变得更加重要。

### 2. Why would a foundation model even work for time series?
### 2. 为什么基础模型对时间序列有效？

“I don’t believe in TSFM. This shouldn’t really work.” That’s what I hear most often from my colleagues, and that skepticism makes sense. Language is bounded and has a finite vocabulary. “Apple” means roughly the same thing in a novel or a grocery list. Numbers are not like that. Numbers are continuous, and their meaning can vary widely across contexts. A “100” in retail demand would have a very different meaning compared to a “100” in a heart-rate trace.
“我不相信 TSFM。这不应该真的有效。”这是我从同事那里听到最多的话，这种怀疑是有道理的。语言是有边界的，且词汇量有限。“Apple”在小说或购物清单中的含义大致相同。数字则不然。数字是连续的，其含义在不同语境下可能大相径庭。零售需求中的“100”与心率轨迹中的“100”含义截然不同。

So why should we hope a pretrained model can work across different contexts? Well, the model isn’t really learning your specific data; it’s learning shapes such as cycles, trends, level shifts, recurring spikes, and those shapes recur across time series of various domains. The shapes are the “vocabulary” here, and there are far fewer of them than there are possible numeric values. A model that has seen enough of them at enough scales and frequencies can hopefully recognize them in your series, even though it has never seen or been trained on your series before.
那么，为什么我们能指望预训练模型在不同语境下都能工作呢？因为模型学习的并不是你的特定数据，而是诸如周期、趋势、水平偏移、重复峰值等形状，而这些形状在不同领域的时间序列中反复出现。这些形状就是这里的“词汇”，它们的数量远少于可能的数值。一个在足够多的尺度和频率下见过足够多此类形状的模型，有望在你的序列中识别出它们，即使它从未见过或训练过你的序列。

Empirically, we have concrete numbers to support this: Chronos-2 currently holds the leading position in zero-shot accuracy across several benchmarks. In addition, recent work shows that Chronos-2 actually beats classical statistical baselines and specialized deep learning architectures, especially at longer horizons, with no task-specific tuning [2]. Of course, that doesn’t mean it always works.
从经验上看，我们有具体的数据支持这一点：Chronos-2 目前在多个基准测试的零样本准确率中处于领先地位。此外，近期研究表明，Chronos-2 在无需针对特定任务进行调优的情况下，实际上击败了经典的统计基准和专门的深度学习架构，尤其是在较长预测周期的情况下 [2]。当然，这并不意味着它总是有效。
