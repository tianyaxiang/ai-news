# Mechanistic origins of catastrophic forgetting: why RL preserves circuits better than SFT?
### 灾难性遗忘的机制起源：为什么强化学习比监督微调更能保留计算回路？

**Abstract:** Fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) frequently induces catastrophic forgetting of prior capabilities. Recent work has shown that reinforcement learning (RL) retains prior capabilities more effectively than supervised fine-tuning (SFT), attributing this to policy-gradient updates remaining closer to the base policy.

**摘要：** 对大型语言模型（LLM）进行微调往往会导致模型对先前能力的“灾难性遗忘”。近期的研究表明，强化学习（RL）比监督微调（SFT）能更有效地保留先前的能力，并将此归因于策略梯度更新（policy-gradient updates）更接近基础策略。

We extend this behavioral account to the mechanistic level and ask whether RL's advantage is mirrored by stronger preservation of internal computational circuits. We introduce differential circuit vulnerability, a head-level measure of how much a circuit degrades under fine-tuning, and use it to compare RL and SFT on Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct adapted to scientific question-answering.

我们将这种行为层面的解释扩展到了机制层面，并探讨 RL 的优势是否反映在对内部计算回路更强的保留上。我们引入了“差异化回路脆弱性”（differential circuit vulnerability）这一指标，用于衡量微调过程中回路退化的程度，并利用该指标在针对科学问答任务进行适配的 Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct 模型上对比了 RL 和 SFT。

We find a clear mechanistic trade-off: SFT adapts more rapidly to the target task but produces substantially greater circuit disruption and forgetting of prior capabilities, whereas RL preserves a larger fraction of the base circuit at the cost of slower task adaptation. These findings suggest that circuit preservation may help explain why RL is more robust to catastrophic forgetting. We released our code here.

我们发现了一个明确的机制权衡：SFT 能更快地适应目标任务，但会导致更严重的回路破坏和对先前能力的遗忘；而 RL 则以较慢的任务适应速度为代价，保留了更大比例的基础回路。这些发现表明，回路保留机制或许有助于解释为什么 RL 在应对灾难性遗忘时表现得更为稳健。我们已在此处发布了相关代码。
