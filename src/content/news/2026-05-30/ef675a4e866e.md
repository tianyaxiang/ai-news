# Trajectory Constraints for Imaging Inverse Problems
# 针对成像反问题的轨迹约束

Diffusion-based and iterative methods have become effective tools for solving imaging inverse problems. Their reconstruction process naturally forms a trajectory of intermediate estimates. Although these intermediate estimates define a reconstruction trajectory, most methods do not explicitly regularize the transitions between consecutive states.

基于扩散模型和迭代的方法已成为解决成像反问题的有效工具。它们的重建过程自然地形成了一条由中间估计值组成的轨迹。尽管这些中间估计值定义了一条重建轨迹，但大多数方法并未显式地对连续状态之间的转换进行正则化。

To address this limitation, we introduce TRACE, a training-free TRAjectory-Constrained rEconstruction framework that stabilizes the reconstruction path by coupling adjacent states along the trajectory. This gives a trajectory-level model that can be interpreted as a sequence of proximal updates. Since the exact proximal update is generally intractable, we approximate it with a neural mapping.

为了解决这一局限性，我们引入了 TRACE，这是一个无需训练的“轨迹约束重建”（TRAjectory-Constrained rEconstruction）框架。它通过耦合轨迹上相邻的状态来稳定重建路径。这提供了一种轨迹层面的模型，可以将其解释为一系列近端更新（proximal updates）。由于精确的近端更新通常难以计算，我们使用神经映射对其进行了近似。

This yields a diffusion-like reconstruction process with an explicit coupling between neighboring states. We provide a stability analysis showing that temporal coupling bounds trajectory variation and that this control is preserved under untrained network updates.

这产生了一种类似于扩散的重建过程，并在相邻状态之间实现了显式耦合。我们提供了稳定性分析，证明了时间耦合限制了轨迹的变化，并且这种控制在未经训练的网络更新下依然能够保持。

Experiments on linear and nonlinear image reconstruction tasks show that TRACE improves reconstruction quality. Trajectory-level analyses and ablations confirm that temporal coupling directly affects state transitions along the reconstruction path.

在线性和非线性图像重建任务上的实验表明，TRACE 提高了重建质量。轨迹层面的分析和消融实验证实，时间耦合直接影响了重建路径上的状态转换。
