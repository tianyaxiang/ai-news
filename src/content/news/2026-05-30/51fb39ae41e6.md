# Self-Play Reinforcement Learning under Imperfect Information in Big 2
# 大老二游戏中的非完美信息自我博弈强化学习

**Abstract:** Imperfect-information multiplayer games test whether agents can act under hidden information, sparse rewards, and non-stationary opponents. We study these challenges in Big 2, a four-player imperfect-information card game. 

**摘要：** 非完美信息的多人游戏旨在测试智能体在隐藏信息、稀疏奖励和非平稳对手环境下的行动能力。我们在“大老二”（Big 2）——一种四人非完美信息纸牌游戏中研究了这些挑战。

We develop a self-play RL framework for Big 2 that enables controlled comparisons between policy-gradient and value-approximating agents. Under a common environment, input representation, training budget, and evaluation protocol, PPO outperforms Monte Carlo Q approximation, SARSA, and Q-learning against random, greedy, and heuristic Big 2 opponents. 

我们为大老二开发了一个自我博弈强化学习框架，能够对策略梯度（policy-gradient）智能体和价值近似（value-approximating）智能体进行受控比较。在统一的环境、输入表示、训练预算和评估协议下，PPO 算法在对抗随机、贪婪和启发式大老二对手时，表现优于蒙特卡洛 Q 近似、SARSA 和 Q-learning。

We further find that moderate entropy regularization improves PPO by preventing the policy from becoming overly deterministic, and that current-policy self-play provides a stronger finite-budget curriculum than checkpoint self-play or fixed-opponent training. 

我们进一步发现，适度的熵正则化（entropy regularization）可以通过防止策略变得过于确定性来提升 PPO 的性能；同时，相比于检查点自我博弈（checkpoint self-play）或固定对手训练，当前策略自我博弈（current-policy self-play）在有限预算下提供了更有效的课程学习方案。

Together, these results show that Big 2 is a useful controlled setting for studying deep RL under imperfect information, multiplayer interaction, delayed rewards, and variable action sets.

综上所述，这些结果表明，大老二是一个有用的受控环境，适合用于研究非完美信息、多人交互、延迟奖励和可变动作集下的深度强化学习。
