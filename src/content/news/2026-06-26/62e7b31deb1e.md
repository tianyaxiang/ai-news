# One Month Into Learning Data Engineering in Public: Here’s What I Didn’t Write About
# 公开学习数据工程的一个月：那些我没写出来的事

A reflection on the first month of learning data engineering in public, and what actually kept me going.
这是对公开学习数据工程第一个月的反思，以及真正支撑我坚持下去的动力。

A few days ago I reached out to someone on LinkedIn I’d never met, asking what he thought was the best path into data engineering, and whether certifications were even worth pursuing. Turns out he’d already read one of my articles. So instead of giving me a fresh answer, he handed me back my own. “You’re on the right path,” he said. “You’re following the 12 month roadmap, stick to it. Don’t take suggestions from anyone, it might confuse you.”
几天前，我在 LinkedIn 上联系了一位素未谋面的同行，询问他认为进入数据工程领域的最佳路径是什么，以及考取证书是否值得。结果发现他已经读过我的一篇文章。因此，他没有给我一个新的答案，而是把我的话原封不动地还给了我。“你走在正确的道路上，”他说，“你正在遵循那份 12 个月的路线图，坚持下去。不要听取任何人的建议，那只会让你感到困惑。”

I read that message twice. Because two weeks before that, I had nearly talked myself out of the very roadmap I’d publicly committed to. Let me back up. It’s been a month since I published my first article on this journey, “From Data Analyst to Data Engineer: My 12-Month Self-Study Roadmap.”
我把那条信息读了两遍。因为就在两周前，我差点就说服自己放弃了那份我曾公开承诺要执行的路线图。让我回顾一下。距离我发布这段旅程的第一篇文章《从数据分析师到数据工程师：我的 12 个月自学路线图》已经过去一个月了。

Since then I’ve written three more, each one walking through something I built. An ETL pipeline from scratch. That same pipeline made production ready with SQLite and idempotency. Then scheduled with GitHub Actions, which turned into a lesson on portability before it became a lesson on scheduling. Those four articles are a fair record of what I did this month. They are not a fair record of what the month actually felt like. So this one is different. No code, no walkthroughs. Just the parts that didn’t fit anywhere else.
从那时起，我又写了三篇文章，每一篇都详细介绍了我的实践成果。从零构建一个 ETL 流水线；通过 SQLite 和幂等性将该流水线转化为生产就绪状态；随后使用 GitHub Actions 进行调度，这不仅是一次关于调度的课程，更演变成了一次关于可移植性的教学。这四篇文章客观记录了我这个月所做的事情，但它们无法真实反映我这个月的心理感受。所以，这一篇会有所不同。没有代码，没有教程，只记录那些无法归入其他地方的感悟。

### The plan said one thing, the month did another
### 计划是一回事，现实是另一回事

The roadmap laid things out in order. SQL first, then Python, then Git, then Spark, then Airflow, then Databricks. Tidy. Sequential. The kind of plan that looks great on a Notion board. I didn’t follow it. Not really. Instead of moving through the stack in order, I built one small pipeline and kept pushing on it until it broke in new ways. SQL showed up. Python showed up. Git showed up. But not in the order I planned, and not because I scheduled it that way. They showed up because the pipeline demanded them. I used to think that meant I was off track. Now I think it just means the plan was a starting point, not a contract. The roadmap got me moving. The pipeline decided what I actually needed to learn next.
路线图按顺序排列了学习内容：先 SQL，然后 Python，接着 Git、Spark、Airflow，最后是 Databricks。整洁、有序，看起来非常适合放在 Notion 看板上。但我并没有完全遵循它。与其按顺序学习技术栈，我选择构建了一个小型流水线，并不断对其进行改进，直到它以新的方式“崩溃”。SQL 出现了，Python 出现了，Git 也出现了。但它们不是按我计划的顺序出现的，也不是因为我预先安排了它们。它们的出现是因为流水线需要它们。我过去认为这意味着我偏离了轨道，但现在我认为这只是说明计划是一个起点，而不是一份合同。路线图让我动了起来，而流水线决定了我接下来真正需要学习什么。

### The walls were never really about the tools
### 遇到的障碍从来不只是关于工具本身

If you read the build articles, there’s a pattern I didn’t call out at the time. Every wall I hit had a technical fix and a non-technical lesson sitting underneath it. Idempotency wasn’t really about SQLite. It was about learning to distrust my own assumption that “it worked once” meant “it will keep working.” Persistence wasn’t really about Google Drive. It was about realizing my work could vanish the moment I closed a tab, and that I’d already lost a file once before without noticing how close I’d come to losing more. Portability wasn’t really about GitHub Actions. It was about discovering that one hardcoded path had quietly made my whole pipeline dependent on a single environment, without me ever deciding that on purpose. None of those are coding lessons. They’re thinking lessons that happened to surface through code. That feels like the real shape of data engineering so far, at least from where I’m standing. The tools are how the lessons show up. They’re not the lessons themselves.
如果你读过那些技术文章，会发现一个我当时没有指出的模式：我遇到的每一个障碍，表面上有技术解决方案，背后却隐藏着非技术的教训。幂等性不仅仅是关于 SQLite，它是关于学会不再盲目相信“运行过一次就代表以后也能一直运行”的假设。持久化不仅仅是关于 Google Drive，它是关于意识到我的工作成果可能在我关闭标签页的那一刻消失，而我之前已经丢失过一次文件，却没意识到自己差点丢失更多。可移植性不仅仅是关于 GitHub Actions，它是关于发现一个硬编码的路径悄悄地让我的整个流水线依赖于单一环境，而我从未刻意做出这种决定。这些都不是编程课，而是通过代码呈现出来的思维课。至少从我的角度来看，这就是目前数据工程的真实面貌。工具是课程呈现的载体，但它们本身并不是课程。

### The part the build articles didn’t show
### 技术文章中未曾展现的部分

Here’s what they didn’t show. Somewhere in the middle of this month, the energy that came from people watching started to fade. Not dramatically. It just got quieter. I stopped feeling the pressure I wrote about in article two, the one where strangers reached out excited to follow along. That pressure didn’t disappear because I stopped caring. It faded because the goal at the end of it, landing a high paying data engineering role, started to feel far away. Far enough that it stopped being motivating and started being exhausting to even think about. It didn’t help that my actual job got busier, and that I had to learn Laravel for work on top of everything else. Three to four hours a day sounds reasonable until you’re tired from your day job and the thing waiting for you at home is another unfamiliar stack. There were stretches where I seriously considered slowing down.
以下是那些文章没有展示的内容。这个月中旬，那种来自“被他人关注”的能量开始消退。不是剧烈地消失，只是变得安静了。我不再感受到我在第二篇文章中提到的那种压力——那种陌生人兴奋地联系我并想要跟随学习的压力。这种压力消失并不是因为我不在乎了，而是因为终极目标——获得一份高薪数据工程工作——开始显得遥不可及。它远到不再能激励我，反而让我想到它时感到疲惫。更糟糕的是，我的本职工作变得更忙了，而且我还需要额外学习 Laravel。每天三到四个小时的学习时间听起来很合理，直到你结束了一天的工作感到疲惫，而回到家等待你的又是另一个陌生的技术栈。有那么一段时间，我认真考虑过放慢脚步。

What kept me at the desk wasn’t the vision of the job anymore. It was smaller than that. I kept thinking about the one person out there who’s exactly where I was in May, stuck somewhere between analytics and engineering with no clear map, who might be waiting on the next article. Helping that one person became more motivating than the abstract idea of my own future role. I also made a point of looking for something genuinely challenging to fill my free time with, instead of the easy, mindless stuff that fills time without leaving anything behind. Even on the days the job felt far away, at least I wasn’t wasting the hours.
支撑我坐在书桌前的不再是那份工作的愿景，而是更具体的东西。我不断想到那个和我五月份处境相同的人——卡在分析与工程之间，没有清晰的路线图，可能正在等待我的下一篇文章。帮助那一个人，比我未来职位的抽象概念更具激励性。我还特意寻找真正具有挑战性的事情来填补空闲时间，而不是做那些轻松、无脑、无法留下任何积累的事情。即使在觉得工作遥不可及的日子里，至少我没有虚度光阴。

### Almost getting pulled off course
### 差点偏离航向

I also have to be honest about something I called out before I even started this journey. In my first article I admitted to having shiny object syndrome, jumping between design, animation, marketing, IT, and now data, before any of them got the chance to stick. I said I’d need to be intentional to avoid that happening here too. It happened anyway. Partway through the month I fell into a rabbit hole about certifications, whether I needed one, which one, whether I was even on the right path at all. That spiraled into seriously considering a pivot toward AI engineering instead, because in the moment it looked shinier and more in demand. I had to consciously stop and remind myself of the path I’d already chosen, and already told an audience I was committed to. Which brings me back to that message. The stranger who handed me my own advice. That LinkedIn message I mentioned at the start came right after that wobble. I’d reached out to someone for advice on the best path into data engineering, half looking for permission to second guess myself, half wonderi...
我必须诚实地面对我在开始这段旅程之前就提到过的问题。在第一篇文章中，我承认自己有“闪光物体综合症”（Shiny Object Syndrome），在设计、动画、市场营销、IT 和现在的数据领域之间跳来跳去，还没来得及深入就放弃了。我说过我需要刻意练习来避免这种情况再次发生。但它还是发生了。月中时，我陷入了关于证书的纠结：我是否需要证书？哪一个更好？我走的路径到底对不对？这导致我开始认真考虑转向 AI 工程，因为那一刻它看起来更耀眼、需求量更大。我不得不有意识地停下来，提醒自己我已经选择了这条路，并且已经向读者承诺过。这又回到了开头提到的那条信息——那位把我的建议还给我的陌生人。那条 LinkedIn 信息正是在我动摇之后发来的。我向他寻求进入数据工程的最佳路径，一半是想寻求许可来质疑自己，另一半则是想知道……
