# Beyond the Straight Line: Choosing Between OLS, Interaction Terms, and Tweedie Regression
# 超越直线：如何在 OLS、交互项和 Tweedie 回归之间做出选择

Mastering GLMs: When to Use OLS, Interaction Terms, or Tweedie Regression for Skewed Data
掌握广义线性模型（GLM）：何时针对偏态数据使用 OLS、交互项或 Tweedie 回归

Imagine you are building a machine learning model to predict how much money customers will spend on an e-commerce platform over the next year. The job is data science business as usual: load the data, clean it, understand it, and model the outcome. The big question that comes to our minds: Which algorithm do you pull out of your toolkit?
想象一下，你正在构建一个机器学习模型，用于预测客户在未来一年内将在电子商务平台上花费多少钱。这项工作是数据科学的日常：加载数据、清洗数据、理解数据并对结果进行建模。我们脑海中浮现出的最大问题是：你应该从工具箱中拿出哪种算法？

For regression models, I assume that we tend to default to the classics like OLS, or at other times, just jump straight to a complex ensemble method like XGBoost. But by experience, I have been seeing that often a generalized linear framework is exactly what you need for inference, speed, and interpretability. The real challenge lies in choosing the right flavor. Should we stick to a standard Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression? Do we need to introduce interaction terms? Or is our data weird enough to warrant a Tweedie regression?
对于回归模型，我认为我们倾向于默认使用像 OLS（普通最小二乘法）这样的经典方法，或者有时直接跳到像 XGBoost 这样复杂的集成方法。但根据经验，我发现广义线性框架往往正是你进行推理、追求速度和可解释性时所需要的。真正的挑战在于选择合适的“口味”。我们应该坚持使用标准的普通最小二乘法（OLS）回归吗？我们需要引入交互项吗？还是说我们的数据足够特殊，需要使用 Tweedie 回归？

Choosing the wrong one can lead to models that output impossible predictions, like a customer spending a negative amount of money. Let’s break down these three approaches so you can confidently pick the right tool for your specific data landscape.
选择错误的方法可能会导致模型输出不可能的预测结果，例如客户花费了负数的金额。让我们剖析这三种方法，以便你能自信地为你的特定数据场景选择正确的工具。

### Dataset
### 数据集

The dataset used for this exercise will be the French Motor Third-Party Liability Claims dataset, from R package CASDatasets, under license GPL >2.
本次练习使用的数据集是来自 R 语言包 CASDatasets 的法国机动车第三方责任险索赔数据集，遵循 GPL >2 许可协议。

After exploring the data a little, there are a couple of interesting transformations we can make, such as clipping the data at $500,000 maximum and keep the number of claims under 4, as numbers over that are way too crazy (outliers) and will definitely distort our predictions.
在对数据进行初步探索后，我们可以进行一些有趣的转换，例如将数据上限截断在 500,000 美元，并将索赔次数限制在 4 次以下，因为超过这些数值的数据过于离谱（异常值），肯定会扭曲我们的预测结果。

### 1. Regular OLS: The Reliable Highway
### 1. 常规 OLS：可靠的高速公路

Let’s start with a baseline model. The simplest Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression is the bedrock of statistical modeling. If data science had a “default” settings button, it would be OLS. But the “downside” is that it requires assumptions that the data will behave in a certain way.
让我们从基准模型开始。最简单的普通最小二乘法（OLS）回归是统计建模的基石。如果数据科学有一个“默认设置”按钮，那一定是 OLS。但它的“缺点”在于，它要求数据必须遵循特定的行为方式。

Mathematically, OLS looks for the line that minimizes the sum of the squared differences between your actual data points and that line. When does it work? OLS is incredibly powerful when your data meets a few core assumptions:
从数学上讲，OLS 寻找的是一条能使实际数据点与该直线之间的平方差之和最小化的直线。它在什么时候有效？当你的数据满足以下几个核心假设时，OLS 非常强大：

*   **Linearity:** The relationship between your features and your target is reasonably straight.
*   **线性：** 特征与目标之间的关系是相当直线的。
*   **Homoscedasticity:** The variance of your residuals (errors) is constant. In plain English, your model isn’t wildly more inaccurate for large values than it is for small values.
*   **同方差性：** 残差（误差）的方差是恒定的。通俗地说，你的模型不会在大数值上的表现比在小数值上表现得离谱得多。
*   **Normal Distribution:** The errors are normally distributed.
*   **正态分布：** 误差呈正态分布。

If you are predicting something stable and continuous (e.g., the height of a tree based on its age, or the temperature based on elevation), regular OLS is often your best bet. It’s transparent, fast, and easily understood. For this problem, though, we’ll see it’s not the best bet, given our data is inflated with zeroes claim amounts.
如果你正在预测某种稳定且连续的事物（例如根据树龄预测树高，或根据海拔预测温度），常规 OLS 通常是你的最佳选择。它透明、快速且易于理解。然而，对于当前这个问题，考虑到我们的数据中充斥着零索赔金额，我们将看到它并不是最佳选择。

### 2. OLS with Interaction Terms: The “It Depends” Factor
### 2. 带交互项的 OLS：“视情况而定”的因素

But what happens when the real world gets complicated? Let’s say you are predicting house prices based on two features: the number of bedrooms and whether the house has a swimming pool. A regular OLS model looks at these independently. It says, “An extra bedroom adds $50,000, and a pool adds $30,000.” But is that always true?
但当现实世界变得复杂时会发生什么呢？假设你正在根据两个特征预测房价：卧室数量和房屋是否有游泳池。常规 OLS 模型会独立地看待这些特征。它会说：“多一个卧室增加 50,000 美元，有一个游泳池增加 30,000 美元。”但这总是正确的吗？

Does a pool add the same value to a 1-bedroom condo as it does to a 5-bedroom luxury estate? Probably not. The value of the pool depends on the size of the house. This is where interaction terms come into play. An interaction term tells your model that the effect of one variable changes depending on the level of another variable.
游泳池对于一居室公寓和五居室豪华庄园的增值效果一样吗？可能不是。游泳池的价值取决于房屋的大小。这就是交互项发挥作用的地方。交互项告诉你的模型：一个变量的影响会随着另一个变量水平的变化而变化。
