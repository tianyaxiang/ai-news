With artificial intelligence, it has become easier to generate code, automate model training, compare metrics, and produce summary tables. A few well-structured prompts can now help a data scientist write Python scripts, estimate logistic regressions, compute AUC and Gini, generate plots, and document the results.
借助人工智能，生成代码、自动化模型训练、比较指标以及制作汇总表变得更加容易。现在，只需几个结构良好的提示词（Prompts），数据科学家就能编写 Python 脚本、估计逻辑回归、计算 AUC 和 Gini 系数、生成图表并记录结果。

But this speed creates a risk. A scoring model is not just an algorithm that runs successfully. It is not simply the model with the highest performance on the training sample. In a professional credit risk environment, a scoring model must be statistically sound, stable over time, interpretable, consistent with business expectations, and easy to monitor after deployment.
但这种速度也带来了风险。评分模型不仅仅是一个能成功运行的算法，也不仅仅是训练样本中表现最好的模型。在专业的信用风险环境中，评分模型必须在统计上合理、随时间推移保持稳定、具有可解释性、符合业务预期，并且在部署后易于监控。

The goal of this article is to present a clear methodology for training several scoring models, comparing their performance, assessing their stability, and selecting a final model based on statistical, business, and operational criteria.
本文旨在提出一套清晰的方法论，用于训练多个评分模型、比较其性能、评估其稳定性，并基于统计、业务和运营标准选择最终模型。

### The Datasets
### 数据集

In this article, we illustrate this foundational step using an open-source dataset available on Kaggle: the Credit Scoring Dataset. This dataset contains 32,581 observations and 12 variables describing loans issued by a bank to individual borrowers.
在本文中，我们将使用 Kaggle 上的开源数据集“信用评分数据集”（Credit Scoring Dataset）来说明这一基础步骤。该数据集包含 32,581 条观测值和 12 个变量，描述了银行向个人借款人发放的贷款情况。

Throughout this series, we have applied a range of processing steps to these variables in order to pre-select the candidate variables for the final model selection, subject to both statistical and regulatory constraints. In this application, the variables retained after the preselection steps are categorical. Most of them have two or three modalities. This is consistent with the previous stages of the methodology, where continuous variables were discretized to improve interpretability and make the final score easier to explain.
在本系列文章中，我们对这些变量应用了一系列处理步骤，以便在统计和监管约束下，为最终模型选择预选候选变量。在此应用中，预选步骤后保留的变量均为分类变量。其中大多数具有两个或三个模态。这与之前的方法论阶段一致，即通过对连续变量进行离散化，以提高可解释性并使最终评分更易于解释。

The target variable, denoted by Y, represents default status. In this case, it corresponds to the variable `loan_status`. It is defined as:
目标变量（记为 Y）代表违约状态。在本例中，它对应于变量 `loan_status`，定义如下：

$$Y = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if the borrower is in default} \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$$

The objective is to estimate the probability of default conditional on the observed characteristics:
其目标是估计在观测特征条件下的违约概率：

$$P(Y = 1 \mid X_1 = x_1, X_2 = x_2, \dots, X_{6} = x_{6})$$

The data are split into three main samples. The training sample is used to estimate the parameters of the candidate models. The test sample is used to evaluate model performance on observations that were not directly used to estimate the coefficients. The out-of-time sample is used to assess temporal stability. This is especially important in credit scoring. A model should not only perform well at the time of development; it should also remain stable when applied to a different time period.
数据被分为三个主要样本。训练样本用于估计候选模型的参数。测试样本用于评估模型在未直接用于估计系数的观测值上的表现。跨时间样本（Out-of-time sample）用于评估时间稳定性。这在信用评分中尤为重要。模型不仅要在开发时表现良好，在应用于不同时间段时也应保持稳定。

### Reformulating the Scoring Problem
### 重新定义评分问题

A scoring model estimates the relationship between a binary target variable $Y$ and a set of explanatory variables $X_1, X_2, \dots, X_6$. For each individual $i$, the model produces a score based on the estimated probability of default:
评分模型估计二元目标变量 $Y$ 与一组解释变量 $X_1, X_2, \dots, X_6$ 之间的关系。对于每个个体 $i$，模型根据估计的违约概率生成一个分数：

$$\text{Score}(x_i) = f \left(P(Y_i = 1 \mid X_{1,i}, X_{2,i}, \dots, X_{q,i})\right)$$

In credit scoring, the score must rank borrowers by risk. A good model should assign higher-risk scores, on average, to borrowers who default and lower-risk scores to borrowers who do not. This ranking ability is why discrimination metrics such as AUC and Gini are central in scoring. However, discrimination alone is not enough. A model can have good predictive power and still be unstable, difficult to interpret, or inconsistent with business logic. That is why the final model must be selected using several criteria, not just one performance metric.
在信用评分中，分数必须根据风险对借款人进行排序。一个好的模型应该平均为违约的借款人分配更高的风险分数，为未违约的借款人分配更低的风险分数。这种排序能力正是 AUC 和 Gini 系数等判别指标在评分中占据核心地位的原因。然而，仅有判别能力是不够的。一个模型可能具有良好的预测能力，但仍可能不稳定、难以解释或与业务逻辑不符。这就是为什么必须使用多个标准（而不仅仅是一个性能指标）来选择最终模型的原因。
