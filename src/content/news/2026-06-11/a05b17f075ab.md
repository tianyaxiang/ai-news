# Time Series as Language: A Universal Tokenizer for General-Purpose Time Series Foundation Models
# 时间序列即语言：用于通用时间序列基础模型的万能分词器

**Abstract:** While Next-Token Prediction (NTP) has unified LLM pretraining, its adaptation to unbounded, continuous time series (TS) remains open. To bridge the gap, we introduce UniTok, a universal tokenizer that transforms TS into discrete tokens, and UniTok-FM, a foundation model pretrained via NTP on these tokens.

**摘要：** 尽管“下一个词预测”（Next-Token Prediction, NTP）已经统一了大语言模型（LLM）的预训练范式，但将其应用于无界、连续的时间序列（TS）仍是一个未解难题。为了弥合这一差距，我们引入了 UniTok——一种将时间序列转换为离散词元（Token）的万能分词器，以及 UniTok-FM——一种通过在这些词元上进行 NTP 预训练的基础模型。

UniTok-FM is a general-purpose foundation model that supports zero-shot and prompt-boosted forecasting, as well as few-shot generation and classification via training-free in-context inference--a capability not achieved by prior works.

UniTok-FM 是一款通用基础模型，支持零样本（Zero-shot）和提示增强（Prompt-boosted）预测，并能通过无需训练的上下文推理（In-context inference）实现少样本（Few-shot）生成与分类——这是以往研究尚未实现的能力。

Technically, UniTok is a vector-quantized autoencoder incorporating prefix normalization for scale stabilization, a progressive-resolution causal architecture for encoding and decoding, and a structure-preserving reconstruction loss for training.

在技术层面，UniTok 是一种向量量化自编码器（Vector-quantized autoencoder），它结合了用于尺度稳定的前缀归一化（Prefix normalization）、用于编码与解码的渐进分辨率因果架构（Progressive-resolution causal architecture），以及用于训练的结构保持重构损失函数（Structure-preserving reconstruction loss）。

UniTok-FM adopts an off-the-shelf LLM architecture without TS-specific modifications. Instead of pretraining on isolated TS, it performs NTP on context windows formed by multiple series with similar patterns, aiming to capture their shared dynamics.

UniTok-FM 采用了现成的 LLM 架构，无需针对时间序列进行特殊修改。它不再基于孤立的时间序列进行预训练，而是在由多个具有相似模式的序列组成的上下文窗口上执行 NTP，旨在捕捉它们共同的动态特征。

Experiments on forecasting, generation, and classification show that a single unified UniTok-FM consistently outperforms statistical and supervised baselines, achieves competitive performance with task-specific foundation models, and uniquely enables training-free in-context inference across tasks.

在预测、生成和分类任务上的实验表明，单一统一的 UniTok-FM 始终优于统计学基准和监督学习基准，达到了与特定任务基础模型相媲美的性能，并独特地实现了跨任务的无需训练上下文推理。
