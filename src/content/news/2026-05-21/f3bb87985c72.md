# From Possible to Probable AI Models
# 从“可能”到“概率”：AI 模型的发展

**The real challenge in building reliable AI**
**构建可靠 AI 的真正挑战**

Sara A. Metwalli | May 20, 2026

Over the past couple of years, I have been involved in many conversations about generative AI (and you probably have, too!). These conversations varied in focus, from ones with the general public about the use of AI to ones with more technical people about the accuracy of models. Regardless of who I am conversing with, people are often fascinated and curious about what models can do.
在过去的几年里，我参与了许多关于生成式 AI 的对话（你可能也一样！）。这些对话的重点各不相同，从与大众探讨 AI 的使用，到与技术人员讨论模型的准确性。无论对话对象是谁，人们往往对模型能做的事情感到着迷和好奇。

Can an LLM write a functional kernel driver? It can. Can it write a song about how much you love your cat? It sure can. Can a diffusion model generate a photo-realistic image of a medieval astronaut? It can. But, does “can” mean it will be good? Turns out what is “possible” for most models can be a surprisingly low bar.
大语言模型（LLM）能写出功能性的内核驱动程序吗？能。它能写一首关于你有多爱你的猫的歌吗？当然能。扩散模型能生成一张中世纪宇航员的照片级图像吗？能。但是，“能做”是否意味着“做得好”？事实证明，对于大多数模型而言，“可能”的门槛低得惊人。

As someone who has studied probability or statistics, you probably know that in a sufficiently large sample space, almost anything becomes possible. The challenge is not determining whether an outcome can happen; it is understanding how likely that outcome is and whether we can depend on it repeatedly. That right there is something many confuse about probability theory: whether it is related to generative AI. That distinction matters because building a production AI system is very different from building a demo. Demos thrive on interesting edge cases. Production systems depend on consistency.
作为一个学习过概率或统计学的人，你可能知道，在一个足够大的样本空间中，几乎任何事情都有可能发生。挑战不在于确定某个结果是否会发生，而在于理解该结果发生的可能性有多大，以及我们是否能反复依赖它。这正是许多人在概率论与生成式 AI 的关系上容易混淆的地方。这种区别至关重要，因为构建生产级 AI 系统与构建演示程序（Demo）截然不同。演示程序依赖于有趣的边缘案例，而生产系统则依赖于一致性。

As AI systems become an increasingly huge and important part of workflows and decision-making, it is worth revisiting fundamental ideas from probability theory and examining where common assumptions about AI reliability begin to break down.
随着 AI 系统日益成为工作流和决策制定的重要组成部分，我们有必要重新审视概率论的基本概念，并考察关于 AI 可靠性的常见假设在何处开始失效。

### 1. Dimensionality and the Space of Possibilities
### 1. 维度与可能性空间

To be fair, talking about reliable systems is so much easier than building them. To understand why reliability remains very difficult, it helps to take a step back and think about sample spaces. Let’s start with the simplest of cases, a coin flip. For a coin toss: Ω={H,T}. The possible outcomes are easy to visualize because there is a small space of possibilities.
平心而论，谈论可靠系统比构建它们要容易得多。为了理解为什么可靠性仍然非常困难，我们需要退一步思考样本空间。让我们从最简单的例子开始：抛硬币。对于抛硬币：Ω={正面, 反面}。由于可能性空间很小，这些可能的结果很容易想象。

Now consider a language model generating a sequence of 512 tokens with a vocabulary of 50,000 possible tokens, which gives a sample space of size 50,000^512. The size of this sample space is almost impossible to comprehend, let alone visualize (in your head or in practice). In such cases, where we have a large space, the region corresponding to useful, coherent, and factually correct outputs can become surprisingly small relative to the number of plausible alternatives. In other words, the sea of possible outcomes, what is probable is a pond…
现在考虑一个语言模型生成 512 个 token 的序列，词汇表大小为 50,000，这产生了一个大小为 50,000^512 的样本空间。这个样本空间的大小几乎无法理解，更不用说在脑海中或实践中将其可视化了。在这种拥有巨大空间的情况下，对应于有用、连贯且事实正确输出的区域，相对于大量看似合理的替代方案而言，可能会变得小得惊人。换句话说，在可能结果的海洋中，概率较高的部分只是一个小池塘……

When the model returns an answer that it is possible, but not probable, we call it a hallucination. And a hallucination, then, is not necessarily a software bug. Instead, it happens because the model is sampling from regions of the distribution with non-zero probability but little practical value. At first glance, you may think: “If we simply collect more data, hallucinations will disappear.” But the challenge is that hallucinations naturally arise in probabilistic systems. Sampling from a distribution always introduces the possibility of landing in low-probability regions.
当模型返回一个“可能”但“概率极低”的答案时，我们称之为“幻觉”。因此，幻觉并不一定是一个软件错误。相反，它的发生是因为模型正在从分布中那些概率非零但几乎没有实际价值的区域进行采样。乍一看，你可能会想：“如果我们简单地收集更多数据，幻觉就会消失。”但挑战在于，幻觉在概率系统中是自然产生的。从分布中采样总是会引入落入低概率区域的可能性。

### 2. Frequentist measurements vs Bayesian expectations
### 2. 频率派测量与贝叶斯期望

When evaluating AI systems, there are often two very different approaches. The first is, more or less, a frequentist perspective: you run 1000 benchmark tasks and measure performance. If a model solves 850 correctly, we call it an 85% accurate system. The second is a Bayesian perspective, where you start with expectations about how an intelligent system should behave and update those beliefs when unexpected failures happen.
在评估 AI 系统时，通常有两种截然不同的方法。第一种或多或少是频率派的视角：运行 1000 个基准测试任务并测量性能。如果模型正确解决了 850 个，我们就称其为 85% 准确的系统。第二种是贝叶斯视角，即从对智能系统应如何表现的期望出发，并在发生意外故障时更新这些信念。

This difference becomes important because prompts are rarely independent events. Suppose a model answers nine math questions correctly. Based on that, we may assume the probability of getting question ten right is its reported accuracy. But language models are not a collection of isolated Bernoulli trials. Their outputs depend on previous context, hidden representations, and the density of related examples within the training distribution. Which means their performance is often conditional rather than static.
这种差异之所以重要，是因为提示词（Prompts）很少是独立的事件。假设一个模型正确回答了九道数学题。基于此，我们可能会假设第十道题答对的概率就是其报告的准确率。但语言模型并非一系列孤立的伯努利试验。它们的输出取决于先前的上下文、隐藏表示以及训练分布中相关示例的密度。这意味着它们的性能通常是条件性的，而非静态的。

### 3. Confidence is not the same as probability
### 3. 置信度不等于概率

One of the most commonly used functions in machine learning is the Softmax function. We often interpret Softmax outputs as confidence scores: “If the model outputs 0.90 for cat, it is 90% sure.” But this interpretation can be misleading. Okay, step back for a second: the Softmax function states that because of the exponential term, small differences between logits can be amplified. So, a model can appear highly confident not because it “knows” something, but because one logit happened to be slightly larger than the others and the exponential operation amplified the difference.
机器学习中最常用的函数之一是 Softmax 函数。我们经常将 Softmax 的输出解释为置信度分数：“如果模型对‘猫’的输出是 0.90，那么它有 90% 的把握。”但这种解释可能会产生误导。退一步讲：Softmax 函数指出，由于指数项的存在，Logit 之间的微小差异会被放大。因此，模型看起来非常自信，并不是因为它“知道”什么，而仅仅是因为某个 Logit 恰好比其他的大了一点点，而指数运算放大了这种差异。

So when ChatGPT predicts the next word, what it is essentially doing is answering: “Of all possible tokens, after Softmax, which one is most likely?” This creates what I think of as the “confident fool” problem: a system confidently asserting something incorrect because it has not learned how to express uncertainty.
因此，当 ChatGPT 预测下一个词时，它本质上是在回答：“在所有可能的 Token 中，经过 Softmax 处理后，哪一个最有可能？”这造成了我所认为的“自信的傻瓜”问题：一个系统自信地断言错误的事情，因为它还没有学会如何表达不确定性。

### 4. The Law of Large Numbers and why more data does not automatically mean more truth
### 4. 大数定律：为什么更多的数据并不自动意味着更多的真理

The Law of Large Numbers states that as sample sizes increase, observed averages approach their expected values. This idea often motivates the use of extremely large datasets to train our models. After all, if a model sees enough examples, eventually it should learn the truth, right? At first glance, this sounds reasonable, mainly because that is how we learn!
