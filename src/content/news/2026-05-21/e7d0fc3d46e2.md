# ReCrit: Transition-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Scientific Critic Reasoning

**Computer Science > Machine Learning arXiv:2605.18799 (cs) [Submitted on 11 May 2026]**

**计算机科学 > 机器学习 arXiv:2605.18799 (cs) [2026年5月11日提交]**

***

**Abstract:** Large language models can fail in critic interaction not only by answering incorrectly, but also by abandoning an initially correct scientific solution after user criticism. This is especially risky in scientific reasoning, where user criticism can turn a valid answer into an incorrect one. We frame critic interaction as an inter-turn correctness-transition problem rather than a final-answer accuracy problem, and identify three challenges: transition awareness, decoupling useful correction from harmful sycophancy, and scalable rollout.

**摘要：** 大型语言模型在评论交互（critic interaction）中可能会失败，这不仅表现为回答错误，还表现为在用户提出批评后放弃了最初正确的科学解决方案。在科学推理中，这种情况尤其危险，因为用户的批评可能会将一个有效的答案变成错误的答案。我们将评论交互定义为一个“轮次间正确性转换”问题，而非单纯的“最终答案准确性”问题，并确定了三个挑战：转换感知（transition awareness）、将有用的修正与有害的阿谀奉承（sycophancy）区分开来，以及可扩展的展开（scalable rollout）。

***

We propose ReCrit, a transition-aware reinforcement learning framework that decomposes Initial-to-Critic behavior into four quadrants: Correction, Sycophancy, Robustness, and Boundary. ReCrit rewards correction and robustness, penalizes sycophancy, and treats persistent errors as weak boundary signals. To make interaction training practical, ReCrit further uses dynamic asynchronous rollout with tail-adaptive completion to reduce rollout waiting.

我们提出了 ReCrit，这是一个转换感知强化学习框架，它将“初始到评论”的行为分解为四个象限：修正（Correction）、阿谀奉承（Sycophancy）、鲁棒性（Robustness）和边界（Boundary）。ReCrit 对修正和鲁棒性给予奖励，对阿谀奉承进行惩罚，并将持续的错误视为弱边界信号。为了使交互训练更具实用性，ReCrit 进一步采用了带有尾部自适应补全的动态异步展开（dynamic asynchronous rollout），以减少展开过程中的等待时间。

***

On three scientific reasoning benchmarks, ChemBench, TRQA, and EarthSE, ReCrit improves average Critic accuracy from 38.15 to 51.49 on Qwen3.5-4B and from 45.40 to 55.59 on Qwen3.5-9B. Ablations show that final-answer rewards provide little interaction-level gain, while transition-aware rewards and quadrant weighting produce more distinguishable training signals and larger net Critic-stage improvement.
