### Accurate Evaluation of Quickest Changepoint Detectors via Non-parametric Survival Analysis
### 通过非参数生存分析对最快变点检测器进行精确评估

**Abstract:** We propose non-parametric estimators for the average run length (ARL) and average detection delay (ADD) in quickest changepoint detection (QCD) under finite and irregular sequence lengths. Although ARL and ADD are widely used as optimality criteria in theoretical and simulation studies, their application to real-world datasets is hindered by limited and irregular sequence lengths.

**摘要：** 我们提出了一种针对最快变点检测（QCD）中平均运行长度（ARL）和平均检测延迟（ADD）的非参数估计方法，适用于有限且不规则的序列长度。尽管 ARL 和 ADD 在理论和模拟研究中被广泛用作最优性准则，但由于现实世界数据集序列长度有限且不规则，其应用受到了限制。

To address this issue, we propose non-parametric estimators for the ARL and ADD, termed KM-ARL and KM-ADD, by drawing an analogy between QCD and survival analysis to model detection probabilities under sequence truncation. We derive estimation bias bounds and prove that they are asymptotically unbiased unless extrapolation is required.

为了解决这一问题，我们通过类比 QCD 与生存分析，对序列截断下的检测概率进行建模，从而提出了名为 KM-ARL 和 KM-ADD 的 ARL 与 ADD 非参数估计量。我们推导了估计偏差界限，并证明了在无需外推的情况下，这些估计量是渐近无偏的。

Experiments on simulated and real-world datasets demonstrate their practical utility, enhancing robustness against limited and irregular sequence lengths, improving interpretability, and facilitating empirical, intuitive model selection. Our Python code is provided at this URL, offering ready-to-use implementations for practitioners.

在模拟数据集和真实数据集上的实验证明了其实用价值，增强了对有限和不规则序列长度的鲁棒性，提高了可解释性，并促进了经验性、直观的模型选择。我们的 Python 代码已在指定链接提供，为从业者提供了可直接使用的实现方案。
