# Stop Evaluating LLMs with “Vibe Checks”
# 停止用“感觉评估”来衡量大语言模型

**Agentic AI: How to build a decision-grade scorecard for AI agents**
**智能体 AI：如何为 AI 智能体构建决策级评分标准**

Ari Joury, PhD | May 15, 2026 | 7 min read
Ari Joury 博士 | 2026年5月15日 | 阅读需 7 分钟

Vibes are not enough for high-stakes business AI.
对于高风险的商业 AI 而言，仅凭“感觉”是远远不够的。

Imagine you are an engineering manager. Your team has just spent three weeks refactoring the prompt chain for your company’s internal AI research agent. They deploy the new version to a staging environment, run a few queries, and report back: “It feels much better. The answers are more detailed.” If you approve that deployment based on a “vibe check,” you are flying blind.
想象一下你是一位工程经理。你的团队刚刚花了三周时间重构了公司内部 AI 研究智能体的提示词链（prompt chain）。他们将新版本部署到测试环境，运行了几次查询后汇报说：“感觉好多了，回答更详细了。”如果你仅凭这种“感觉评估”就批准了部署，那你就是在盲目决策。

In traditional software engineering, we would never accept “it feels better” as a passing test grade. We demand unit tests, integration tests, and deterministic assertions. Yet, when it comes to Large Language Models (LLMs) and agentic systems, many teams abandon engineering rigor and revert to subjective human evaluation. This is a primary reason why enterprise AI projects fail to scale. You cannot optimize what you cannot measure, and you cannot safely iterate on a system if you do not know when it breaks. To move an AI system from a fragile demo to a robust production asset, you must build a decision-grade evaluation scorecard.
在传统的软件工程中，我们绝不会接受“感觉更好”作为通过测试的标准。我们要求单元测试、集成测试和确定性断言。然而，在大语言模型（LLM）和智能体系统方面，许多团队放弃了工程严谨性，退回到主观的人工评估。这就是企业 AI 项目难以规模化的主要原因。你无法优化无法衡量的事物，如果你不知道系统何时崩溃，也就无法安全地进行迭代。要将 AI 系统从脆弱的演示版转变为稳健的生产资产，你必须构建一套决策级的评估评分标准。

### The Accuracy Trap
### 准确率陷阱

The most common mistake teams make is optimizing solely for accuracy. Accuracy is necessary, but it is entirely insufficient for production. A system that consistently gives the wrong answer is inaccurate but reliable. A system that gives the perfect answer 9 times out of 10, but crashes the orchestration pipeline on the 10th try, is accurate but unreliable.
团队最常犯的错误是仅仅为了准确率而优化。准确率是必要的，但对于生产环境来说完全不够。一个始终给出错误答案的系统是不准确的，但它是可靠的。一个 10 次中有 9 次给出完美答案，但在第 10 次尝试时导致编排流水线崩溃的系统，虽然准确，但不可靠。

Furthermore, accuracy does not capture the operational realities of the business. An agent that costs $50 per run because it recursively calls GPT-4o twenty times is not production-ready, regardless of how accurate it is. An agent that takes five minutes to respond to a real-time customer support query has already failed, even if the eventual answer is flawless. As noted in recent discussions on agentic AI latency and cost, these operational metrics are just as critical as the model’s intelligence.
此外，准确率无法反映业务的运营现实。如果一个智能体因为递归调用 GPT-4o 二十次而导致单次运行成本高达 50 美元，那么无论它多么准确，都无法投入生产。如果一个智能体需要五分钟才能响应实时客户支持查询，那么即使最终答案完美无缺，它也已经失败了。正如近期关于智能体 AI 延迟和成本的讨论中所指出的，这些运营指标与模型本身的智能水平同样关键。

When you optimize only for accuracy, you often inadvertently degrade latency and cost. A more complex prompt might yield a slightly better answer, but if it doubles the token count and adds three seconds to the response time, the overall user experience may actually be worse. This trade-off is a fundamental challenge in evaluating AI agents, where balancing intelligence with operational efficiency is key.
当你只优化准确率时，往往会无意中牺牲延迟和成本。更复杂的提示词可能会带来稍微好一点的答案，但如果它使 Token 数量翻倍并增加了三秒的响应时间，那么整体用户体验实际上可能会变差。这种权衡是评估 AI 智能体的一个根本挑战，平衡智能与运营效率是关键所在。

### The 5 Dimensions of Decision-Grade Quality
### 决策级质量的 5 个维度

A robust evaluation framework must measure five distinct dimensions. When you build your automated test suites, you must define specific, quantifiable metrics for each of these:
一个稳健的评估框架必须衡量五个不同的维度。在构建自动化测试套件时，你必须为每个维度定义具体的、可量化的指标：

1. **Accuracy (准确性):** Is the output factually correct and grounded in the provided source data? (Measurement: Automated comparison against a golden dataset using an LLM-as-a-judge to check for hallucinated entities).
   输出是否在事实层面正确，并基于提供的源数据？（衡量方式：使用“LLM 作为裁判”对黄金数据集进行自动比对，检查是否存在幻觉实体）。
2. **Reliability (可靠性):** Does the system consistently produce a valid output without crashing the pipeline? (Measurement: Schema validation pass rate. JSONDecodeError rate must be 0%).
   系统是否能持续产生有效输出而不导致流水线崩溃？（衡量方式：模式验证通过率。JSONDecodeError 率必须为 0%）。
3. **Latency (延迟):** Is the system fast enough for the specific workflow it serves? (Measurement: P90 and P99 response times measured in milliseconds or seconds). The hidden costs of agentic AI often manifest as unacceptable latency spikes when agents get stuck in recursive loops.
   系统对于其服务的特定工作流来说是否足够快？（衡量方式：以毫秒或秒为单位测量的 P90 和 P99 响应时间）。智能体 AI 的隐性成本往往表现为当智能体陷入递归循环时出现的不可接受的延迟峰值。
4. **Cost (成本):** Is the token usage and compute cost sustainable at scale? (Measurement: Average cost per successful run, tracked via API billing metrics).
   Token 使用量和计算成本在规模化时是否可持续？（衡量方式：通过 API 账单指标跟踪的单次成功运行平均成本）。
5. **Decisions (决策):** Does the output actually help the user make a better business decision? (Measurement: Downstream business metrics, such as reduction in manual review time or increase in task completion rate).
   输出是否真正帮助用户做出了更好的商业决策？（衡量方式：下游业务指标，例如人工审核时间的减少或任务完成率的提高）。

### Building the Golden Dataset
### 构建黄金数据集

You cannot automate evaluation without a baseline. This is your “golden dataset.” A golden dataset is a curated collection of diverse inputs paired with their expected, ideal outputs. It should not just cover the “happy path”; it must include edge cases, malformed inputs, and adversarial prompts. As detailed in guides on building golden datasets for AI evaluation, this dataset is the foundation of your entire testing strategy.
没有基准，就无法实现自动化评估。这就是你的“黄金数据集”。黄金数据集是一个精心策划的集合，包含多样化的输入及其对应的预期理想输出。它不仅应涵盖“理想路径”，还必须包括边缘情况、格式错误的输入和对抗性提示词。正如关于构建 AI 评估黄金数据集的指南中所述，该数据集是你整个测试策略的基石。

Creating a golden dataset is labor-intensive. It requires domain experts to manually review and annotate hundreds or thousands of examples. However, this upfront investment pays massive dividends down the line. Once you have a robust golden dataset, you can evaluate new models or prompt changes in minutes rather than days.
创建黄金数据集是劳动密集型的。它需要领域专家手动审查和标注成百上千个示例。然而，这种前期投入在未来会带来巨大的回报。一旦拥有了稳健的黄金数据集，你就可以在几分钟内（而不是几天内）评估新模型或提示词的变更。

When you update your agent’s prompt or swap out the underlying foundation model, you run the new version against the entire golden dataset. You then use an automated evaluation pipeline (often utilizing a separate, highly capable LLM as an evaluator) to compare the new outputs against the golden outputs across the five dimensions. If the new version improves accuracy but spikes latency beyond your acceptable threshold, the deployment fails. If it reduces cost but introduces schema validation errors, the deployment fails. This rigorous approach is essential for regulated AI applications, where failures can have severe legal and financial consequences.
当你更新智能体的提示词或更换底层基础模型时，你需要用整个黄金数据集来运行新版本。然后，使用自动化评估流水线（通常利用另一个能力强大的 LLM 作为评估者）在五个维度上将新输出与黄金输出进行比对。如果新版本提高了准确率但导致延迟超过了可接受的阈值，则部署失败。如果它降低了成本但引入了模式验证错误，则部署失败。这种严谨的方法对于受监管的 AI 应用至关重要，因为在这些领域，失败可能导致严重的法律和财务后果。

### The Evaluation Pyramid
### 评估金字塔

Building this scorecard requires thinking about evaluation at four distinct levels:
构建这套评分标准需要从四个不同的层面思考评估：

* **Unit (单元):** Does the specific prompt or function work in isolation?
  特定的提示词或函数是否能独立工作？
* **Integration (集成):** Do the multiple agents or tools in the chain pass data to each other correctly?
  链中的多个智能体或工具是否能正确地相互传递数据？
* **System (系统):** Does the entire pipeline work end-to-end under realistic load conditions?
  整个流水线在真实的负载条件下是否能端到端地工作？
* **Decision (决策):** Does the final output drive the intended business outcome?
  最终输出是否推动了预期的业务成果？

Most teams never leave the Unit level. They test a prompt in a playground environment and assume the system is ready. But agentic systems are complex, interacting components. A prompt that works perfectly in isolation might fail catastrophically when its output is passed to a downstream tool that expects a different format.
