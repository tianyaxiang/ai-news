# The Clapper was a bad smart home gadget — and a viral sensation
# “The Clapper”：一款糟糕的智能家居产品，却成了病毒式传播的现象级爆款

Clap on. Clap off. Well, more like, Clap, pause for half a beat but no longer because otherwise it’ll stop hearing you, clap again because you waited too long, clap louder and faster, that didn’t work, clap two more times, and suddenly: on. The Clapper didn’t always work — and even when it did, it might fry your gadgets in the process — but it managed to become a big hit anyway.

“拍手开，拍手关。” 实际上，它的操作更像是：拍一下，停顿半拍（不能太久，否则它就听不到了），再拍一下（因为你等太久了），再拍得更响、更快一点，还是没反应，再多拍两下，突然：开了。The Clapper 并不总是管用——即便它真的起作用了，有时甚至还会顺带烧坏你的电器——但它最终还是成功地成为了一个大热门。

On this episode of Version History, we tell the story of The Clapper, from its beginnings as The Great American Turn-On to its debut alongside one of the great advertising campaigns of all time. The Verge’s David Pierce and Victoria Song are joined by Allison Marsh, a writer and professor at The University of South Carolina, to dig into the device’s history and legacy, and to try and figure out once and for all whether this was a good product or just a great commercial.

在本期《Version History》播客中，我们将讲述 The Clapper 的故事，从它最初以“The Great American Turn-On”之名诞生，到它伴随着史上最伟大的广告宣传活动之一首次亮相。The Verge 的 David Pierce 和 Victoria Song 邀请了作家兼南卡罗来纳大学教授 Allison Marsh，共同挖掘这款设备的历史与遗产，并试图彻底弄清楚：这究竟是一款好产品，还是仅仅是一个伟大的广告营销案例？
