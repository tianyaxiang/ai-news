# Loop Engineering with Adaptive Parsing in Action: Parsing Flat Tables with Azure and Figures with a Vision LLM

**Large Language Models Loop Engineering with Adaptive Parsing in Action: Parsing Flat Tables with Azure and Figures with a Vision LLM Enterprise Document Intelligence [Vol.1 #10B] – The LLM as last line of defence, then two real escalations walked end to end: a flat table to Azure, a figure to a vision model**

大型语言模型循环工程与自适应解析实战：使用 Azure 解析平面表格，使用视觉大模型解析图表——企业文档智能 [第1卷 #10B] —— 将大模型作为最后一道防线，并完整演示两个实际的升级案例：将平面表格交给 Azure 处理，将图表交给视觉模型处理。

***

Some bad parses do not look bad until the answer comes out (classic OCR is the textbook case: EasyOCR recovers the words and quietly drops the table structure around them, and the answer reads fine until you check it against the page). A deterministic check catches the obvious failures for free, but a table that parsed into plausible-looking nonsense sails right through it, and the model answers from that nonsense with full confidence. Nothing upstream flagged it, because on paper the parse looked fine. The last line of defence is the LLM reading its own input before the user ever sees the answer.

有些糟糕的解析在答案出来之前看起来并不明显（经典的 OCR 就是教科书式的案例：EasyOCR 提取了文字，却悄无声息地丢弃了周围的表格结构，答案读起来很通顺，直到你将其与原页面进行核对）。确定性检查可以免费捕获明显的失败，但如果表格被解析成看起来“合情合理”的乱码，它就能轻易通过检查，模型会满怀信心地基于这些乱码给出答案。上游没有任何环节标记出错误，因为从文档上看，解析结果似乎没问题。最后一道防线就是让大模型在用户看到答案之前，先审视自己的输入。

PyMuPDF parses a page in five milliseconds for free. A vision LLM on the same page can cost ten thousand times more and take ten seconds. When the question lives in plain prose, the cheap parser wins. When the answer hides inside a table, a figure, a scanned region, or a flattened layout, the cheap parser silently returns nothing useful and the LLM confidently answers the wrong question. Running the heaviest parser on every page is wasteful. Running the cheapest one everywhere is wrong.

PyMuPDF 解析一个页面只需 5 毫秒且免费。而对同一页面使用视觉大模型，成本可能高出万倍，耗时需 10 秒。当问题存在于纯文本中时，低成本解析器胜出。当答案隐藏在表格、图表、扫描区域或扁平化布局中时，低成本解析器会悄无声息地返回无用信息，而大模型则会自信地回答错误的问题。对每一页都运行最强大的解析器是浪费，而到处都只运行最便宜的解析器则是错误的。

***

The trick is to start cheap and escalate only when something says the cheap parse missed the answer. That signal has to come from the pipeline itself: a chain of checks against the parse output, the question, and the LLM’s own footprint when it tries to use what came out. The way around this is a feedback loop the pipeline builds for itself. Start with the cheapest parser. Evaluate its output at every check of the pipeline. Escalate to a deeper parser only when at least one check flags the cheap parse as insufficient for the question.

诀窍在于：从低成本开始，仅在有迹象表明低成本解析遗漏了答案时才进行升级。该信号必须来自流水线本身：即针对解析输出、问题以及大模型在尝试使用解析结果时留下的“足迹”进行一系列检查。解决这个问题的办法是流水线自建的反馈循环。从最便宜的解析器开始，在流水线的每个检查点评估其输出。仅当至少有一个检查点标记低成本解析不足以回答问题时，才升级到更深层的解析器。

***

The evaluation is not one check at one place. It is a cascade: pre-parsing metadata routes most of the corpus before any extraction; parsing-time outputs flag flattened tables and opaque figures; retrieval scoring catches anchors that drift; generation flags what survived all of the above. Each check is cheaper than the next, more reliable than the next, and asks its own question: “did the parser produce enough to answer?”.

评估并非在单一位置进行一次检查，而是一个级联过程：预解析元数据在提取前对大部分语料进行路由；解析时的输出标记出扁平化表格和不透明图表；检索评分捕获偏移的锚点；生成阶段标记出所有上述检查都未发现的问题。每一项检查都比下一项更便宜、更可靠，并提出自己的问题：“解析器产生的内容足以回答问题吗？”

***

The first part caught failures cheaply, before generation. This part is what happens when they slip through: the LLM’s own footprint at generation time (check 7, self-evaluation; check 8, groundedness) flags a parse the deterministic checks missed, and the pipeline escalates the offending page to a deeper parser. Two recurring examples are walked end to end: Table 3 of the Attention paper (page 9, a flat-parsed grid escalated to Azure Layout) and Figure 1 (page 3, an opaque diagram escalated to a vision LLM). Both are real, both come up in enterprise corpora every day.

第一部分在生成之前以低成本捕获了失败。本部分探讨的是当错误漏网时会发生什么：大模型在生成时的“足迹”（检查 7：自我评估；检查 8：扎根性/事实一致性）标记出了确定性检查所遗漏的解析错误，流水线随后将出错页面升级到更深层的解析器。文中完整演示了两个反复出现的例子：Attention 论文中的表 3（第 9 页，平面解析的网格升级到 Azure Layout）和图 1（第 3 页，不透明图表升级到视觉大模型）。两者都是真实的，且每天都会出现在企业语料库中。
