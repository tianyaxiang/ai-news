# From hyperplanes to hyperellipsoids: characterizing the inherent interpretability of linear and single-qubit mixed-state binary classification models
# 从超平面到超椭球体：表征线性模型与单量子比特混合态二分类模型的内在可解释性

**Abstract:** We characterize and compare the inherent interpretability offerings of a standard linear model with a single qubit mixed state model for the task of supervised binary classification.
**摘要：** 我们针对监督二分类任务，表征并比较了标准线性模型与单量子比特混合态模型所提供的内在可解释性。

A side by side comparison reveals that a single qubit mixed state model for binary classification is just the ``ellipsoid version" of standard linear model classification. More precisely, rather than learning a hyperplane to classify data, we learn a hyperellipsoid.
通过对比分析发现，用于二分类的单量子比特混合态模型实际上就是标准线性分类模型的“椭球体版本”。更准确地说，我们学习的不再是用于分类数据的超平面，而是一个超椭球体。

We discuss the consequences of the geometric inductive biases of both models, as well as how each model contains a different feature importance inductive bias.
我们讨论了两种模型几何归纳偏置所带来的影响，以及每种模型如何包含不同的特征重要性归纳偏置。

This short characterization offers an accessible route to quantum machine learning (ML) ideas for readers who have zero background in quantum and are only familiar with linear classification in ML.
这一简短的表征为那些没有量子背景、仅熟悉机器学习中线性分类的读者，提供了一条通往量子机器学习（ML）概念的易懂路径。

In support of ML pedagogy, we encourage instructors to utilize this piece to smoothly introduce quantum ML ideas into the undergraduate ML classroom.
为了支持机器学习教学，我们鼓励教师利用本文，将量子机器学习的概念平滑地引入本科机器学习课堂。
