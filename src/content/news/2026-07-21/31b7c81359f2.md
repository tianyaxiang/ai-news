# A Transportable Threshold-Based Framework for Interpretable Classification of Medical Data
# 一种用于医疗数据可解释分类的可迁移阈值框架

**Abstract:** Black-box models limit the adoption of artificial intelligence in medicine due to their lack of interpretability and reproducibility. We introduce a statistically grounded framework that provides fully interpretable, rule-based clinical classification using the Bernoulli Naïve Bayes (BNB) model.

**摘要：** 由于缺乏可解释性和可重复性，黑盒模型限制了人工智能在医学领域的应用。我们引入了一个基于统计学的框架，利用伯努利朴素贝叶斯（BNB）模型提供完全可解释的、基于规则的临床分类。

The method applies supervised $\chi^2$-guided statistical binarization to continuous variables, identifying thresholds that maximize association with clinical outcomes within the training data. This transformation allows BNB to operate effectively on continuous medical data without sacrificing its inherent transparency.

该方法对连续变量应用了受监督的 $\chi^2$ 引导统计二值化，识别出在训练数据中与临床结果关联度最大的阈值。这种转换使得 BNB 能够在不牺牲其固有透明度的情况下，有效地处理连续医疗数据。

The approach was evaluated on three benchmark datasets, Pima Indians Diabetes, Wisconsin Breast Cancer, and Heart Failure Prediction, achieving area-under-the-curve (AUC) scores of 0.800 for the Pima analysis, 0.984 for Wisconsin Breast Cancer, and 0.919 for Heart Failure Prediction.

该方法在三个基准数据集（Pima 印第安人糖尿病、威斯康星州乳腺癌和心力衰竭预测）上进行了评估，Pima 分析的曲线下面积（AUC）得分为 0.800，威斯康星州乳腺癌为 0.984，心力衰竭预测为 0.919。

In addition to discrimination, probabilistic reliability was assessed using leakage-safe cross-validated calibration analysis including Brier score, calibration intercept/slope, and post-hoc beta calibration, which improved probability calibration across datasets.

除了判别能力外，我们还使用防泄漏交叉验证校准分析评估了概率可靠性，包括 Brier 分数、校准截距/斜率以及事后 Beta 校准，这些方法改善了各数据集的概率校准效果。

These results suggest that a statistically interpretable framework can achieve performance comparable to more complex models while providing explicit, clinically meaningful decision rules and calibrated risk estimates.

这些结果表明，一个具有统计学可解释性的框架可以在实现与复杂模型相当的性能的同时，提供明确的、具有临床意义的决策规则和校准后的风险评估。

To illustrate this transparency concretely, a complete worked example demonstrates that model inference can be reproduced using only a reference table and basic arithmetic, without access to software or proprietary tools. This work offers a practical approach to supporting trustworthy and generalizable AI in real-world healthcare settings.

为了具体说明这种透明度，文中提供了一个完整的示例，证明了仅使用参考表和基本算术即可复现模型推理过程，而无需访问任何软件或专有工具。这项工作为在现实医疗环境中支持可信且可推广的人工智能提供了一种实用的方法。
