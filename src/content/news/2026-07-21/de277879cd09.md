# Your Application Is Ready... According to Whom?
# 你的应用程序准备好了吗……由谁说了算？

Over the past few years, AI has fundamentally changed how software gets built. Teams can go from an idea to a working application in a fraction of the time it used to take, and founders can create products with resources that would have been unimaginable just a few years ago. That's an incredible shift, and I think it's one of the most exciting changes our industry has seen.
在过去的几年里，人工智能从根本上改变了软件的构建方式。团队从构思到开发出可运行的应用程序，所需时间仅为过去的一小部分；创始人能够利用几年前难以想象的资源来创造产品。这是一个令人难以置信的转变，我认为这是我们行业所见过的最令人兴奋的变化之一。

What hasn't changed, though, is the question that comes after the application is built: Is it actually ready? Throughout my career, I've been involved in delivering enterprise software across many different industries and organizations. One thing I've learned is that there isn't a single definition of what makes an application "ready." If you ask six different stakeholders whether a system is ready, you'll probably get six different answers—and they're all likely to be valid.
然而，没有改变的是应用程序构建完成后随之而来的问题：它真的准备好了吗？在我的职业生涯中，我参与过许多不同行业和组织的各种企业级软件交付工作。我学到的一点是，对于什么是“准备就绪”的应用程序，并没有统一的定义。如果你问六个不同的利益相关者系统是否准备好了，你可能会得到六个不同的答案——而且它们很可能都是合理的。

That's because each person is looking at the software through the lens of the outcome they're responsible for: A founder may be wondering whether the application can handle the growth they're hoping for over the next year. A CTO is often focused on where the biggest technical risks are and what should be improved first. An engineering leader is thinking about production readiness, security, reliability, and operational support. An agency inheriting a client application wants to understand what they're taking ownership of before making commitments. An acquirer is trying to estimate the cost of technical debt. An Investor wants confidence that the technology is creating long-term value rather than future expense. Those perspectives are different because the questions they're trying to answer are different.
这是因为每个人都是通过自己所负责的成果视角来看待软件的：创始人可能在考虑应用程序能否支撑他们期望的未来一年的增长；CTO 通常关注最大的技术风险在哪里以及应该优先改进什么；工程负责人则在思考生产就绪性、安全性、可靠性和运维支持；接手客户应用程序的代理机构在做出承诺之前，想了解他们所接管的内容；收购方试图评估技术债务的成本；投资者则希望确信该技术正在创造长期价值，而不是未来的开支。这些视角之所以不同，是因为他们试图回答的问题不同。

The challenge is that we often evaluate all software the same way. Traditional assessments tend to focus on the health of the codebase. They look at architecture, security, maintainability, testing, complexity, and technical debt. Those are all important, and they should absolutely be part of any technical review. But they're only part of the picture.
挑战在于，我们往往以同样的方式评估所有软件。传统的评估倾向于关注代码库的健康状况。它们考察架构、安全性、可维护性、测试、复杂性和技术债务。这些都很重要，绝对应该成为任何技术审查的一部分。但它们只是全貌的一部分。

As AI makes software development faster and more accessible, the bottleneck is shifting. Building software is becoming easier. Understanding whether what we've built is appropriate for the business, the users, and the future of the product is becoming the harder problem to solve. An internal business application serving a few dozen employees shouldn't be evaluated the same way as a customer-facing SaaS platform expecting thousands of users. Software that's about to be acquired deserves a different review than software preparing for its first public release. Even if two applications have similar code quality, the recommendations may be completely different because the risks—and the business objectives—aren't the same.
随着人工智能使软件开发变得更快、更易于上手，瓶颈正在发生转移。构建软件变得越来越容易。理解我们所构建的东西是否适合业务、用户以及产品的未来，正成为更难解决的问题。一个服务于几十名员工的内部业务应用程序，不应该与一个预期拥有数千名用户的面向客户的 SaaS 平台采用相同的评估方式。即将被收购的软件，其审查标准应不同于准备首次公开发布的软件。即使两个应用程序的代码质量相似，建议也可能完全不同，因为它们面临的风险和业务目标并不相同。

That's why I've started thinking less about code quality and more about application readiness. To me, readiness isn't just about whether the code is clean or whether security scans pass. It's about whether the application is prepared for what the business expects it to do. Can it support future growth? Will another team be able to maintain it? Is the architecture appropriate for where the product is headed? Are there operational risks that haven't been considered? Those questions often matter just as much as the code itself.
这就是为什么我开始减少对代码质量的关注，转而更多地思考“应用程序就绪性”。对我而言，就绪性不仅仅关乎代码是否整洁或安全扫描是否通过。它关乎应用程序是否为业务期望它完成的任务做好了准备。它能支持未来的增长吗？其他团队能维护它吗？架构是否适合产品的发展方向？是否存在尚未考虑到的运营风险？这些问题往往与代码本身一样重要。

I don't think AI changes that reality. If anything, it reinforces it. AI is helping us build software faster than ever before, but faster development also means we'll be evaluating more applications, making more technology decisions, and inheriting more code than ever before. That makes it even more important to look beyond whether an application simply works and ask whether it's truly ready for the role it's expected to play.
我不认为人工智能改变了这一现实。相反，它强化了这一点。人工智能正在帮助我们比以往任何时候都更快地构建软件，但更快的开发也意味着我们将评估更多的应用程序，做出更多的技术决策，并继承比以往更多的代码。这使得我们更有必要超越“应用程序是否能运行”这一层面，去审视它是否真正为预期的角色做好了准备。
