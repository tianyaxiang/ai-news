# Position: Quantum Program Generation Must Prioritize Validity Over Probabilistic Scaling
# 观点：量子程序生成必须优先考虑有效性，而非概率性扩展

**Abstract:** The scaling hypothesis assumes that increasing model parameters yields emergent reasoning capabilities. This position paper argues that applying this probabilistic paradigm to generic quantum circuit synthesis is a directional error.
**摘要：** 扩展假说（Scaling hypothesis）认为，增加模型参数会产生涌现的推理能力。本立场论文认为，将这种概率范式应用于通用量子电路合成是一个方向性的错误。

Unlike natural languages, quantum circuits require strict adherence to mathematical constraints that manifest a significant syntax-semantics gap. Training on unverified quantum programs means that models learn syntax but fail to capture the physical semantics of the Hilbert space.
与自然语言不同，量子电路要求严格遵守数学约束，这表现出显著的语法与语义鸿沟。在未经核实的量子程序上进行训练，意味着模型虽然学会了语法，却无法捕捉希尔伯特空间的物理语义。

Since the valid subset of circuit designs decays exponentially with the number of qubits, post-hoc filtering is mathematically intractable. We propose a pivot from human-centric copilots to verifier-centric agents.
由于有效的电路设计子集会随着量子比特数量的增加呈指数级衰减，事后过滤在数学上是难以实现的。我们建议从以人为中心的副驾驶（copilots）转向以验证者为中心的智能体（verifier-centric agents）。

We integrate hierarchical constraints, topological masks, and symbolic proxies directly into generation. Our analysis suggests that scale alone cannot bridge the validity gap. Verification-aware architectures offer a viable path for modular quantum program generation.
我们将分层约束、拓扑掩码和符号代理直接集成到生成过程中。我们的分析表明，仅靠规模无法弥合有效性鸿沟。具备验证意识的架构为模块化量子程序生成提供了一条可行的路径。

These considerations point toward generation methods that encode task-specific rules of quantum information, rather than relying on imitation alone.
这些考量指向了那些能够编码量子信息特定任务规则的生成方法，而非仅仅依赖模仿。
