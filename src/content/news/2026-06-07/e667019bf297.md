# Picking an Experimentation Platform: A Retrospective
# 选择实验平台：回顾与反思

**My approach to guiding the choice between Eppo and Statsig, and the lessons learned**
**我指导团队在 Eppo 和 Statsig 之间做出选择的方法，以及从中汲取的经验教训**

Alejandro Alvarez Perez | Jun 6, 2026 | 14 min read

---

Do good pipes provide clean water? There is a moment, in every company that wants to ship products people love, when “we should experiment more” becomes “we cannot keep experimenting like this.” Hand-tuned holdouts; traffic-allocation tickets bouncing between PMs and engineers; analyst calendars booked weeks out. The wish to be data-driven sort of outgrows the machinery that was supposed to make it so.

好的管道能提供干净的水吗？对于每一家想要发布用户喜爱产品的公司来说，总会有一个时刻，“我们应该多做实验”会变成“我们不能再这样继续实验下去了”。手动调整的对照组、在产品经理和工程师之间来回推诿的流量分配工单、排期排到几周后的分析师日程表——这种想要实现数据驱动的愿望，最终超出了原本支撑它的基础设施的承载能力。

That was where we sat at ManyChat last year. We chose Eppo, but that decision is the smallest part of the story, and the part you can least transplant to your company. What I want to share instead is the process I walked through to get there, what I got wrong along the way, and what surprised me on the other side of the contract (yep, doctors hate me for this trick).

去年，ManyChat 就处于这种境地。我们最终选择了 Eppo，但这个决定只是整个故事中最微不足道的一部分，也是最难照搬到贵公司的那部分。我想分享的是我达成这一决策的过程、我在过程中犯的错误，以及在签署合同后让我感到惊讶的事情（没错，医生们讨厌我用这招）。

A note on timing. We picked Eppo at an unusually exciting moment in the industry, as the vendor map was shifting under us mid-evaluation. Eppo itself had been acquired by Datadog some months before. Statsig had recently been acquired by OpenAI, and would later be sold on to Amplitude. I do not think any of what I describe below depends on that particular news cycle, but I want to acknowledge that some of it shaped our mood while we were deciding. I break what follows into three acts: before the decision, during it (making the decision), and after.

关于时间节点的一点说明：我们选择 Eppo 时正值行业内一个异常动荡的时期，评估过程中供应商格局发生了剧变。Eppo 本身在几个月前被 Datadog 收购；Statsig 最近被 OpenAI 收购，后来又被转卖给了 Amplitude。我不认为下文描述的内容依赖于这些新闻周期，但我必须承认，其中一些事件确实影响了我们决策时的心态。我将下文分为三幕：决策前、决策中（做出决定）以及决策后。

### Before
### 第一幕：决策前

Let me get you in the mood we were in before everything happened. As I onboarded to the company, an engineer told me that if there were two simultaneous opportunities to run experiments, his team would simply postpone the second idea to a later sprint because the technical headache of configuring the two allocations. The risk of getting it wrong eventually outweighed the excitement to test. This is quite literally: anti-velocity at best; no experiment at worst. And for that one experiment that would be configured, copy-pasting boilerplate allocation logic was their bread and butter.

让我带你回到一切发生前的状态。当我入职公司时，一位工程师告诉我，如果同时有两个实验机会，他的团队会直接把第二个想法推迟到下一个冲刺周期，因为配置两个流量分配方案的技术负担太重了。最终，出错的风险超过了测试的兴奋感。这简直就是：往好了说是“反速度”，往坏了说就是“不做实验”。而对于那唯一一个会被配置的实验，复制粘贴样板式的分配逻辑成了他们的家常便饭。

An analyst on the other side of that same pipe described herself as a “human microservice”; she meant the holdout groups, defined by hand, refreshed by hand, passed on to the engineer, and so on … an exciting opportunity to experience the entire flow in first-person POV, indeed. But, irony aside, that was the moment the case for a platform stopped being abstract.

管道另一端的分析师形容自己是一个“人类微服务”；她的意思是，对照组需要手动定义、手动刷新、再传给工程师，以此类推……这确实是一个以第一人称视角体验整个流程的“绝佳”机会。但抛开讽刺意味不谈，那一刻，引入一个平台的需求不再是抽象的了。

I had seen versions of this room before. At Marktplaats, some years earlier, I had written the in-house Python libraries that try to absorb this kind of pain, and we saw time-to-insight go down from days to hours, in the tail cases. I watched the same build-or-buy debate play out again at Adevinta, globally, at a larger scale, where it landed on building rather than buying. Lucky for us at Manychat, by the end of 2025 the platform offerings had matured enough that, for a company our size and at that moment, buying was the obvious move.

我以前见过类似的情况。几年前在 Marktplaats，我曾编写过内部 Python 库来试图缓解这种痛苦，我们看到在极端情况下，洞察时间从几天缩短到了几小时。后来在 Adevinta，我在全球范围内更大规模地目睹了同样的“自研还是购买”的争论，最终结果是选择了自研。幸运的是，对于 ManyChat 来说，到 2025 年底，平台产品已经足够成熟，对于我们当时的公司规模而言，购买显然是明智之举。

We wanted the tool that would give us the best shot at getting our experimentation program where we want it: cutting-edge statistics, yes, but more importantly a tool that nudges its users toward conclusive experiments by default; product managers included. Two problems stood between us and the choice. The first was simple: we had named the pain, but it was only anecdotal so far. Leadership had a (very good) notion of what was broken, and I had heard devs and product managers grumble about the current stack when I first met them. But none of that was the same kind of object as a vendor requirements list. Until we could put the two side by side, we could not tell which capabilities were nice-to-haves and which were the point.

我们想要的是一个能让我们实验项目步入正轨的工具：不仅要有前沿的统计学功能，更重要的是，它能默认引导用户进行结论明确的实验，包括产品经理在内。在我们做出选择前，有两个问题横亘在面前。第一个很简单：我们指出了痛点，但目前还只是轶事。领导层对哪里出了问题有（非常好的）认知，我在初次见面时也听到了开发人员和产品经理对现有技术栈的抱怨。但这些都不能等同于一份供应商需求清单。除非我们将两者并列对比，否则我们无法判断哪些功能是“锦上添花”，哪些才是“核心诉求”。

The second was harder. The decision carried a lot of weight as no matter how you put it, there is always a lock-in element to any platform; culturally, if not technically. And resources are finite: we could not POC every platform on the market. Let alone the opportunity cost of having to reverse the decision and start over again. Choosing one to bet on, in one sitting, with no chance to course-correct, would have been asking to be wrong. And with the offerings being so similar in most ways, finding the best one for us was a matter of precision. We needed a way to break a single high-stakes decision into smaller, lower-stakes ones that built on each other.

第二个问题更难。这个决定分量很重，因为无论你怎么说，任何平台都存在锁定效应；即使技术上没有，文化上也会有。而且资源是有限的：我们不可能对市场上的每个平台都进行概念验证（POC）。更不用说如果决策错误需要推倒重来所带来的机会成本。在没有纠错机会的情况下，一次性押注某一个平台无异于自寻死路。由于各家产品在大多数方面都非常相似，找到最适合我们的那一个需要极高的精确度。我们需要一种方法，将一个高风险的决策拆解为一系列相互关联、风险较低的小决策。

### Interviews, and de-risking the decision
### 面试与降低决策风险

I started with interviews. PMs, product analysts, engineers, marketers. The point was to convert anecdote into something we could hold up against a vendor’s feature list. The engineer’s calendar story, the analyst’s “human microservice”, the PM who had given up on running atomic experiments and was bundling changes into bigger releases instead, postponing some of them entirely: these became the job description for the tool.

我从访谈开始。对象包括产品经理、产品分析师、工程师和市场人员。目的是将轶事转化为我们可以与供应商功能列表进行比对的指标。工程师的日程表故事、分析师的“人类微服务”、放弃原子化实验转而将变更打包发布甚至完全推迟的产品经理——这些都成了该工具的“职位描述”。

I cannot overstate how much this paid me back later. Every time the process drifted, and it drifted, the interviews were the anchor we came back to. They were also what made the whole effort credible inside the organization: telling my CPO why we were spinning up a POC was a different conversation when I could quote a specific friction back to her.

我怎么强调这在后来带来的回报都不为过。每当流程偏离轨道时（确实发生过），这些访谈就是我们回归的锚点。它们也让整个工作在组织内部变得可信：当我能向首席产品官（CPO）引用具体的痛点来解释为什么要进行 POC 时，对话的性质就完全不同了。

For the single-shot problem, we phased the discovery into three layers, each focusing on the next level of depth in the evaluation:
1. **Desk research.** Read the vendor docs, sketch a long list. Most platforms self-eliminated here, before we ever opened a sales funnel. Plenty of Claude Code at this step, too.
2. **Demos.** A focused conversation with each shortlisted vendor. A little sales pitch, sure, but mostly us probing the areas we had decided mattered most.
3. **POC.** Hands on the platforms, with real data and real evaluators, only for the two finalists.

为了解决“一锤子买卖”的问题，我们将探索过程分为三个阶段，每一阶段都专注于评估的深度：
1. **案头研究**：阅读供应商文档，列出长名单。大多数平台在这一步就自我淘汰了，甚至还没进入销售漏斗。这一步也大量使用了 Claude Code。
2. **演示（Demo）**：与入围的每家供应商进行深入交流。当然会有一些销售推销，但主要是我们针对认为最重要的领域进行质询。
3. **概念验证（POC）**：仅针对最后两名入围者，使用真实数据和评估人员进行实操。

Each layer narrowed the field and bought us information at a “price” we could afford. By the time we reached the POC we were down to two, and the decision in front of us had shrunk to something...

每一层都缩小了范围，并以我们能承受的“代价”换取了信息。当我们到达 POC 阶段时，只剩下两家，摆在我们面前的决策已经缩小到了……
