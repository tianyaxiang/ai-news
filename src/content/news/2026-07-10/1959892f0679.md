# Where Does an AI’s Personality Actually Come From?
# AI 的个性究竟从何而来？

Agentic AI: Where Does an AI’s Personality Actually Come From? They aren’t designed, you can’t help perceiving one anyway, and that makes them an engineering problem almost no one is solving.
代理式 AI：AI 的个性究竟从何而来？它们并非被刻意设计，但你却总会不由自主地感知到它们拥有个性，这使得它成为了一个几乎无人解决的工程难题。

An AI voice agent picks up a call to take a dinner reservation. A hopeful human voice: “I’d like a table for dinner at six,” the caller says. The AI agent answers without a pause: “Just to confirm, you would like to make a reservation for a dinner booking at 6 PM, correct?” The caller says yes, a table for four. “Understood, so that’s a party of four, also at 6 PM, is that right?” Yes. A few turns later, after the caller mentions it’s for a birthday: “Got it, and just to make sure, this booking is for a birthday celebration, correct?” By the end the booking is perfect, every field captured, nothing missed. The human caller hangs up irritated.
一个 AI 语音代理接听电话进行晚餐预订。一个充满期待的人声说道：“我想预订六点的晚餐。”AI 代理毫不迟疑地回答：“为了确认一下，您是想预订晚上 6 点的晚餐，对吗？”来电者说是，四人桌。“明白了，那是四位客人，也是在晚上 6 点，对吗？”是的。几轮对话后，当来电者提到这是为了庆祝生日时：“明白了，为了确保无误，这次预订是为了庆祝生日，对吗？”最终，预订非常完美，所有信息都已记录，没有任何遗漏。但来电者挂断电话时却感到很恼火。

Pull up the transcript and you will not find a single mistake. Confirm the intent, reduce ambiguity, verify before committing. This is the behavior that wins points in every test a dialogue system is normally graded on. The task succeeded. The metrics were spotless. And the person on the other end came away deeply disappointed, feeling like she’d been talking to someone who couldn’t quite trust their own ears.
查看通话记录，你找不到任何错误。确认意图、减少歧义、在提交前进行核实。这是对话系统在常规测试中得分的行为准则。任务成功了，指标完美无缺。但电话那头的人却感到极度失望，感觉自己像是在和一个完全不相信自己耳朵的人说话。

That gap, between a system that is correct and a system that is good to deal with, has little to do with capability and almost everything to do with something we rarely name: the agent’s personality. And here is the strange part. Take two language models with, for practical purposes, the same capabilities, the same benchmark scores, even the same prompt, and they can still behave like two different people. One hedges, one asserts. One asks, one decides. None of it shows up in the accuracy column, and none of it was designed. So where does it come from?
这种“系统正确”与“系统好用”之间的差距，与能力几乎无关，而与我们很少提及的东西密切相关：代理的个性。奇怪的地方在于：拿两个在实际应用中能力相同、基准测试分数相同、甚至提示词（prompt）也相同的语言模型，它们依然可能表现得像两个不同的人。一个喜欢回避，一个喜欢断言；一个喜欢询问，一个喜欢决策。这些差异在准确率指标中完全体现不出来，而且也不是被设计出来的。那么，它究竟从何而来？

### A contradiction inside every deployment
### 每个部署背后的矛盾

You probably want two things from your model at once, and they fight each other quietly. The first is consistency. A reliable tone, predictable helpfulness, stable behavior when it should refuse, a style that holds together from one conversation to the next. You want it to have a recognizable character instead of a mood.
你可能同时希望模型具备两样东西，而它们在暗中相互博弈。第一是“一致性”。可靠的语调、可预测的帮助、在需要拒绝时稳定的表现，以及在不同对话中保持统一的风格。你希望它拥有可辨识的性格，而不是反复无常的情绪。

The second is adaptability. A different register for an executive than for a student. More assertiveness in some moments, more caution in others. Willingness to explore when the stakes are low, and to commit when the user just needs a decision. Set those next to each other and the tension surfaces. The more consistent a system becomes, the more it reads as a personality you can predict. The more adaptive it becomes, the less it coheres as any single personality at all. Push hard toward consistency and you get a character who can’t read the room. Push toward adaptability and you get someone with no center.
第二是“适应性”。面对高管和学生时使用不同的语体；在某些时刻更果断，在另一些时刻更谨慎；在风险较低时愿意探索，在用户需要决策时敢于拍板。将这两者放在一起，矛盾就出现了。系统越一致，它表现出的个性就越可预测；系统越适应，它作为一个单一性格的连贯性就越差。过度追求一致性，你会得到一个“读不懂空气”的角色；过度追求适应性，你会得到一个没有主见的人。

Every team is already resolving that tension. They do it in post-training, in the reward model, in the system prompt, in a hundred small choices about how the thing should talk. They do it constantly, and almost always without admitting that resolving it is the job. So what most of us call “model personality” is something less deliberate than it sounds. It is a pile of unresolved tradeoffs wearing the costume of alignment choices. We don’t have a theory of AI personality. We have a bag of heuristics that accidentally produce one.
每个团队都在解决这种矛盾。他们通过训练后处理、奖励模型、系统提示词，以及在关于“它该如何说话”的数百个微小选择中进行调整。他们不断地这样做，却几乎从不承认解决这种矛盾本身就是他们的工作。因此，我们大多数人所说的“模型个性”，其实远没有听起来那么刻意。它只是一堆未解决的权衡，披上了“对齐选择”的外衣。我们并没有一套 AI 个性理论，我们只有一堆偶然产生个性的启发式方法。

### Think of the model as a control system
### 将模型视为一个控制系统

There’s a more precise way to see this, and it comes from engineering rather than psychology. Stop picturing the model as a speaker. Picture it as a control system operating in the space of a conversation. Every reply is a control signal, and that signal is juggling several objectives at once: helpfulness, truthfulness, safety, coherence, user satisfaction, the plain momentum of keeping the exchange moving.
有一种更精确的视角，它来自工程学而非心理学。不要把模型想象成一个说话者，把它想象成在对话空间中运行的控制系统。每一次回复都是一个控制信号，而这个信号需要同时兼顾多个目标：有用性、真实性、安全性、连贯性、用户满意度，以及维持对话进行的基本动力。

Now the definition that makes everything click. Personality is the weighting function across those objectives under uncertainty. It’s how the system decides, in the moment, which goal wins when they collide. Watch the familiar “types” fall out of that one idea, no new capability required. A helpful personality weights action over caution; it would rather move than hedge. A scientific personality weights uncertainty signaling over fluency; it would rather flag a doubt than read smoothly. A consultative personality delays the answer to widen the question. A directive one collapses ambiguity into a decision fast. None of those need a smarter model. They are four control policies running on the same brain.
现在，这个定义让一切豁然开朗：个性是在不确定性下，对这些目标进行加权的函数。它是系统在目标冲突时，瞬间决定哪个目标优先的机制。看看那些熟悉的“性格类型”是如何从这个概念中衍生出来的，无需任何新能力。一个“乐于助人”的个性将行动置于谨慎之上，宁愿行动也不愿回避；一个“科学严谨”的个性将不确定性标记置于流畅度之上，宁愿指出疑点也不愿读起来顺口；一个“咨询式”的个性会推迟回答以拓宽问题；一个“指令式”的个性则会迅速将歧义转化为决策。这些都不需要更聪明的模型，它们只是运行在同一个大脑上的四种控制策略。

Which points somewhere uncomfortable for an industry fixated on capability. We may not need more intelligent systems nearly as badly as we need better-defined objective landscapes for the intelligence we already have.
这对于一个沉迷于提升能力的行业来说，指向了一个令人不安的结论：我们可能并不那么迫切需要更智能的系统，我们更需要的是为现有的智能定义出更好的目标蓝图。

### The layer underneath the tone
### 语调之下的层面

Strip away tone, word choice, and verbosity, the things people usually mean by “voice,” and something more basic is still sitting there. How does the model relate to its own uncertainty? Call it the model’s epistemic posture. It’s where most current systems are implicitly tuned and almost never explicitly designed. Lay it out as a set of dials. For example: Assertive to hedged. Exploratory to decisive. Convergent to divergent. Stable to adaptive. Most alignment work nudges a model along these dials without ever drawing them. Picture treating this as a first-class design surface instead, something you set on purpose rather than discover after launch.
剥离掉语调、措辞和冗长——即人们通常所说的“声音”——之后，还有更本质的东西存在。模型如何处理自身的不确定性？称之为模型的“认知姿态”（epistemic posture）。这是目前大多数系统隐式调整、却几乎从不显式设计的地方。将其想象成一组旋钮：例如，从“断言”到“回避”，从“探索”到“决策”，从“收敛”到“发散”，从“稳定”到“适应”。大多数对齐工作都在这些旋钮上微调，却从未真正画出过这些旋钮。试想一下，如果将其视为一种一流的设计界面，即你是有意设置它，而不是在发布后才发现它，那会怎样？

Here’s what makes posture sneaky. Two models can hand you the same correct answer and feel completely unalike, depending only on where they sit on those dials. One gives you the fact and stops. The other wraps it in caveats. Same payload, opposite experience. People don’t respond only to correctness. They respond to posture. It’s why one system earns the word “trustworthy” and another, no less accurate, earns “exhausting.”
这就是姿态的狡猾之处。两个模型可以给你同样的正确答案，但感觉完全不同，这仅仅取决于它们在这些旋钮上的位置。一个给你事实然后停止，另一个则用各种警告包裹它。同样的信息载荷，截然不同的体验。人们不仅对正确性做出反应，他们还对姿态做出反应。这就是为什么一个系统赢得了“值得信赖”的评价，而另一个同样准确的系统却被评价为“令人心累”。
