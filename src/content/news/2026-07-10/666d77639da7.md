# NEST: Tackling Dataset-Level Distribution Shifts via Regime-Oriented Mixture-of-Experts
# NEST：通过面向机制的专家混合模型解决数据集层面的分布偏移

**Abstract:** Accurate long-term forecasting in complex systems is frequently compromised by dataset-level distribution shifts, where diverse underlying behavioral modes and evolving system states drive the dynamic multivariate time-series.
**摘要：** 在复杂系统中，精确的长期预测经常受到数据集层面分布偏移的影响，其中多样的潜在行为模式和不断演变的系统状态驱动着动态多元时间序列。

While existing methods predominantly focus on local temporal shifts, they fail to explicitly model the global structural challenge where datasets are composites of distinct operational regimes.
虽然现有方法主要关注局部时间偏移，但它们未能显式地建模数据集由不同运行机制组成的全局结构性挑战。

In this paper, we propose NEST, a specialized framework designed to model and recompose these evolving structures through a two-phase dense MoE architecture.
在本文中，我们提出了 NEST，这是一个专门设计的框架，旨在通过两阶段密集型专家混合（MoE）架构来建模和重构这些演变结构。

NEST first facilitates structural specialization by partitioning the dataset into distinct operational regimes through unsupervised clustering in a principled moment-entropy space.
NEST 首先通过在原则性的矩-熵空间中进行无监督聚类，将数据集划分为不同的运行机制，从而促进结构专业化。

We introduce a regime-oriented router mechanism that generates initial expert weights based on temporal content, subsequently refined through geometric modulation to regime centroids.
我们引入了一种面向机制的路由机制，该机制基于时间内容生成初始专家权重，随后通过几何调制对机制质心进行细化。

Crucially, rather than acting as monolithic predictors, individual experts function as specialized kernels that capture regime-specific dynamics by evolving unique variate-attention patterns.
至关重要的是，各个专家不再充当单一的预测器，而是作为专门的核函数，通过演化独特的变量注意力模式来捕捉特定机制下的动态。

Extensive evaluations on diverse benchmarks, including heterogeneous network traffic and physical phenomena, demonstrate that NEST consistently achieves state-of-the-art performance.
在包括异构网络流量和物理现象在内的多种基准测试上的广泛评估表明，NEST 始终能达到最先进的性能水平。
