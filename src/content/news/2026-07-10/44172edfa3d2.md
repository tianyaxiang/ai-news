# Inertia-1: An Open Exploration of Wearable Motion Foundation Models
# Inertia-1：可穿戴运动基础模型的开放式探索

Wearable motion sensing provides a continuous and scalable window into human behavior and health, making it a natural fit for foundation models, yet its pretraining and scaling principles remain poorly understood. 
可穿戴运动传感技术为观察人类行为和健康提供了一个持续且可扩展的窗口，使其成为基础模型的天然适配对象，然而其预训练和扩展原则目前仍未得到充分研究。

Prior work studies isolated design choices, such as sensor placement or sampling frequency, often under fixed settings and narrow downstream tasks that fail to capture real-world sensing diversity. 
以往的研究通常在固定设置和狭窄的下游任务下，对传感器放置位置或采样频率等孤立的设计选择进行研究，这无法涵盖现实世界中传感环境的多样性。

We introduce Inertia-1, a fully open exploration of wearable motion foundation models. Using massive corpora of accelerometer data from global sources spanning more than 18.2M hours, we build a controlled framework for studying the full lifecycle of wearable motion foundation models, covering data choices such as sensor modality, device placement, sampling rate, window length; model choices such as architectures and model size; and training choices such as pretraining objective and data scale. 
我们推出了 Inertia-1，这是一项关于可穿戴运动基础模型的全面开放式探索。通过利用来自全球、总时长超过 1820 万小时的海量加速度计数据语料库，我们构建了一个受控框架，用于研究可穿戴运动基础模型的全生命周期。该框架涵盖了传感器模态、设备放置位置、采样率、窗口长度等数据选择；架构和模型大小等模型选择；以及预训练目标和数据规模等训练选择。

Extensive evaluations across 15 datasets spanning human activity recognition, freezing-of-gait detection, and disease prediction reveal intriguing findings for building motion foundation models that generalize across tasks and sensing conditions. 
通过对涵盖人类活动识别、步态冻结检测和疾病预测等 15 个数据集的广泛评估，我们揭示了构建能够跨任务和跨传感条件泛化的运动基础模型的重要发现。

Collectively, Inertia-1 not only presents state-of-the-art recipes for diverse downstream tasks, but also serves as a comprehensive, practical, and open cookbook for wearable motion representation learning. 
总而言之，Inertia-1 不仅为各种下游任务提供了最先进的方案，还为可穿戴运动表征学习提供了一本全面、实用且开放的“烹饪指南”。
