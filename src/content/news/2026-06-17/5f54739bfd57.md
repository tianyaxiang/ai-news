# Drilling Into AI’s Financial Sustainability
# 深入探讨人工智能的财务可持续性

Artificial Intelligence budgets for AI tokens can’t be infinite, no matter how much hyperscalers wish they were.
无论超大规模云服务商多么希望人工智能（AI）的代币（token）预算是无限的，事实是它们不可能无限。

In my April column, I talked about how the opaqueness of the true cost of AI is a potentially fatal flaw for the profitable commercialization of the technology long term. Interestingly, in the two months since, we’ve seen some remarkable headlines from the tech industry potentially validating my argument at catastrophic scale.
在四月份的专栏中，我曾谈到 AI 真实成本的不透明性，是该技术长期商业化盈利的一个潜在致命缺陷。有趣的是，在随后的两个月里，科技行业出现了一些引人注目的头条新闻，它们在灾难性的规模上验证了我的观点。

It feels like the winds in the AI industry are changing direction so fast that it’s difficult to keep track. A matter of a few months ago, tech companies and even some other businesses were cracking the whip to get staff to use AI more, demanding that teams integrate it into workflows, regardless of whether they had any clear need or particular desire for the software.
AI 行业的风向似乎转变极快，让人难以跟上。就在几个月前，科技公司甚至其他一些企业还在挥舞鞭子，强迫员工更多地使用 AI，要求团队将其整合到工作流程中，而不论他们是否有明确的需求或使用该软件的意愿。

### Hindsight is 20–20
### 事后诸葛亮

As anyone who thought about it could probably have predicted, when you tie people’s material livelihoods to using a thing more, a large sector of people will, in fact, use the thing more. This led to “tokenmaxxing”, token usage leaderboards inside companies like Amazon, and shocking quarterly AI token expense figures at tons of places such as Uber (and other companies that have not been willing to name names).
正如任何深思熟虑过的人所能预料的那样，当你把人们的物质生计与对某项事物的使用频率挂钩时，很大一部分人确实会增加使用量。这导致了“代币最大化”（tokenmaxxing）、像亚马逊内部那样的代币使用排行榜，以及优步（Uber）等众多公司（还有其他不愿透露姓名的公司）令人震惊的季度 AI 代币支出数据。

It’s frankly unclear to me why these companies are surprised at these results, but nonetheless, this has led to a pivot in the instructions to staff both because this cost is unsustainable for any length of time, but also because the use of the AI has not produced sufficiently spectacular business outcomes.
坦率地说，我不明白为什么这些公司会对这样的结果感到惊讶。但无论如何，这导致了对员工指令的转变，原因不仅是这种成本在任何时间跨度内都不可持续，还因为 AI 的使用并没有产生足够显著的业务成果。

It’s possible that executive leadership believed that some semi-miraculous productivity explosion was going to come from AI usage, but if so, they really hadn’t done their homework. Lots of us in the field as well as people in media covering the industry sounded warnings about how AI is a tool, which can be used effectively or ineffectively, and expecting miracles will always disappoint.
高管层可能认为 AI 的使用会带来某种半奇迹般的生产力爆发，但如果是这样，他们显然没有做好功课。我们许多业内人士以及报道该行业的媒体人都曾发出警告：AI 只是一种工具，使用得当或不当效果迥异，指望奇迹总是会让人失望的。

I’ve used this kind of metaphor before, but consider if these companies were in construction, and electric drills were newly invented, making exceptional productivity improvements in building possible. The correct reaction would not be to buy as many drills as they can, to the point of making drill components scarce and driving up their price, and instructing staff to use a drill in every task, producing scoreboards displaying who was using drills for the most minutes of the day.
我以前用过类似的隐喻：试想如果这些公司是建筑公司，而电钻是刚发明出来的，能够显著提高建筑生产力。正确的反应绝不是尽可能多地购买电钻，以至于导致钻头零件短缺并推高价格，更不是指示员工在每项任务中都使用电钻，并制作排行榜来显示谁每天使用电钻的时间最长。

You’d have buildings that had swiss cheese patterns of holes in them, you’d have spent exorbitantly on the drills and the electricity to power them, and you’d have about as much to show for it as tech companies do from AI now.
那样的话，你的建筑物上会布满像瑞士奶酪一样的孔洞，你在电钻和电力上花费了巨额资金，而最终取得的成果，大概就和现在科技公司从 AI 那里得到的一样多。

### Money Isn’t Infinite
### 金钱并非无限

At any rate, reality has begun to come crashing down, and it was at least a quick return to earth. Some businesses are still buying drills, but the big players have noticed that the cost-benefit ratio here is not making sense, and are adjusting. However, as I explained in April, this is not going to be as easy as they think.
无论如何，现实已经开始崩塌，至少这是一种快速回归现实的过程。一些企业仍在购买“电钻”，但大玩家们已经注意到这里的成本效益比并不合理，并正在进行调整。然而，正如我在四月份所解释的那样，这并不像他们想象的那么容易。

Some companies are beginning to tell their teams that the use of AI needs to be for fruitful purposes, not just tokenmaxxing, to try and bring down costs while still reaping the benefits of the technology where it can generate value. What they are not yet grasping is that budgeting for tokens and clearly defining when AI is going to help with a problem is a much more indeterminate task than using other kinds of technology.
一些公司开始告诉团队，使用 AI 必须是为了实现富有成效的目标，而不仅仅是“代币最大化”，试图在降低成本的同时，在 AI 能产生价值的地方继续获取收益。他们尚未意识到的是，为代币编制预算并明确界定 AI 何时能解决问题，是一项比使用其他技术更具不确定性的任务。

Let’s go back to my April article and recollect the experience of using AI for the individual. “[Y]ou can ostensibly control how many tokens you submit, and thus control your costs, but that control is limited. You can make your prompts brief, limit extraneous instructions, and keep down your costs for input as a result. However, when agentic tools get involved, and the LLM is constructing prompts to pass to other LLMs, you’re no longer in charge of the length of the prompts. Even more significantly, you have only the most minimal control over the number of tokens that any model responds with (such as by asking it to “be concise”). For the most part, the number of output tokens is a part of that nondeterministic unknown I described before. And, you’ll note, an output token costs 5x the price of an input token.”
让我们回到我四月份的文章，回顾一下个人使用 AI 的体验：“表面上你可以控制提交的代币数量，从而控制成本，但这种控制是有限的。你可以让提示词（prompt）简短，限制无关指令，从而降低输入成本。然而，当智能体（agentic）工具介入，且大语言模型（LLM）开始构建提示词传递给其他模型时，你就无法再掌控提示词的长度了。更重要的是，对于模型回复的代币数量，你几乎没有任何控制权（即使要求它‘简洁一点’）。在大多数情况下，输出代币的数量属于我之前描述的那种非确定性的未知数。而且请注意，一个输出代币的价格是输入代币的 5 倍。”

To expand this further, any time you use AI, it has a chance of failing to successfully answer your question. So the slot-machine component piles on to the problem. The tech worker doesn’t know A. how many tokens any prompt will return or B. how many times a prompt will need to be fed in (potentially with edits) to get a successful answer to a question.
进一步扩展来看，每次使用 AI，它都有可能无法成功回答你的问题。因此，“老虎机”式的随机性加剧了这个问题。技术人员不知道：A. 任何提示词会返回多少代币；B. 需要输入多少次提示词（可能需要修改）才能得到问题的成功答案。

To calculate the cost, we need to sum all the input prompt token counts, and all the output prompt token counts (A, which is unknown) for the length of the number of attempts required (B, which is also unknown). A and B vary indeterminately based on model architecture, the problem at hand, the randomness in the model, and other factors we are probably not even aware of behind the scenes.
要计算成本，我们需要将所有输入提示词的代币计数，以及所有输出提示词的代币计数（A，未知数）相加，并乘以所需的尝试次数（B，也是未知数）。A 和 B 会根据模型架构、当前问题、模型随机性以及我们可能甚至未察觉的幕后因素而发生不确定的变化。

Then, we multiply by the price per token for whatever model or models are being used, which, as I explained in April, also varies. So, if you’re in the financial department of a tech company, and you need to determine the budget in dollars for AI tokens for the next year, I wish you all the best of luck. Even estimating based on the past usage, or with very fine detail about the company’s productivity goals, your chances of budgeting the correct amount seem pretty slim to me. However, you have to implement some kind of limit, this can’t be a blank check scenario, so you’re going to have to cut people off at some point.
然后，我们再乘以所用模型（或多个模型）的单位代币价格，正如我在四月份解释的那样，这个价格也是波动的。所以，如果你在一家科技公司的财务部门，需要确定明年 AI 代币的美元预算，我祝你好运。即使基于过去的使用情况进行估算，或者结合公司生产力目标的极细致细节，在我看来，你预算出准确金额的可能性也非常渺茫。然而，你必须实施某种限制，这不能是一个空白支票的场景，所以你迟早得在某个节点切断供应。

### 实际影响

这在实际中会如何运作？是在上半年密集使用 AI 后，下半年回归“手动编码”吗？我们在第三和第四季度所有的电子邮件和营销文档都要手写吗？我们是否会被关闭……
