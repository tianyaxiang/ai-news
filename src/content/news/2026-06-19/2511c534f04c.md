# Artemis: Anatomy-Resolved inTervention for Eliminating Multimodal NeuroImage confounderS
# Artemis：用于消除多模态神经影像混杂因素的解剖解析干预框架

**Abstract:** Multimodal neuroimaging, integrating functional connectivity from fMRI and structural connectivity from DTI, enables non-invasive analysis of brain networks using graph neural networks.
**摘要：** 多模态神经影像通过整合来自 fMRI 的功能连接和来自 DTI 的结构连接，使得利用图神经网络（GNN）对大脑网络进行非侵入性分析成为可能。

However, demographic factors such as age and sex systematically confound the relationship between brain connectivity and clinical outcomes, causing GNNs to exploit spurious shortcuts rather than learning causally invariant representations.
然而，年龄和性别等人口统计学因素系统性地混淆了大脑连接与临床结果之间的关系，导致 GNN 利用虚假的捷径，而非学习因果不变的表征。

While recent causal GNN methods introduce causality at the graph-modeling level, their causal mechanisms remain domain-agnostic without accounting for the real-world confounders inherent in clinical neuroimaging data.
尽管近期的因果 GNN 方法在图建模层面引入了因果关系，但其因果机制仍处于领域无关（domain-agnostic）状态，未能考虑临床神经影像数据中固有的现实世界混杂因素。

Moreover, brain networks are constructed from atlas-based parcellations where each region exhibits distinct sensitivity to demographic factors, necessitating region-aware adjustment.
此外，大脑网络是基于图谱分区构建的，其中每个区域对人口统计学因素表现出不同的敏感性，因此需要进行区域感知的调整。

We propose Artemis, a region-level causal framework that bridges this gap with causal intervention at each brain region independently by learning region-specific confounder representations with lightweight parameters.
我们提出了 Artemis，这是一个区域级的因果框架。它通过学习具有轻量级参数的区域特定混杂因素表征，在每个大脑区域独立进行因果干预，从而弥补了这一差距。

Our adjustment comprehensively utilized the multimodal functional and structural features for graph reasoning as a plug-in module compatible with arbitrary GNN backbones.
我们的调整方案综合利用了多模态功能和结构特征进行图推理，并作为一个可与任意 GNN 主干网络兼容的插件模块使用。

Experiments on three benchmarks, ADNI for disease diagnosis, OASIS for dementia staging, and HCP for sex classification, demonstrate consistent improvements over representative GNN-based baselines.
在三个基准测试上的实验——用于疾病诊断的 ADNI、用于痴呆分期的 OASIS 以及用于性别分类的 HCP——证明了该方法相比代表性的 GNN 基准模型具有持续的性能提升。

Multiple supporting experiments further demonstrate statistical significance and neuroscientific interpretability.
多项支持性实验进一步证明了其统计学意义和神经科学的可解释性。
