# A Link between Shock-wave Theory and Symmetry-reduced Stochastic Gradient Descent for Artificial Neural Networks
# 冲击波理论与人工神经网络对称约化随机梯度下降之间的联系

**Abstract:** We develop a mathematically explicit link between shock-wave theory and the symmetry-quotiented learning dynamics of stochastic gradient descent, drawing on differential geometry, Lie group theory, and fluid mechanics.

**摘要：** 我们利用微分几何、李群理论和流体力学，在冲击波理论与随机梯度下降的对称商学习动力学之间建立了一种数学上明确的联系。

Specifically, after quotienting parameter symmetries and applying local-entropy coarse-graining, the effective dynamics satisfy a viscous Hamilton--Jacobi equation on the quotient manifold.

具体而言，在对参数对称性进行商化并应用局部熵粗粒化后，其有效动力学满足商流形上的粘性 Hamilton--Jacobi 方程。

Moreover, under the assumption that the raw parameter dynamics can be summarized by a gradient field on the quotiented space, the gradient of the coarse-grained loss function obeys a Burgers-type equation, and shock formation can be established rigorously.

此外，在假设原始参数动力学可以由商空间上的梯度场来概括的前提下，粗粒化损失函数的梯度服从 Burgers 型方程，且可以严格证明冲击波的形成。

We apply our theory to multilayer perceptrons, convolutional neural networks, Transformers, and mean-field networks, and show that they obey the Hamilton--Jacobi or Burgers-type equations.

我们将该理论应用于多层感知机、卷积神经网络、Transformer 和平均场网络，并证明它们均服从 Hamilton--Jacobi 或 Burgers 型方程。

We conjecture that this framework also yields practical diagnostics for deep learning. In architectures such as Transformers, raw parameter norms are often distorted by symmetry redundancy and may therefore be misleading, whereas symmetry-corrected quotient observables provide a principled basis for monitoring, forecasting, and controlling training-phase transitions.

我们推测该框架也能为深度学习提供实用的诊断方法。在 Transformer 等架构中，原始参数范数往往会因对称冗余而失真，从而产生误导；而经过对称修正的商可观测量则为监测、预测和控制训练阶段的转变提供了原则性的基础。
