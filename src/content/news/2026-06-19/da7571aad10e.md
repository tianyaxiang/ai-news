**TL;DR:** Machine learning enables the development of predictive/forecasting applications for an ever-increasing number of different purposes. However, ML is also involved in data mining, i.e the identification of hidden patterns in the data itself. In this sense, the RCSB PDB database constitutes a valuable resource of biological data, containing thousands of protein 3D structures obtained by different experimental techniques. Among these, X-Ray diffraction is the method that renders a more accurate description of the position of each atom in the structure of proteins. Analyzing the dataset of X-Ray-determined protein structures (over 160,000 structures), a pattern can be found if we take into consideration both the position of amino acids and their chemical type. The biological role of this pattern is still not understood completely, but it seems well conserved across the structures of very different organisms, from animals and plants to fungi and bacteria.
**简而言之：** 机器学习使得开发用于各种目的的预测性应用成为可能。然而，机器学习也参与了数据挖掘，即识别数据本身隐藏的模式。从这个意义上说，RCSB PDB 数据库构成了宝贵的生物数据资源，其中包含了通过不同实验技术获得的数千种蛋白质 3D 结构。在这些技术中，X 射线衍射法能最准确地描述蛋白质结构中每个原子的位置。通过分析 X 射线测定的蛋白质结构数据集（超过 160,000 个结构），如果我们同时考虑氨基酸的位置及其化学类型，就能发现一种模式。这种模式的生物学作用尚不完全清楚，但它似乎在从动物、植物到真菌和细菌等截然不同的生物体结构中都得到了很好的保守。

### Starting from the beginning
### 从头说起

The journey began some years ago. At the time, I was doing research in bacterial adhesins, which are proteins on the surface of bacteria which are responsible for interacting, i.e. fixing, the bacteria to surfaces, such as our teeth, or other bacteria to form biofilms (bacterial communities). I remember back then I was still impressed with one of the subjects I had studied years earlier at college: elegant and beautiful organic chemistry. So, when I came across a classification of amino acids (the building blocks of proteins) according to their chemical type it was a natural thing to try and, somehow, relate the properties of adhesins (their stickiness to surfaces) to the chemical type of the amino acids in their structure.
这段旅程始于几年前。当时，我正在研究细菌粘附素，这是一种位于细菌表面的蛋白质，负责与表面（如我们的牙齿）相互作用（即固定），或者与其他细菌相互作用以形成生物膜（细菌群落）。我记得那时我对大学几年前学过的一门学科印象深刻：优雅而美丽的有机化学。因此，当我偶然发现一种根据化学类型对氨基酸（蛋白质的构建模块）进行分类的方法时，尝试将粘附素的特性（它们对表面的粘附性）与结构中氨基酸的化学类型联系起来，是一件很自然的事情。

Following advice from another PhD student colleague, I decided to try and come up with a method to quantify the organization of amino acids according to their chemical type in the 3D structure of adhesins. Having myself a background in biology/biotechnology I would not have quantified it directly, but rather assess it qualitatively. But this other PhD student was a physicist, and so he convinced me while having lunch that a more serious approach would imply quantification. Now, my intuition was that if amino acids of the same type are together in the 3D structure (forming clusters) that would facilitate specific chemical interaction with surfaces. Accordingly, the goal was to generate a numeric descriptor of amino acid clustering according to their chemical type (hydrophobic, polar, acidic, basic, special).
在另一位博士生同事的建议下，我决定尝试提出一种方法，来量化粘附素 3D 结构中氨基酸按其化学类型的组织方式。由于我本人有生物学/生物技术背景，我原本不会直接进行量化，而是进行定性评估。但这位博士生是一位物理学家，他在午餐时说服了我，认为更严谨的方法应该包含量化。我的直觉是，如果相同类型的氨基酸在 3D 结构中聚集在一起（形成簇），将有助于与表面进行特定的化学相互作用。因此，目标是生成一个根据化学类型（疏水性、极性、酸性、碱性、特殊）对氨基酸聚类进行描述的数值描述符。

Although this was a side project during my doctorate program, my thesis directors helped out and suggested measuring distances between amino acids to generate that descriptor. And so I did, and now I had a numerical parameter that described the degree in which the amino acids of the same type were grouped together in the 3D structure of a protein. The hypothesis was simple: the parameter/descriptor should render distinctive values for adhesins (in comparison to other proteins), and that would explain why adhesins actually adhere to surfaces. For that purpose, I had available an entire database of thousands of entries of 3D structural data of proteins, the RCSB PDB database. Also, powerful libraries like Biopython facilitated enormously the extraction and treatment of data, so the development phase, even though there was no generative AI back then, was relatively fast.
虽然这是我博士期间的一个副项目，但我的论文导师提供了帮助，并建议通过测量氨基酸之间的距离来生成该描述符。我照做了，现在我得到了一个数值参数，它描述了蛋白质 3D 结构中相同类型的氨基酸聚集在一起的程度。假设很简单：该参数/描述符应该为粘附素提供独特的数值（与其他蛋白质相比），这将解释为什么粘附素能够粘附在表面上。为此，我可以使用包含数千条蛋白质 3D 结构数据的完整数据库——RCSB PDB 数据库。此外，像 Biopython 这样强大的库极大地促进了数据的提取和处理，因此开发阶段尽管当时还没有生成式 AI，但进展相对较快。

What I found was not what I was looking for. There was no distinctive descriptor value for adhesins. Instead, the descriptor seemed only to depend on the number of amino acids following a relatively straight curve. That was boring; I knew that the descriptor would tend to increase with protein size (number of amino acids), but the hope was to find some irregularities, for example that for adhesins the value of the descriptor was notably higher than in other families of proteins. So, that was a “failure”, nothing interesting had come up.
我发现的结果并不是我想要的。粘附素并没有独特的描述符数值。相反，该描述符似乎只取决于氨基酸的数量，并遵循一条相对平直的曲线。这很无聊；我知道描述符会随着蛋白质大小（氨基酸数量）的增加而增加，但我希望发现一些不规则之处，例如粘附素的描述符数值明显高于其他蛋白质家族。所以，那是一次“失败”，没有出现任何有趣的结果。

For about three days I was reviewing the process, trying to find some bug in the code, or in the methodology. After all, I only got that simple straight curve when plotting the descriptor vs protein size (number of amino acids). OK, but why that relatively straight curve? Actually, when fitting a simple model the R2 was very high (0.979). This may mean something. And besides, why that particular slope of the curve, why not another? This may mean something — not about adhesins, but maybe about proteins in general. The fact is, the curve tells us that any two proteins, as long as they have the same number of amino acids, will have a very similar value of the descriptor. This may not seem so relevant at first, but let’s think about it. Any two proteins? That means for example one globular, almost spherical protein, and another very elongated one. Or, for example, a protein in mammals and another one in plants. Any two pair, or actually any set of proteins with equal number of amino acids would have the same value of the descriptor. There was something there.
大约三天时间里，我一直在审查这个过程，试图找出代码或方法论中的错误。毕竟，在绘制描述符与蛋白质大小（氨基酸数量）的关系图时，我只得到了那条简单的直线。好吧，但为什么是那条相对平直的曲线？事实上，在拟合一个简单模型时，R² 非常高（0.979）。这可能意味着什么。此外，为什么是那个特定的曲线斜率，而不是其他斜率？这可能意味着什么——不是关于粘附素，而是关于蛋白质本身。事实是，这条曲线告诉我们，任何两个蛋白质，只要它们具有相同数量的氨基酸，就会有非常相似的描述符数值。起初这看起来并不重要，但让我们思考一下。任何两个蛋白质？这意味着，例如一个球状、几乎是球形的蛋白质，和另一个非常细长的蛋白质。或者，例如哺乳动物中的一种蛋白质和植物中的另一种。任何一对，或者实际上任何一组具有相同数量氨基酸的蛋白质，都将具有相同的描述符数值。这里面确实有些名堂。

### The Mosaic Q model
### Mosaic Q 模型

Months and years went by. I only had one equation, and that was not enough evidence. When completing my PhD, I decided to look for help to better understand this apparent conserved property. After talking to different researchers I came across José Antonio, a professor of mathematics in the University of Seville. We were talking about characterizing the shape and size of clusters of amino acid of the same type in the 3D structure of proteins, and somehow relating that to the empirical equation.
几个月、几年过去了。我只有一个方程，这证据还不够。在完成博士学位时，我决定寻求帮助，以更好地理解这种明显的保守特性。在与不同的研究人员交谈后，我遇到了塞维利亚大学的数学教授 José Antonio。我们讨论了如何表征蛋白质 3D 结构中相同类型氨基酸簇的形状和大小，并以某种方式将其与经验方程联系起来。
