### A. Introduction
### A. 引言

Semantic search did not emerge overnight. Today’s transformer-based systems can feel almost magical, capable of capturing context and even subtle relationships between ideas. But the origin of today’s semantic search systems is actually gradual.
语义搜索并非一蹴而就。如今基于 Transformer 的系统几乎让人感觉拥有“魔法”，能够捕捉语境甚至观点之间微妙的联系。但现代语义搜索系统的起源实际上是一个渐进的过程。

Before embeddings, transformers, and large language models, researchers used keyword matching, TF–IDF vectors, and traditional machine learning methods to analyze text. Many of those earlier ideas never truly disappeared. In fact, modern systems still build on concepts developed decades ago.
在嵌入、Transformer 和大语言模型出现之前，研究人员使用关键词匹配、TF-IDF 向量和传统机器学习方法来分析文本。许多早期的思想从未真正消失。事实上，现代系统依然建立在几十年前开发的概念之上。

The field evolved layer by layer, with each generation solving some problems while exposing new ones. Understanding that evolution is important. In machine learning, as in science generally, knowing where we came from often helps us understand where we are heading.
该领域层层演进，每一代技术在解决某些问题的同时，也暴露了新的挑战。理解这种演变至关重要。在机器学习乃至整个科学领域，了解我们从何而来，往往有助于我们理解将向何处去。

The history of semantic search is also the story of an important shift in AI itself: from transparent, human-designed systems to increasingly intelligent models whose internal reasoning is much more difficult to interpret. In that way, we move from explicit retrieval rules and manually engineered features to systems capable of learning abstract representations of meaning directly from data.
语义搜索的历史也是人工智能自身发生重大转变的故事：从透明的、由人类设计的系统，转向越来越智能、但其内部推理过程难以解释的模型。通过这种方式，我们从显式的检索规则和人工设计的特征，转向了能够直接从数据中学习意义的抽象表示的系统。

In this article, we will explore that progression through a concrete example: comparing a student’s art critique with critiques written by experts about the same painting. Instead of jumping immediately into embeddings and transformers, we will build a sequence of increasingly sophisticated retrieval systems, examining both their strengths and their limitations.
在本文中，我们将通过一个具体的例子来探索这一演进过程：比较学生对某幅画作的艺术评论与专家撰写的评论。我们不会直接跳入嵌入和 Transformer，而是构建一系列日益复杂的检索系统，并审视它们的优势与局限性。

We will cover four major stages in the evolution of semantic search:
我们将涵盖语义搜索演进的四个主要阶段：

*   **Method 1 — Handcrafted Retrieval Features + TF–IDF**: A transparent ranking system combining TF–IDF cosine similarity with interpretable features such as keyword overlap, critique length normalization, and recency weighting.
*   **方法 1 — 手工检索特征 + TF-IDF**：一个透明的排序系统，结合了 TF-IDF 余弦相似度与可解释特征，如关键词重叠度、评论长度归一化和时效性加权。

*   **Method 2 — Classical Machine Learning for Semantic Ranking**: Using TF–IDF feature vectors together with supervised learning models such as Logistic Regression to learn ranking behavior from labeled examples.
*   **方法 2 — 用于语义排序的经典机器学习**：使用 TF-IDF 特征向量结合逻辑回归等监督学习模型，从标注样本中学习排序行为。

*   **Method 3 — Embedding-Based Semantic Search**: Replacing sparse lexical representations with dense semantic embeddings generated by Sentence Transformers.
*   **方法 3 — 基于嵌入的语义搜索**：用 Sentence Transformers 生成的稠密语义嵌入取代稀疏的词汇表示。

*   **Method 4 — Transformer Fine-Tuning**: Fine-tuning pretrained transformer architectures such as BERT to directly model semantic relationships between critiques.
*   **方法 4 — Transformer 微调**：微调 BERT 等预训练 Transformer 架构，以直接建模评论之间的语义关系。

### B. Data
### B. 数据

To keep the focus on semantic search rather than dataset engineering, we will use a small synthetic dataset of art critiques. The dataset was intentionally designed to mimic realistic differences in vocabulary, writing style, interpretation, and analytical depth among critics discussing the same painting.
为了将重点放在语义搜索而非数据集工程上，我们将使用一个小型的艺术评论合成数据集。该数据集旨在模拟不同评论家在讨论同一幅画作时，在词汇、写作风格、解读方式和分析深度上的真实差异。

Each critique contains both metadata and free-form text. Our task throughout the article will be to compare a new student’s critique with expert critiques of the same painting and to determine semantic similarity using progressively more advanced retrieval methods.
每篇评论都包含元数据和自由文本。本文的任务是比较学生的新评论与专家对同一画作的评论，并使用日益先进的检索方法来确定语义相似度。

The structure of each critique is represented using a simple Python dataclass:
每篇评论的结构都使用一个简单的 Python 数据类（dataclass）来表示：

```python
@dataclass
class Critique:
    critique_id: str
    painting_id: str
    critic_name: str
    title: str
    text: str
    published_at: datetime
```

The text field above contains the main critique content used for semantic analysis, while fields such as painting_id, critic_name, and published_at provide metadata that can support filtering, grouping, or ranking experiments.
上面的 `text` 字段包含了用于语义分析的主要评论内容，而 `painting_id`、`critic_name` 和 `published_at` 等字段提供了可支持过滤、分组或排序实验的元数据。

一篇典型的评论可能如下所示：

### C. Methods
### C. 方法

#### C.1 Method 1-Rule-Based Retrieval and TF–IDF Ranking
#### C.1 方法 1 — 基于规则的检索与 TF-IDF 排序

We begin with one of the most classical and interpretable approaches to semantic search: combining TF–IDF ranking with a small set of handcrafted retrieval features. Although simple compared to modern deep learning systems, this approach captures many of the core ideas behind document retrieval and similarity scoring.
我们从语义搜索中最经典且最具可解释性的方法开始：将 TF-IDF 排序与一组手工设计的检索特征相结合。尽管与现代深度学习系统相比很简单，但这种方法捕捉到了文档检索和相似度评分背后的许多核心思想。

At this stage, the system does not truly “understand” language. Instead, it identifies patterns in word usage and combines them with manually designed scoring heuristics.
在这一阶段，系统并不真正“理解”语言。相反，它识别词汇使用的模式，并将它们与人工设计的评分启发式规则相结合。

The foundation of the method is TF–IDF (Term Frequency–Inverse Document Frequency), a classic technique for converting text into numerical vectors. TF–IDF increases the importance of words that appear frequently within a document but remain relatively uncommon across the larger collection. Common words such as “the” or “painting” receive very little weight, while more distinctive terms such as “composition,” “contrast,” or “symbolism” become more influential.
该方法的基础是 TF-IDF（词频-逆文档频率），这是一种将文本转换为数值向量的经典技术。TF-IDF 增加了在文档中频繁出现但在整个集合中相对少见的词汇的重要性。“the”或“painting”等常用词权重很低，而“composition”（构图）、“contrast”（对比）或“symbolism”（象征主义）等更具辨识度的词汇则更具影响力。
