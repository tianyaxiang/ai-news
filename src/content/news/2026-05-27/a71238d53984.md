# Nano World Models: A Minimalist Implementation of Future Video Prediction
# Nano World Models：未来视频预测的极简实现

World models have become a central paradigm for learning predictive simulators that support generation, planning, and decision-making. Yet, despite rapid progress in industry-scale interactive video generation, the broader research community still lacks compact, reproducible, and easily extensible implementations for studying the design choices underlying modern world models.

世界模型已成为学习预测性模拟器的核心范式，为生成、规划和决策提供支持。然而，尽管工业级交互式视频生成领域进展迅速，但广大研究社区仍缺乏紧凑、可复现且易于扩展的实现方案，以深入研究现代世界模型背后的设计选择。

We introduce Nano World Models, a minimalist codebase for future video prediction centered around diffusion forcing. Nano World Models provides a unified interface for generative objectives, model scales, action-conditioning mechanisms, latent observation spaces, datasets, evaluation protocols, and long-horizon rollout procedures.

我们推出了 Nano World Models，这是一个以“扩散强制”（diffusion forcing）为核心的未来视频预测极简代码库。Nano World Models 为生成目标、模型规模、动作调节机制、潜在观测空间、数据集、评估协议以及长程推演流程提供了一个统一的接口。

This design enables controlled studies of world-modeling components that are often entangled across separate implementations. Through experiments across simple control environments, game simulation, and real-robot data, we examine how prediction parameterization, architecture scale, action injection, sampling budget, and domain complexity affect video prediction quality and autoregressive rollout behavior.

这种设计使得对世界建模组件进行受控研究成为可能，而这些组件在不同的实现中往往是相互纠缠的。通过在简单控制环境、游戏模拟和真实机器人数据上的实验，我们研究了预测参数化、架构规模、动作注入、采样预算和领域复杂度如何影响视频预测质量及自回归推演行为。

By releasing code, configurations, evaluation scripts, and pretrained checkpoints, Nano World Models aims to provide a compact yet extensible experimental substrate for open, reproducible, and scientific world-model research.

通过发布代码、配置、评估脚本和预训练检查点，Nano World Models 旨在为开放、可复现且科学的世界模型研究提供一个紧凑而可扩展的实验基底。
