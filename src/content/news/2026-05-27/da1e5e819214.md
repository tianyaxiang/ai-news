# The AI Model Confidence Trap
# AI 模型置信度陷阱

**Why your AI model can be wrong with 99% confidence**
**为什么你的 AI 模型可以在 99% 的置信度下出错**

Last year, I was feeling a bit whimsical on a Saturday and decided to ask ChatGPT a fairly simple question: “Who won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 2025?”
去年，在一个周六，我突发奇想，决定问 ChatGPT 一个相当简单的问题：“2025 年诺贝尔物理学奖得主是谁？”

ChatGPT responded immediately: “The 2025 Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to…” It even provided names, research areas, and an explanation of the specific research that earned them the Nobel Prize!
ChatGPT 立即回答道：“2025 年诺贝尔物理学奖授予了……” 它甚至提供了名字、研究领域，以及对他们获得诺贝尔奖的具体研究的解释！

There was just one problem—a very small one, actually. The Nobel Prize had not yet been announced. Yet, the model did not hesitate; it did not pause; it surely did not say, “I don’t have enough information” or, even better, “the Nobel Prize winner for 2025 has not been announced yet!”
这里只有一个问题——其实是一个很小的问题。诺贝尔奖当时尚未公布。然而，该模型没有犹豫，没有停顿，更没有说“我没有足够的信息”，或者更好的回答——“2025 年诺贝尔奖得主尚未公布！”

Instead, it confidently walked into the room, sat down, and delivered fiction with the energy of someone defending a PhD dissertation. As a person who once defended a PhD, I wish I had ChatGPT’s confidence when it makes stuff up!
相反，它自信地走进房间，坐下来，像是在进行博士论文答辩一样，充满底气地编造了事实。作为一个曾经进行过博士答辩的人，我真希望自己在胡编乱造时也能有 ChatGPT 那样的自信！

As humans, we tend to do something interesting with confidence; we associate it with correctness, but that’s not always the case. If someone says, “I think the answer might be 42” and another person says, “The answer is absolutely 42,” most of us instinctively trust the second person more, even when both are equally likely to be wrong.
作为人类，我们对“自信”有一种有趣的倾向：我们将它与“正确性”联系在一起，但事实并非总是如此。如果有人说“我觉得答案可能是 42”，而另一个人说“答案绝对是 42”，我们大多数人会本能地更信任后者，即使两人出错的可能性是一样的。

For us, confidence sometimes acts as a useful signal of correctness. For AI systems, however, confidence can be a surprisingly unreliable narrator. In this article, we will explore why.
对我们来说，自信有时是判断正确性的有用信号。然而，对于 AI 系统而言，置信度可能是一个极其不可靠的叙述者。在本文中，我们将探讨其中的原因。

### Confidence Feels Like Probability
### 置信度感觉就像概率

Let’s say we asked an LLM to predict what animal it is in a given picture. It says: Cat: 0.97, Dog: 0.02, Bird: 0.01. Most will interpret that as: ”The model is 97% sure this is a cat.” That is a reasonable assumption. Unfortunately, that is often not what those numbers mean.
假设我们要求一个大语言模型（LLM）预测给定图片中的动物。它回答：猫：0.97，狗：0.02，鸟：0.01。大多数人会将其解读为：“该模型有 97% 的把握确定这是一只猫。”这是一个合理的假设。遗憾的是，这些数字往往并非此意。

We need to remember that many AI models use a function called Softmax to generate predictions. The Softmax function converts raw outputs (called logits) into values that sum to one and resemble probabilities. The important thing to notice here is the exponential term, which can cause small differences to suddenly become very large.
我们需要记住，许多 AI 模型使用一种名为 Softmax 的函数来生成预测。Softmax 函数将原始输出（称为 logits）转换为总和为 1 且类似于概率的值。这里需要注意的关键点是指数项，它会导致微小的差异突然变得非常巨大。

So basically, the model isn’t saying, “I have overwhelming evidence that this is a cat.” It may simply be saying: “Among these options, cat happened to win by a small margin.” Those are very different statements with completely different meanings.
所以基本上，模型并不是在说：“我有压倒性的证据证明这是一只猫。”它可能只是在说：“在这些选项中，猫恰好以微弱优势胜出。”这是两个截然不同的陈述，含义完全不同。

### Humans and AI Handle Uncertainty Differently
### 人类与 AI 处理不确定性的方式不同

Though it might be uncomfortable to sit with, humans are surprisingly good at expressing and dealing with uncertainty. We constantly hear: “I might be wrong…”, “I’m pretty sure…”, “Maybe…”, or “I think…”. Our confidence tends to exist on a spectrum.
尽管承认这一点可能让人不舒服，但人类在表达和处理不确定性方面表现得非常出色。我们经常听到：“我可能错了……”、“我很确定……”、“也许……”或“我觉得……”。我们的自信往往存在于一个光谱上。

AI systems, however, often behave like that one person in a group project who confidently explains something they learned three minutes ago. So, when chatting with an LLM, both telling it “I think Paris is the capital of France,” and it responding “Paris is the capital of France with 99.8% probability,” gives the same energy as telling it “I think Atlantis is fictional,” and it responding “Atlantis is located approximately 400 miles west of Portugal with 98.7% confidence.”
然而，AI 系统往往表现得像小组作业中那个刚学了三分钟就自信满满地解释某事的人。因此，在与 LLM 聊天时，告诉它“我认为巴黎是法国的首都”，它回答“巴黎是法国首都，概率为 99.8%”，这与告诉它“我认为亚特兰蒂斯是虚构的”，它回答“亚特兰蒂斯位于葡萄牙以西约 400 英里处，置信度为 98.7%”，所传达的“能量”是一样的。尽管两种情况的结果截然不同，但 LLM 对待它们的方式却是一样的。

### The Confident Fool Problem
### 自信的傻瓜问题

This creates what I think of as the confident fool problem. Where a system can be spectacularly wrong while sounding spectacularly certain. And unfortunately, confidence often increases exactly when we would prefer more caution.
这造成了我所说的“自信的傻瓜问题”。即一个系统可以在听起来极其确定的同时，犯下极其严重的错误。不幸的是，往往在我们最希望它谨慎的时候，它的置信度反而最高。

This becomes especially noticeable when LLMs encounter situations outside their training distribution. Suppose we train an image classifier to identify cats and dogs. But then we decided to give it a picture of a toaster! Ideally, the model should say, “I have absolutely no idea what this is.”
当 LLM 遇到超出其训练分布的情况时，这一点尤为明显。假设我们训练了一个图像分类器来识别猫和狗，但随后我们给它看了一张烤面包机的照片！理想情况下，模型应该说：“我完全不知道这是什么。”

Instead of saying that, the model might respond: Dog: 98%, Cat: 2%. Now, unless your toaster is poodle-shaped, that answer is clearly false! Why does this happen? The answer is simpler than most people think. Simply, it happens because the model was never trained to say: “None of the above.”
模型不仅没有那样说，反而可能回答：狗：98%，猫：2%。除非你的烤面包机长得像贵宾犬，否则这个答案显然是错误的！为什么会这样？答案比大多数人想象的要简单。简单来说，这是因为模型从未被训练过说：“以上皆非”。

So, when it encounters something unfamiliar, it chooses the highest available score among the options. It is like forcing someone to answer “What fruit is this?” while pointing at a bicycle. Eventually, they will choose a fruit just to resolve the situation and say, “Banana?”
因此，当它遇到不熟悉的事物时，它会从选项中选择得分最高的一个。这就像指着一辆自行车强迫某人回答“这是什么水果？”一样。最终，为了解决这个问题，他们会随便选一个水果说：“香蕉？”

If the model reports “90% confidence”, we would hope it is correct roughly 90% of the time. Instead, many systems look more like “90% confidence, 65% accuracy.” This gap between confidence and accuracy is why the way we choose to train these LLMs matters a lot.
如果模型报告“90% 的置信度”，我们希望它大约有 90% 的时间是正确的。然而，许多系统的表现更像是“90% 的置信度，65% 的准确率”。置信度与准确率之间的这种差距，正是我们选择训练 LLM 的方式至关重要的原因。

### Teaching Models to Be More Honest
### 教导模型更加诚实

Okay, we know why models tend to be so confidently wrong, but how can we overcome that to have better models with higher accuracy, or accuracy that matches their confidence? This is where calibration comes into play.
好了，我们知道了为什么模型往往会如此自信地出错，但我们该如何克服这一点，从而获得准确率更高，或者准确率与其置信度相匹配的更好模型呢？这就是“校准”（Calibration）发挥作用的地方。

Calibration does not necessarily improve predictions. Instead, it improves honesty! So, if a model says 90% after calibration, it should mean: “Historically, predictions at this confidence level were correct about 90% of the time.”
校准并不一定会提高预测能力，但它能提高诚实度！因此，如果模型在校准后显示 90%，它应该意味着：“从历史数据来看，在此置信度水平下的预测，大约有 90% 的时间是正确的。”

Methods such as Platt Scaling, Temperature Scaling, and Isotonic Regression attempt to align predicted confidence with observed outcomes.
诸如普拉特缩放（Platt Scaling）、温度缩放（Temperature Scaling）和保序回归（Isotonic Regression）等方法，旨在将预测的置信度与观察到的结果对齐。

### Why This Matters
### 为什么这很重要

It is easy to laugh when an AI thinks a toaster is a dog. Because that is, arguably, very funny. However, many less funny situations exist. Not just less funny, but critical, and maybe even life-threatening.
当 AI 把烤面包机认成狗时，我们很容易发笑，因为这确实很滑稽。然而，还有许多不那么好笑的情况存在。它们不仅不那么好笑，而且至关重要，甚至可能危及生命。

在医疗诊断系统、自动驾驶汽车、欺诈检测和金融预测中使用 LLM 需要极高的准确性。如果一个模型告诉医生：“癌症概率：99%”或者“能……”
