# GIRAF: Towards Generalizable Human Interactions with Articulated Objects
# GIRAF：迈向具有通用性的人体与铰接物体交互

**Abstract:** Synthesizing realistic full-body human interactions with articulated objects is a fundamental challenge for embodied AI and graphics, with applications in robotics training and virtual agents. Existing models remain limited: some focus on simple activities with static objects, while others restrict attention to hand-only manipulation. This leaves open the problem of generating coordinated full-body motion that approaches, manipulates, and moves articulated objects in a realistic and generalizable way.

**摘要：** 合成逼真的人体全身与铰接物体（articulated objects）交互是具身智能和图形学领域的一项基础挑战，在机器人训练和虚拟智能体方面有着广泛应用。现有的模型仍存在局限性：一些模型仅关注静态物体的简单活动，而另一些则将注意力限制在手部操作上。这使得如何生成协调的全身动作——即以逼真且具有通用性的方式接近、操纵和移动铰接物体——这一问题仍未得到解决。

The key difficulty lies in reasoning jointly about locomotion, fine-grained contact, and object articulation. Models must capture subtle hand-object correspondences that transfer across object geometries, while also producing seamless transitions from navigation to manipulation. At the same time, the scarcity of large-scale paired motion-scene data makes it difficult to generalize across diverse object positions and shapes.

其核心难点在于如何联合推理运动（locomotion）、细粒度接触以及物体铰接。模型必须捕捉能够跨物体几何形状迁移的微妙手-物对应关系，同时还要实现从导航到操作的无缝过渡。与此同时，大规模配对运动-场景数据的匮乏，使得模型难以在不同的物体位置和形状之间实现泛化。

We introduce a text-conditioned diffusion model that addresses these challenges through three core ideas: an object-centric representation that unifies hand-object contact with object surfaces, a mixed-domain training strategy that balances locomotion and interaction, and a contact-based augmentation scheme that expands training diversity. Through experiments, our method demonstrated strong generalization to unseen object configurations, surpassing current state-of-the-art methods.

我们引入了一种文本条件扩散模型，通过三个核心理念来应对这些挑战：一种将手-物接触与物体表面统一起来的以物体为中心的表示方法；一种平衡运动与交互的混合域训练策略；以及一种扩展训练多样性的基于接触的增强方案。实验表明，我们的方法在未见过的物体配置上表现出了强大的泛化能力，超越了当前最先进的方法。
