# Enterprise Document Intelligence: A Series on Building RAG Brick by Brick, from Minimal to Corpus scale
# 企业文档智能：从零开始构建 RAG 系列，从极简到语料库规模

For AI engineers who want to understand every step, not just call the library.
专为那些不仅想调用库，还想理解每一个步骤的 AI 工程师而写。

Angela Shi | May 22, 2026 | 25 min read
Angela Shi | 2026年5月22日 | 25分钟阅读

About three years ago, generative AI took off and RAG showed up as the standard answer for “we have documents, we want to ask questions.” The pitch sounded miraculous. The implementation everyone described was the same one, over and over: chunk the documents, push the chunks into a vector store, embed the question, retrieve top-k by cosine similarity, optionally rerank, send the hits to an LLM.
大约三年前，生成式 AI 开始腾飞，RAG 成为“我们有文档，想进行提问”这一需求下的标准答案。这个方案听起来近乎奇迹。每个人描述的实现方式都如出一辙：对文档进行分块，将分块推入向量数据库，对问题进行向量化，通过余弦相似度检索前 K 个结果，可选地进行重排序，最后将检索到的内容发送给大语言模型（LLM）。

Vendors converged on it. Consulting decks converged on it. Conference talks converged on it.
供应商们采用了它，咨询方案采用了它，会议演讲也都在谈论它。

Then the deployments started shipping, and the results were often disappointing. Users didn’t trust the answers. Citations were vague or missing. Retrieved passages were beside the point as often as they were useful. And the team’s reflex, every time, was to pull more tools from the same toolbox: a stronger model, a longer context window, a better reranker, more MLOps for the production side. The framing was always the same: “this is an IT problem. Better infrastructure, better tools, better models will fix it.”
然而，当部署开始上线时，结果往往令人失望。用户不信任答案，引用模糊或缺失，检索到的段落往往文不对题。团队的惯性反应总是从同一个工具箱里掏出更多工具：更强的模型、更长的上下文窗口、更好的重排序器、更多的生产环境 MLOps。逻辑始终如一：“这是一个 IT 问题。更好的基础设施、更好的工具、更好的模型就能解决它。”

I started looking at it myself, on real enterprise documents, with real domain experts in the room. My experience didn’t match that framing. The work that actually made a real difference wasn’t infrastructural. It was engineering, plus understanding the business domain, plus a little of the underlying math. Not deep math. Just enough to see what an embedding actually measures, what a reranker actually does, why a particular trick helps in some cases and hurts in others.
我开始亲自研究，使用真实的商业文档，并邀请真正的领域专家参与。我的经验与那种逻辑并不相符。真正产生实质性影响的工作并非基础设施层面的，而是工程实践，加上对业务领域的理解，以及一点点底层数学知识。不需要深奥的数学，只需足以理解嵌入（Embedding）到底衡量了什么、重排序器到底做了什么，以及为什么某些技巧在某些情况下有效，而在另一些情况下反而有害。

And then, the piece most teams skip: knowing the documents the system is supposed to answer questions on. Who reads them. What they contain. What vocabulary the experts use. What questions come up week after week.
还有一点是大多数团队所忽略的：了解系统需要回答问题的文档。谁在阅读这些文档？它们包含什么？专家使用什么词汇？每周会出现哪些重复的问题？

Most companies aren’t Google. They’re not research labs either. They’re not running open-domain QA over the open web. They’re not training their own embedding models. They have a few core document types, a few dozen domain experts who already know the corpus inside out, and a recurring set of questions that need answers with citations and an audit trail.
大多数公司不是谷歌，也不是研究实验室。他们不需要在开放网络上运行开放域问答，也不需要训练自己的嵌入模型。他们只有几种核心文档类型、几十位对语料库了如指掌的领域专家，以及一系列需要带引用和审计追踪的重复性问题。

The right architecture for that context is not what vendor decks pitch and not what research papers chase. It’s an architecture that amplifies the experts and uses cheap, predictable retrieval where it can.
针对这种场景，正确的架构不是供应商方案中推销的那样，也不是研究论文所追求的那样。它应该是一个能够放大专家能力，并在可能的情况下使用廉价、可预测检索的架构。

Most of the RAG systems I’ve seen in enterprise production are worse than a hundred-line Python script. The basics are broken, and stacking more on top doesn’t help. Embeddings are too fuzzy in meaning to pick the right passage, and parsing is sloppy enough that the LLM gets garbage in, garbage out.
我所见过的企业生产环境中的大多数 RAG 系统，甚至不如一个百行 Python 脚本。基础部分已经损坏，再往上堆砌功能也无济于事。嵌入在语义上过于模糊，无法选出正确的段落；而解析过程又过于粗糙，导致 LLM 接收到的是垃圾信息，输出的自然也是垃圾。

When a system like that starts to break, the standard reflex is to add layers: a re-ranker, a fine-tuned embedding model nobody can tell is helping, a query-rewriter agent, a grader agent, an orchestrator framework that turns every question into ten LLM calls. Each layer adds plausibility to the demo. None of them fixes the foundation: there is still no way to tell whether the retrieved passages are the right ones, and still no way to explain to a user why a particular page came back.
当这样的系统开始崩溃时，标准的反应是增加层级：重排序器、没人能确定是否有用的微调嵌入模型、查询重写代理、评分代理、将每个问题转化为十次 LLM 调用的编排框架。每一层都让演示看起来更合理，但没有一层能修复基础问题：依然无法判断检索到的段落是否正确，也无法向用户解释为什么返回了特定的页面。

The script we’ll build in the first article fits in about a hundred lines and has no vector database, no framework, and no agents. It takes a PDF and a question, parses, retrieves the top three pages by simple cosine similarity, sends them to an LLM with a Pydantic schema, and returns a structured answer with line citations and a highlighted source PDF.
我们在第一篇文章中构建的脚本大约只有一百行，没有向量数据库，没有框架，也没有代理。它接收一个 PDF 和一个问题，进行解析，通过简单的余弦相似度检索前三页，使用 Pydantic 模式将其发送给 LLM，并返回一个带有行引用和高亮源 PDF 的结构化答案。

That script is more verifiable and more useful than many of the production systems I’ve seen up close. The gap between the two isn’t prompt engineering, and it isn’t a better retrieval algorithm. It comes from three habits the industry skips: knowing the documents, knowing what the experts already know, and not confusing RAG with machine learning.
这个脚本比我近距离观察过的许多生产系统更具可验证性，也更有用。两者之间的差距不在于提示词工程，也不在于更好的检索算法。它源于行业所忽略的三个习惯：了解文档、了解专家已知的内容，以及不要将 RAG 与机器学习混为一谈。

This series wires those habits into a four-brick pipeline: document parsing, question parsing, retrieval, generation, with an optional PDF annotation step that hands the citation back to the reader.
本系列将这些习惯整合为一个四块基石组成的流水线：文档解析、问题解析、检索、生成，以及一个可选的 PDF 注释步骤，将引用反馈给读者。

### 1. How RAG is used in enterprise
### 1. RAG 在企业中是如何使用的

#### 1.1 The 2020 paper: retrieval as context
#### 1.1 2020年的论文：作为上下文的检索

In May 2020, Patrick Lewis and colleagues at Facebook AI Research coined the term in Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Knowledge-Intensive NLP Tasks (Lewis et al. 2020, arXiv preprint). Their abstract names the three failings the architecture was meant to fix, quoted directly:
2020年5月，Facebook AI Research 的 Patrick Lewis 及其同事在《Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Knowledge-Intensive NLP Tasks》（Lewis 等人，2020年，arXiv 预印本）中提出了这一术语。他们的摘要指出了该架构旨在修复的三个缺陷，直接引用如下：

Pre-trained models “cannot easily expand or revise their memory, can’t straightforwardly provide insight into their predictions, and may produce ‘hallucinations’.”
预训练模型“无法轻易扩展或修改其记忆，无法直接提供对其预测的洞察，并且可能会产生‘幻觉’。”

The fix combined a generator (BART) with a dense vector index over Wikipedia, accessed at inference time. The architectural move that mattered: pull a passage from a corpus, hand it to the LLM, let it generate from that context rather than from training-time memory alone.
该修复方案将生成器（BART）与维基百科的密集向量索引相结合，并在推理时进行访问。关键的架构举措是：从语料库中提取一段内容，交给 LLM，让它基于该上下文进行生成，而不是仅仅依赖训练时的记忆。

Those three failings map cleanly onto the three properties enterprise RAG fights for: corpus freshness, citations, grounded answers. The series is a direct continuation of that 2020 line of thinking, applied to enterprise constraints.
这三个缺陷与企业 RAG 所追求的三个特性完美对应：语料库的新鲜度、引用来源、有据可查的答案。本系列是 2020 年这一思路的直接延续，并将其应用于企业的约束条件中。

#### 1.2 What “RAG” means in this series
#### 1.2 本系列中“RAG”的含义

For most developers today, “RAG” has narrowed to mean one specific recipe: a vector store, embedding-similarity retrieval, and an LLM at the end. The series will keep using the word, but in its broader original sense: information extraction, information search, and question answering over a corpus of documents. The retrieval mechanism is a design choice the architecture admits, not part of the definition. Many of the enterprise pipelines this series defends do not use a vector store at all; some use it as one channel among several.
对于今天的大多数开发者来说，“RAG”已经狭义地指代一种特定的配方：向量数据库、嵌入相似度检索，以及末端的 LLM。本系列将继续使用这个词，但取其更广泛的原始含义：针对文档语料库的信息提取、信息搜索和问答。检索机制是架构允许的一种设计选择，而不是定义的一部分。本系列所支持的许多企业流水线根本不使用向量数据库；有些则将其作为多种渠道中的一种。
