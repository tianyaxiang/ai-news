# The Hidden Bottleneck in Quantum Machine Learning: Getting Data into a Quantum Computer
# 量子机器学习中隐藏的瓶颈：如何将数据输入量子计算机

Exploring one of the most overlooked bottlenecks in QML: getting data into a quantum computer efficiently.
探索量子机器学习（QML）中最容易被忽视的瓶颈之一：如何高效地将数据输入量子计算机。

***

Modern Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) rely heavily on processing large volumes of data and learning patterns from them. In general, a model’s ability to generalise improves as the amount of available data increases. However, when we move from classical machine learning to Quantum Machine Learning (QML), one of the first major challenges we encounter is that quantum computers cannot directly read classical bits.
现代人工智能（AI）和机器学习（ML）高度依赖于处理海量数据并从中学习模式。通常情况下，随着可用数据量的增加，模型的泛化能力也会随之提升。然而，当我们从经典机器学习转向量子机器学习（QML）时，遇到的首要重大挑战之一就是量子计算机无法直接读取经典比特。

Before any computation can happen, the data must first be embedded into quantum states (qubits). This may sound simple at first, but in practice it is surprisingly difficult. As the size and complexity of the data increase, the cost of preparing these quantum states can grow exponentially. In fact, no universally efficient method for loading arbitrary classical data into quantum systems is currently known.
在进行任何计算之前，数据必须首先被嵌入到量子态（量子比特）中。这听起来很简单，但在实践中却异常困难。随着数据规模和复杂性的增加，准备这些量子态的成本可能会呈指数级增长。事实上，目前还没有一种通用的高效方法可以将任意经典数据加载到量子系统中。

***

### How Classical Neural Networks Read Data
### 经典神经网络如何读取数据

Neural Networks (NNs) are one of the foundational building blocks of modern Machine Learning. Much of their success comes from our growing ability to collect, store, and process massive amounts of data. At their core, neural networks are mathematical systems designed to learn patterns from data. During training, they gradually adjust their internal parameters to capture the relationships that generated the data in the first place. This allows them to perform tasks such as prediction, generation, and classification.
神经网络（NN）是现代机器学习的基础构件之一。它们之所以取得巨大成功，很大程度上归功于我们收集、存储和处理海量数据能力的不断提升。从本质上讲，神经网络是旨在从数据中学习模式的数学系统。在训练过程中，它们会逐渐调整内部参数，以捕捉生成数据的内在关系。这使得它们能够执行预测、生成和分类等任务。

One of the biggest strengths of classical neural networks is their flexibility. They can process many different types of data and learn the relationships that exist within them:
经典神经网络最大的优势之一是其灵活性。它们可以处理多种不同类型的数据，并学习其中存在的各种关系：

*   **Sequential data** → language, financial time series, audio signals
*   **Spatial data** → images, videos, geographical maps
*   **Probabilistic or noisy data** → sensor measurements, radioactive decay, experimental observations
*   **序列数据** → 语言、金融时间序列、音频信号
*   **空间数据** → 图像、视频、地理地图
*   **概率或噪声数据** → 传感器测量值、放射性衰变、实验观测数据

Despite being able to handle many different types of data, neural networks do not directly “see” images, audio, or text the way humans do. Under the hood, everything is ultimately converted into numerical vectors or tensors before being processed by the network.
尽管能够处理多种不同类型的数据，但神经网络并不能像人类那样直接“看到”图像、音频或文本。在底层，所有数据在被网络处理之前，最终都会被转换为数值向量或张量。

### Quantum Computers Can’t Read Bits
### 量子计算机无法读取比特

Quantum computers are a fundamentally different way of processing information. Instead of operating on classical bits, they use quantum bits, or qubits, which follow the principles of quantum mechanics such as superposition and entanglement. A classical bit is a binary value which is either 0 or 1. A qubit, however, can exist in a superposition of both states simultaneously.
量子计算机是一种从根本上不同的信息处理方式。它们不操作经典比特，而是使用遵循量子力学原理（如叠加和纠缠）的量子比特（qubits）。经典比特是 0 或 1 的二进制值，而量子比特可以同时处于两种状态的叠加态。

Since we live in a classical world, most of our data naturally exists as bits stored in classical memory. A quantum processor cannot directly read an image, a sentence, or an audio waveform the way a neural network running on a GPU can. Before any quantum computation can happen, this classical information must be encoded into qubits — a task that turns out to be far more difficult than it sounds.
由于我们生活在经典世界中，我们的大多数数据自然是以存储在经典内存中的比特形式存在的。量子处理器无法像运行在 GPU 上的神经网络那样直接读取图像、句子或音频波形。在进行任何量子计算之前，这些经典信息必须被编码到量子比特中——事实证明，这项任务比听起来要困难得多。

***

### Embedding Classical Data into Quantum States
### 将经典数据嵌入量子态

Classical information must somehow be translated into quantum states. This process is known as quantum data embedding or quantum state preparation. Possible ways to do this are amplitudes, phases, or rotations of qubits. Over the years, researchers have proposed multiple approaches for embedding classical data into quantum systems. Two of the most commonly used techniques are:
经典信息必须以某种方式转换为量子态。这个过程被称为量子数据嵌入或量子态准备。实现这一目标的方法包括利用量子比特的振幅、相位或旋转。多年来，研究人员提出了多种将经典数据嵌入量子系统的方法。其中两种最常用的技术是：

1.  **Angle-based encoding** (基于角度的编码)
2.  **Amplitude encoding** (振幅编码)

Each approach comes with its own advantages, limitations, and computational costs.
每种方法都有其自身的优势、局限性和计算成本。

#### Angle-based encoding
#### 基于角度的编码

One of the simplest and most widely used approaches for quantum data embedding is angle encoding (also called rotation-based embedding). In this method, classical features are encoded as rotation angles applied to qubits using quantum gates such as R-X, R-Y and R-Z which rotate a qubit along the X, Y, and Z axes respectively.
量子数据嵌入中最简单且最广泛使用的方法之一是角度编码（也称为基于旋转的嵌入）。在这种方法中，经典特征被编码为施加在量子比特上的旋转角度，使用如 R-X、R-Y 和 R-Z 等量子门，分别沿 X、Y 和 Z 轴旋转量子比特。
