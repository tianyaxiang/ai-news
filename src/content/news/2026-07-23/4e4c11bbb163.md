# Recti-Q: Feature-Space Rectification for Out-of-Distribution-Robust Quantized Perception in Edge Robotics
# Recti-Q：边缘机器人中面向分布外鲁棒量化感知的特征空间校正技术

**Abstract:** Robotic perception pipelines increasingly rely on large vision backbones deployed on SWaP-constrained edge platforms, making post-training quantization (PTQ) attractive for real-time inference. However, while PTQ often preserves clean in-distribution accuracy, we show that it can substantially degrade reliability under deployment-relevant distribution shifts (e.g., sensor noise, severe weather, and novel operating environments), creating a Quantization-Induced Robustness Gap.

**摘要：** 机器人感知流水线日益依赖部署在受限边缘平台（SWaP-constrained）上的大型视觉骨干网络，这使得训练后量化（PTQ）成为实现实时推理的理想选择。然而，尽管 PTQ 通常能保持良好的分布内（in-distribution）准确率，但我们研究发现，在部署中常见的分布偏移（如传感器噪声、恶劣天气和新颖的操作环境）下，它会显著降低系统的可靠性，从而产生“量化诱导的鲁棒性差距”（Quantization-Induced Robustness Gap）。

Across foundational vision benchmarks (ImageNet-C and PACS), 4-bit PTQ models exhibit pronounced robustness degradation despite negligible ID accuracy loss. To address this, we propose Recti-Q, a lightweight feature-space rectification framework that freezes the quantized backbone and trains a small classifier-head LoRA adapter using only source data.

在基础视觉基准测试（ImageNet-C 和 PACS）中，4-bit PTQ 模型在分布内准确率损失微乎其微的情况下，表现出了明显的鲁棒性下降。为了解决这一问题，我们提出了 Recti-Q，这是一个轻量级的特征空间校正框架。该框架冻结了量化后的骨干网络，并仅使用源数据训练一个小型分类器头部 LoRA 适配器。

Recti-Q is architecture-agnostic across CNNs and Transformers, supports efficient teacher-free training, and recovers a significant portion of the lost robustness, in some cases matching or exceeding FP32 performance.

Recti-Q 具有架构无关性，适用于 CNN 和 Transformer，支持高效的无教师（teacher-free）训练，并能恢复大部分丢失的鲁棒性，在某些情况下甚至能达到或超过 FP32 的性能水平。

At less than 1% parameter overhead (as small as 6 KB), Recti-Q preserves over 99% of PTQ memory savings, adds negligible compute, and enables low-bandwidth Over-The-Air (OTA) resilience patching for deployed robotic fleets operating in unpredictable physical environments.

Recti-Q 的参数开销不到 1%（最小仅为 6 KB），保留了超过 99% 的 PTQ 内存节省优势，几乎不增加计算负担，并为在不可预测物理环境中运行的机器人集群提供了低带宽的无线（OTA）鲁棒性补丁更新能力。
