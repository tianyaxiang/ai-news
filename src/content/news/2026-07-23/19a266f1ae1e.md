# AniGS: Bridging Rendering and Diffusion Prior for 3D Scene Animation
# AniGS：连接渲染与扩散先验以实现 3D 场景动画

**Abstract:** Novel view rendering of large and complex reconstructed scenes is becoming increasingly photorealistic. However, most reconstructions remain static and lack the ambient motion that makes environments immersive. We present AniGS, a method for scene-level animation of 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) reconstructions that adds subtle, distributed dynamics, e.g., vegetation motion, while preserving rigid structures.

**摘要：** 大型复杂重建场景的新视角渲染正变得越来越逼真。然而，大多数重建结果仍然是静态的，缺乏使环境具有沉浸感的环境动态。我们提出了 AniGS，这是一种用于 3D 高斯泼溅 (3DGS) 重建的场景级动画方法，它在保持刚性结构的同时，增加了微妙的、分布式的动态效果，例如植被的运动。

Unlike existing 3D animation techniques which are limited to object-centric subjects or small regions, AniGS is designed for large, cluttered, navigable scenes. AniGS represents the scene with a canonical 3DGS and models motion using a time-conditioned deformation field. To animate the entire scene, we leverage a pretrained video diffusion model and introduce an iterative dataset-model update strategy that progressively expands viewpoint coverage and repeatedly updates camera-fixed training videos using a render-and-refine scheme.

与现有的局限于以物体为中心或小范围区域的 3D 动画技术不同，AniGS 专为大型、杂乱且可导航的场景而设计。AniGS 使用规范的 3DGS 来表示场景，并利用时间条件变形场来建模运动。为了实现整个场景的动画化，我们利用预训练的视频扩散模型，并引入了一种迭代式的数据集-模型更新策略，通过“渲染与精炼”（render-and-refine）方案，逐步扩大视点覆盖范围并反复更新相机固定的训练视频。

To prevent artifacts from unintended motion in static areas, we further introduce a composed video-to-video refinement scheme that restricts motion to desired regions. Experiments on five real-world, large-scale outdoor scenes demonstrate that AniGS produces natural ambient dynamics and high-quality novel view videos, enabling more immersive viewing experiences of reconstructed environments.

为了防止静态区域中非预期运动产生的伪影，我们进一步引入了一种组合式的视频到视频（video-to-video）精炼方案，将运动限制在所需的区域内。在五个真实世界的大规模户外场景上的实验表明，AniGS 能够产生自然的场景动态和高质量的新视角视频，从而为重建环境提供了更具沉浸感的观看体验。
