# Here’s what Samsung’s smart glasses actually look like
# 三星智能眼镜真机外观首度曝光

Samsung has given us our first chance to check out its upcoming smart glasses in person, revealing two new designs and the first specs in the process, including an impressive 9-hour battery life. The glasses, developed in collaboration with Google and the eyewear brands Gentle Monster and Warby Parker, are due to launch this fall.

三星近日首次让我们近距离接触了其即将推出的智能眼镜，并展示了两种新设计及初步规格，其中包括令人印象深刻的 9 小时续航能力。这款眼镜由三星与谷歌，以及眼镜品牌 Gentle Monster 和 Warby Parker 合作开发，预计将于今年秋季发布。

Google and Samsung have been teasing the new glasses for some time, and revealed the first two designs at I/O in May. Now there are two more frames: Both are unsurprisingly boxy, with a new set of squared off black glasses from Gentle Monster, and a more rounded, red-brown pair from Warby Parker. Jaein Choi, the executive vice president running Samsung’s mobile experience division, told CNBC that there are “more designs” on the way.

谷歌和三星此前已对这款新眼镜进行了多次预热，并于 5 月的 I/O 大会上首次展示了两种设计。现在又增加了两款镜框：不出所料，它们都采用了方正的造型，包括 Gentle Monster 的一款黑色方框眼镜，以及 Warby Parker 的一款更圆润的红棕色眼镜。负责三星移动体验部门的执行副总裁 Jaein Choi 向 CNBC 表示，未来还会有“更多设计”推出。

This was our first chance to see any of the four frames in person, though Samsung wouldn’t let me try them on — or even hold them up to my face. They’re all impressively light — not much heavier than the thick-rimmed acrylic glasses I used to wear, and barely bigger than them either. Speaker and microphone grilles are hidden along the stems, a single button sits on the right with a power switch tucked inside on the left, and of course there’s a camera and recording LED mounted on the front. Much like Meta’s collaborations with Ray-Ban and Oakley, these basically look like normal glasses most people would happily wear — privacy connotations aside.

这是我们第一次亲眼见到这四款镜框中的任何一款，尽管三星不允许我试戴，甚至不允许我将其举到脸前。它们都轻得惊人，并不比我过去戴的厚框亚克力眼镜重多少，体积也几乎没有大出多少。扬声器和麦克风格栅隐藏在镜腿上，右侧设有一个按钮，左侧内侧则藏着电源开关，当然，正面还安装了摄像头和录制指示灯。就像 Meta 与雷朋（Ray-Ban）和欧克利（Oakley）的合作款一样，抛开隐私方面的顾虑，它们看起来基本上就是大多数人乐意佩戴的普通眼镜。

The new glasses will be powered by Qualcomm’s Snapdragon AR1 Gen 1 chip, the same silicon that powers Meta’s latest smart glasses. Samsung says they’ll run for up to nine hours on a single charge, while the accompanying charging case delivers up to seven additional charges.

这款新眼镜将搭载高通骁龙 AR1 Gen 1 芯片，这与 Meta 最新款智能眼镜所使用的芯片相同。三星表示，单次充电可续航长达 9 小时，随附的充电盒可额外提供 7 次充电。

You’ll be able to use either Google Gemini or Samsung’s Bixby with the glasses, though the AI features themselves sound pretty familiar: message summaries, live translation, navigation guidance, and analysis or description of what’s captured by the cameras.

用户可以在眼镜上使用 Google Gemini 或三星的 Bixby，不过这些 AI 功能本身听起来相当熟悉：包括消息摘要、实时翻译、导航引导，以及对摄像头捕捉到的内容进行分析或描述。

Speaking of, just like Meta’s, there is a recording LED for when the camera is capturing video. Choi told Bloomberg that, like Meta’s, the glasses will prevent recording if they detect the LED has been blocked or obscured. That hasn’t been enough to prevent Meta’s from being branded “pervert glasses,” so we’ll have to see if Samsung and Google have other plans for tackling what Choi called an “an industry-shared problem.”

说到这里，和 Meta 的产品一样，当摄像头录制视频时，会有一个录制指示灯亮起。Choi 向彭博社表示，与 Meta 的产品类似，如果眼镜检测到指示灯被遮挡或覆盖，将无法进行录制。但这并没能阻止 Meta 的眼镜被贴上“变态眼镜”的标签，因此我们还得看看三星和谷歌是否有其他计划来解决 Choi 所称的“行业共同难题”。

Samsung still isn’t revealing a firm release date or price for the upcoming glasses, but Choi described them as a “premium” product.

三星目前尚未透露这款即将推出的眼镜的具体发布日期或价格，但 Choi 将其描述为一款“高端”产品。
