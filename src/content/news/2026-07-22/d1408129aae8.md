# LLM Unlearning for Cyber Defense: A Survey on Methods, Challenges, and Emerging Threats
# 用于网络防御的大语言模型“遗忘”技术：方法、挑战与新兴威胁综述

**Abstract:** LLMs are increasingly deployed in security-critical systems across healthcare, finance, education, and decision support, yet their inability to forget creates serious cybersecurity, privacy, and safety risks. 
**摘要：** 大语言模型（LLM）正日益被部署在医疗、金融、教育和决策支持等安全关键型系统中，然而它们无法“遗忘”信息的特性，带来了严峻的网络安全、隐私和安全风险。

Sensitive personal information, copyrighted material, hazardous domain knowledge, and memorized training data remain encoded across billions of parameters long after deployment, leaving models vulnerable to extraction, jailbreak attacks, membership inference, and regulatory non-compliance. 
敏感的个人信息、受版权保护的材料、危险的领域知识以及被记忆的训练数据，在模型部署后仍长期编码在数十亿个参数中，这使得模型极易受到信息提取、越狱攻击、成员推理攻击以及合规性问题的威胁。

Real-world incidents, from chatbots regenerating private information to fabricated legal citations producing direct legal and financial cost, place the problem at the center of the emerging-threats landscape rather than the realm of speculation. 
从聊天机器人泄露隐私信息，到伪造的法律引用导致直接的法律和经济损失，这些现实世界的案例表明，该问题已处于新兴威胁领域的核心，而非仅仅停留在理论推测阶段。

Because retraining billion-parameter models on revised corpora is computationally infeasible, and because knowledge within an LLM is distributed and entangled across parameters rather than localized to identifiable units, LLM unlearning has emerged as the principal cyber defense response, aiming to remove or suppress targeted knowledge from a trained model without retraining and without eroding what the model should still know. 
由于在修订后的语料库上重新训练数十亿参数的模型在计算上是不可行的，且大语言模型内部的知识是分布并纠缠在各个参数中，而非局限于可识别的单元，因此“大语言模型遗忘”（LLM Unlearning）已成为主要的网络防御手段。其目标是在无需重新训练的情况下，从已训练的模型中移除或抑制目标知识，同时不损害模型应保留的其他知识。

A central question, however, remains unresolved. Do current methods genuinely remove knowledge, or do they only stop the model from expressing it under ordinary prompting conditions? 
然而，一个核心问题仍未解决：当前的方法是真正移除了知识，还是仅仅阻止了模型在常规提示条件下表达这些知识？

This survey examines LLM unlearning through the lens of security, robustness, and verifiable forgetting, with primary focus on gradient-based methods, which have come to dominate the field due to their compatibility with existing training pipelines and their scalability to billion-parameter models.
本综述从安全性、鲁棒性和可验证遗忘的角度审视了大语言模型遗忘技术，重点关注基于梯度的方法。由于这些方法与现有的训练流程兼容，且能够扩展至数十亿参数的模型，它们已成为该领域的主流。
