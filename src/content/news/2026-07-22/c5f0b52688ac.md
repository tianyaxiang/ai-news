# RouteCost: A Production-Inspired Multi-Stage Framework for Pre-Order Shipping Cost Estimation in E-Commerce
# RouteCost：一种受生产实践启发、用于电子商务预购运费估算的多阶段框架

**Abstract:** Accurate pre-order shipping cost estimation is important in e-commerce because it affects price presentation, margin planning, and conversion. In practice, shipping cost is shaped not only by distance but also by destination demand mix, billable weight, dimensional pricing, surcharge triggers, and latent operational effects such as shipment consolidation. 

**摘要：** 在电子商务中，准确的预购运费估算至关重要，因为它直接影响价格展示、利润规划和转化率。在实际操作中，运费不仅受距离影响，还受到目的地需求组合、计费重量、体积定价、附加费触发条件以及诸如货物拼箱等潜在运营效应的影响。

Static lookup methods therefore miss important sources of variation, while monolithic regressors may exploit strong but non-causal correlations. We propose RouteCost, a production-inspired multi-stage framework that decomposes the problem into time-aware demand forecasting, fee-card-informed baseline pricing, Stage 2 residual correction, and proxy-based box-consolidation inference. 

因此，静态查询方法往往会忽略重要的变异来源，而单一的回归模型可能会利用那些强相关但非因果的特征。我们提出了 RouteCost，这是一个受生产实践启发的多阶段框架，它将该问题分解为：时间感知需求预测、基于费率卡的基准定价、第二阶段残差校正以及基于代理的装箱合并推理。

Route-level cost estimates are aggregated through a route-weighted expectation formulation to produce product-level shipping cost predictions. Across over 250,000 orders, 260 products, and 18 months of order history, the framework improves predictive quality and aggregate calibration while preserving route-level interpretability.

通过路由加权期望公式，将路由级别的成本估算进行汇总，从而得出产品级别的运费预测。在涵盖超过 25 万份订单、260 种产品以及 18 个月订单历史的数据集上，该框架在保持路由级可解释性的同时，显著提升了预测质量和总体校准效果。
