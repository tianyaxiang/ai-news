# DocOCR-Eval: A Correction-Based Framework for OCR Tool Selection Without Ground Truth

**DocOCR-Eval：一种无需真值标签的 OCR 工具选择校正评估框架**

Document parsing is a foundational step for document understanding tasks such as visual question answering and key information extraction, as it transforms unstructured scanned images into structured representations by extracting textual, visual, and layout information.

文档解析是视觉问答和关键信息提取等文档理解任务的基础步骤，它通过提取文本、视觉和布局信息，将非结构化的扫描图像转换为结构化表示。

While numerous Optical Character Recognition (OCR) engines and multimodal large language models (MLLMs) have been developed for this purpose, selecting an appropriate document parsing solution for a given document collection remains challenging, particularly in label-scarce settings.

尽管目前已经开发了许多用于此目的的光学字符识别（OCR）引擎和多模态大语言模型（MLLM），但针对特定文档集选择合适的文档解析方案仍然具有挑战性，尤其是在标签稀缺的情况下。

In this work, we conduct a systematic evaluation of text recognition performance across a diverse set of OCR engines and state-of-the-art MLLMs on multiple scanned document benchmarks spanning different domains and languages.

在这项工作中，我们对多种 OCR 引擎和最先进的 MLLM 在跨越不同领域和语言的多个扫描文档基准测试上的文本识别性能进行了系统评估。

Motivated by the limited contextual reasoning capabilities of many OCR engines and the high cost of manual annotations, we propose DocOCR-Eval, an annotation-free evaluation framework for automatic OCR assessment and selection.

受限于许多 OCR 引擎有限的上下文推理能力以及人工标注的高昂成本，我们提出了 DocOCR-Eval，这是一个用于自动 OCR 评估和选择的无需标注的评估框架。

DocOCR-Eval employs a three-staged correction and ranking strategy to approximate annotation-based tool ordering without ground-truth labels.

DocOCR-Eval 采用三阶段校正和排序策略，在没有真值标签的情况下近似实现基于标注的工具排序。

We show that aggregating across multiple MLLMs progressively improves alignment with annotation-based rankings.

我们证明，通过聚合多个 MLLM 的结果，可以逐步提高与基于标注的排序结果的一致性。

Extensive experiments further demonstrate that reliable OCR tool selection can be achieved in realistic, label-limited settings, providing practical guidance for deploying document parsing systems across diverse real-world document collections.

广泛的实验进一步证明，在现实的标签受限环境下，可以实现可靠的 OCR 工具选择，从而为在各种真实世界文档集中部署文档解析系统提供了实践指导。
