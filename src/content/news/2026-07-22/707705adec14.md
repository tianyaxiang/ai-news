# Prompt Engineering Isn’t Enough: How Four Bricks of Context Engineering Stop RAG Hallucinations
# 提示工程还不够：如何通过“上下文工程”的四大基石阻止 RAG 幻觉

Enterprise Document Intelligence [Vol.1 #9bis] – Your RAG isn’t hallucinating, it’s answering the wrong context faithfully. On real NIST and World Bank documents, watch each of the four bricks break, and the contract that closes it.
企业文档智能 [第1卷 #9bis] —— 你的 RAG 系统并没有产生幻觉，它只是忠实地回答了错误的上下文。通过真实的 NIST 和世界银行文档，观察四个基石是如何失效的，以及如何通过契约来修复它们。

In the previous two articles (Part A and Part B) we built the upgraded pipeline and watched it work: pointed at a research paper, a NIST standard, and a report with a broken table of contents, it returned a typed, cited answer each time. So why does it work? And would a naive RAG, the kind almost everyone builds first, embed the pages, keep the closest few, ask, have done the same?
在之前的两篇文章（A 部分和 B 部分）中，我们构建了升级后的流水线并观察了其运行效果：针对研究论文、NIST 标准以及带有损坏目录的报告，它每次都能返回带有引用来源的结构化答案。那么，为什么它能奏效？如果是一个原始的 RAG（大多数人最先构建的那种，即嵌入页面、保留最相似的几页并进行提问），它能做到同样的效果吗？

The short answer is no, and the useful part is where the naive one breaks: not in one place, but at each of the four bricks in turn, document parsing, question parsing, retrieval, and generation, each time for a reason you can name and fix. It is also why the usual reflexes — rewrite the prompt, shrink the chunks, swap the embedding model — do not help. The wrong answer does not come from the prompt. It comes from a brick upstream handing the model the wrong context, which the model then answers faithfully.
简短的回答是：不能。而有价值的部分在于原始 RAG 在哪里失效：它不是在一个地方失效，而是在文档解析、问题解析、检索和生成这四个基石上依次失效，且每次失效的原因都是可以明确并修复的。这也是为什么通常的应对措施——重写提示词、缩小分块、更换嵌入模型——往往无济于事。错误的答案并非源于提示词，而是源于上游的某个基石向模型传递了错误的上下文，而模型只是忠实地回答了这些错误信息。

Getting all four bricks right, not just the prompt, is what this series calls context engineering. Every failure below is a real run, never an assumed one; the companion notebook reproduces each one.
不仅要优化提示词，还要确保所有四个基石都正确，这就是本系列所称的“上下文工程”。以下展示的每一个失败案例都是真实运行的结果，而非假设；配套的 Notebook 可以复现每一个案例。

### 1. The naive baseline, brick by brick
### 1. 原始基准：逐个基石分析

The baseline is the 100-line pipeline from Article 1 (minimal RAG): parse the PDF, turn the question into keywords, keep the top few pages by match, ask the model to answer. On a short, clean, prose paper it works, and Article 9’s own tests confirmed it: on *Attention Is All You Need* and the RAG paper, naive and upgraded both answer correctly.
基准系统是我们在第一篇文章（最小化 RAG）中构建的 100 行代码流水线：解析 PDF，将问题转化为关键词，根据匹配度保留最相关的几页，然后让模型回答。在简短、整洁的散文类论文中，它运行良好，文章 9 的测试也证实了这一点：在《Attention Is All You Need》和 RAG 论文上，原始系统和升级系统都能给出正确答案。

The upgraded pipeline is the same four bricks, each with a tighter contract: parsing returns a relational `line_df` instead of flat text, question parsing expands the query into the document’s vocabulary, retrieval routes on the table of contents, generation returns a typed answer. A naive RAG runs the loose version of every brick. The gap opens exactly where each loose brick hands the model the wrong thing.
升级后的流水线同样包含这四个基石，但每个基石都有更严格的契约：解析阶段返回关系型 `line_df` 而非纯文本，问题解析阶段将查询扩展为文档的词汇表，检索阶段基于目录进行路由，生成阶段返回结构化答案。原始 RAG 运行的是每个基石的“松散版本”。差距恰恰出现在每个松散的基石向模型传递错误信息的地方。

### 2. Parsing: a table flattened into noise
### 2. 解析：被扁平化为噪音的表格

**Document:** World Bank Commodity Markets Outlook (a report built around price tables).
**Question:** What is the 2025 annual average price forecast for U.S. natural gas (Henry Hub)?
**文档：** 世界银行《大宗商品市场展望》（一份围绕价格表构建的报告）。
**问题：** 美国天然气（Henry Hub）2025 年的年度平均价格预测是多少？

A naive pipeline parses a PDF the common way: dump flat text with `get_text()` and cut it into fixed-size chunks. That is fine for prose and fatal for tables. A price table is a grid; flat extraction linearises it, and a fixed-size chunker then cuts the stream wherever the character budget runs out. The row label "Henry Hub" lands in one chunk and its "3.5" cell in another. Retrieval hands the model chunks that never carry both, and it answers honestly: “not stated in these lines,” confidence 0.00.
原始流水线通常以常见方式解析 PDF：使用 `get_text()` 导出纯文本，并将其切分为固定大小的块。这对散文有效，但对表格却是致命的。价格表是一个网格；扁平化提取将其线性化，而固定大小的分块器会在字符数耗尽时切断数据流。行标签“Henry Hub”落入一个块中，而其对应的“3.5”单元格落入另一个块中。检索系统交给模型的块中从未同时包含这两者，因此模型诚实地回答：“这些行中未说明”，置信度为 0.00。

The fix is relational parsing. Instead of flat text, the upgraded brick returns a `line_df`: one row per text line, each with its bounding box. The table’s row stays intact, the model reads "Henry Hub" and "3.5" on the same line, and returns “$3.5 per mmbtu” at confidence 0.99.
解决方案是关系型解析。升级后的基石不再返回纯文本，而是返回 `line_df`：每一行文本对应一行数据，并带有边界框信息。表格的行保持完整，模型可以在同一行读取到“Henry Hub”和“3.5”，并以 0.99 的置信度返回“$3.5 per mmbtu”。

### 3. Question: a word the document never uses
### 3. 问题：文档中从未使用的词汇

**Document:** NIST SP 800-207, Zero Trust Architecture.
**Question:** What are the pillars of zero trust architecture?
**文档：** NIST SP 800-207，零信任架构。
**问题：** 零信任架构的支柱（pillars）是什么？

The document never uses the word "pillars." It calls them "tenets." A naive pipeline searches the pages for the query’s own words, "pillars" scores against nothing, and the model reports “the specific pillars are not listed,” confidence 0.20. The answer is right there, under a different name; the right pages were never even searched.
该文档从未使用“pillars”（支柱）一词，而是称其为“tenets”（原则/信条）。原始流水线在页面中搜索查询词，由于“pillars”匹配不到任何内容，模型报告称“未列出具体的支柱”，置信度为 0.20。答案其实就在那里，只是换了个名字；正确的页面甚至从未被检索到。

The fix is question parsing. Before retrieval runs, the brick normalizes and expands the query, mapping domain synonyms (pillars → tenets, principles) so retrieval searches for the words the document actually uses.
解决方案是问题解析。在检索运行之前，该基石会对查询进行标准化和扩展，映射领域同义词（pillars → tenets, principles），从而使检索能够搜索到文档实际使用的词汇。
