# How I Mastered Data Structures and Algorithms for ML (In 6 Weeks)
# 我是如何在 6 周内掌握机器学习数据结构与算法的

Data Science: How I Mastered Data Structures and Algorithms for ML (In 6 Weeks). The strategies, questions, and process I used to ace coding interviews.
数据科学：我是如何在 6 周内掌握机器学习数据结构与算法的。这是我用来在编程面试中脱颖而出的策略、题目和流程。

Since 2024, I’ve passed over 90% of coding interviews, and it’s not because I am some sort of genius. The majority of coding interviews in the data science and machine learning space are either on LeetCode or HackerRank, typically asking a data structures and algorithms question or something closely related. And what I have discovered over the last couple of years is that you can “gamify” your prep for these interviews through certain strategies and by practising specific questions. So, in this article, I want to break down exactly the process I went through to master coding/data structures and algorithm interviews in just 6 weeks. Let’s get into it!
自 2024 年以来，我的编程面试通过率超过了 90%，这并不是因为我是什么天才。数据科学和机器学习领域的编程面试大多在 LeetCode 或 HackerRank 上进行，通常会考察数据结构与算法问题或与之密切相关的内容。过去几年我发现，通过特定的策略和练习特定的题目，你可以将面试准备“游戏化”。因此，在本文中，我想详细拆解我在短短 6 周内掌握编程/数据结构与算法面试的过程。让我们开始吧！

### Stop Learning Data Structures & Algorithms
### 停止（传统方式）学习数据结构与算法

The first step may seem counterintuitive, but it’s to actually stop trying to learn data structures and algorithms the traditional way. For those of you unfamiliar with data structures and algorithms, or DSA for short, let me give you a quick definition of these terms:
第一步可能看起来有些反直觉，但实际上就是停止尝试以传统方式学习数据结构与算法。对于那些不熟悉数据结构与算法（简称 DSA）的人，我先简单定义一下这些术语：

*   **Data structures** — Organising and storing data so it can be accessed and modified efficiently.
*   **Algorithms** — Step-by-step procedures or sets of rules for solving a problem or performing a task.
*   **数据结构** — 组织和存储数据，以便能够高效地访问和修改。
*   **算法** — 解决问题或执行任务的分步过程或规则集。

Together, DSA is the study of how to structure data and design efficient methods to process it. DSA has become a bit of a “meme” in the tech space, because these interviews test skills that you will rarely use in the actual job. However, it’s part of many interview processes, so it’s a necessary evil we need to practise.
总的来说，DSA 是关于如何构建数据并设计高效方法来处理数据的学科。DSA 在科技圈已经成了一个“梗”，因为这些面试考察的技能在实际工作中很少用到。然而，它是许多面试流程的一部分，所以我们必须练习，这是一种“必要的恶”。

If you did a degree in computer science, you would have likely studied DSA as a module, but many people looking to break into data science and machine learning come from other STEM backgrounds like physics, maths and engineering. So naturally, these people start watching tutorials, reading textbooks, and are highly likely to use NeetCode as their primary learning resource. I did the exact same.
如果你拥有计算机科学学位，你可能已经修过 DSA 这门课，但许多想要进入数据科学和机器学习领域的人来自物理、数学和工程等其他 STEM 背景。因此，这些人自然会开始观看教程、阅读教科书，并且极有可能将 NeetCode 作为主要学习资源。我当初也是这么做的。

The problem is, this actually didn’t make me good at data structures and algorithms or at solving coding interviews. I still struggled to solve basic problems like 2sum. Don’t get me wrong, Neetcode is a great resource for learning concepts in DSA and wider areas like system design. But, its not the resource itself, rather the general approach to learning. Sure, learning the theory is helpful, but you should learn it whilst practising at the same time to solidify your understanding.
问题在于，这并没有让我擅长数据结构与算法，也没有让我擅长解决编程面试题。我仍然难以解决像“两数之和”（2sum）这样的基础问题。别误会，Neetcode 是学习 DSA 概念及系统设计等更广泛领域知识的绝佳资源。但问题不在于资源本身，而在于学习的通用方法。当然，学习理论是有帮助的，但你应该在练习的同时学习，以巩固你的理解。

It’s like wanting to play tennis and learning the theory of how to swing the racquet and learning the technique, but you never actually get to practise hitting the ball — which is fundamentally the whole game. The same goes for LeetCode. So, what I did is take the complete reverse approach. I started doing the problems before even learning the topics. And then, after I have completed the problem or simply couldn’t solve it, learn the efficient solution and the accompanying theory with that topic.
这就像你想打网球，学习了如何挥拍的理论和技巧，但却从不练习击球——而这本质上才是比赛的全部。LeetCode 也是如此。所以我采取了完全相反的方法：在学习主题之前就开始做题。然后，在完成题目或实在解不出时，再去学习高效的解法以及该主题相关的理论。

### My High-Level Process
### 我的核心流程

*   Spend 30–60 min per day on two problems for about 6 weeks straight first thing in the morning.
*   Give myself 20 minutes to solve each problem. If I couldn’t do it in that time frame, use the remaining 10 minutes to look through the solution.
*   When looking through the solution, I focussed on learning the pattern, not just the answer. This is so key, memorising solutions do not help you in the long run.
*   Close the solution, wait 5 minutes, and try to solve the problem again. Rinse and repeat.
*   每天早上第一件事，花 30-60 分钟做两道题，持续约 6 周。
*   给自己 20 分钟解决每道题。如果在这个时间内做不出来，就利用剩下的 10 分钟查看答案。
*   查看答案时，我专注于学习解题模式，而不仅仅是答案。这一点至关重要，死记硬背答案对长远没有帮助。
*   关闭答案，等待 5 分钟，然后再次尝试解决该问题。循环往复。

This approach transformed my understanding of DSA because it forced me to learn from doing, and to put myself through a so-called “mental sweat” as Andrej Karpathy calls it. Passively watching courses and consuming books feels productive, but it’s actually procrastination — avoiding the actual work of solving problems and learning through failure.
这种方法改变了我对 DSA 的理解，因为它迫使我从实践中学习，并让自己经历 Andrej Karpathy 所说的“脑力流汗”。被动地观看课程和阅读书籍感觉很充实，但实际上这是一种拖延——逃避了解决问题和通过失败来学习的实际工作。

### Stop Practising All Topics
### 停止练习所有主题

Now, I don’t want you to go ahead and solve any random problem on Leetcode, but rather focus on a select few topics that appear the most. When it comes to data science and machine learning roles, the DSA knowledge doesn’t need to be as extensive as it is for software engineering positions, even if you are going for senior jobs. In reality, only certain topics appear frequently in interviews, which are:
现在，我不想让你去盲目地做 LeetCode 上的随机题目，而是专注于出现频率最高的几个主题。对于数据科学和机器学习岗位，DSA 知识不需要像软件工程岗位那样广泛，即使你申请的是高级职位。实际上，面试中只有某些主题频繁出现，它们是：

*   **Arrays & Hashing** — Use hash maps/sets for O(1) lookups to avoid rescanning data.
*   **Two Pointers** — Move two indices toward each other (usually in sorted data) to skip nested loops.
*   **Sliding Window** — Expand/shrink a contiguous range to track the best subarray/substring efficiently.
*   **Linked List** — Rewire node pointers carefully, often with fast/slow pointers or a dummy head.
*   **Binary Search** — Halve the search space when data (or the answer) is sorted/monotonic.
*   **Stacks** — LIFO structure for matching/undoing things or tracking “next greater/smaller” elements.
*   **Trees** — Traverse recursively (DFS) or level-by-level (BFS) to compute or search hierarchical data.
*   **Heaps / Priority Queues** — Get the min/max fast, ideal for top-k or “process most urgent next” problems.
*   **Graphs** — Traverse nodes/edges via DFS/BFS to explore paths, connectivity, or shortest routes.
*   **数组与哈希** — 使用哈希映射/集合进行 O(1) 查找，避免重复扫描数据。
*   **双指针** — 将两个索引向彼此移动（通常在已排序数据中），以跳过嵌套循环。
*   **滑动窗口** — 扩展/收缩连续范围，以高效跟踪最佳子数组/子字符串。
*   **链表** — 小心重连节点指针，通常使用快慢指针或虚拟头节点。
*   **二分查找** — 当数据（或答案）已排序/单调时，将搜索空间减半。
*   **栈** — 用于匹配/撤销操作或跟踪“下一个更大/更小”元素的后进先出结构。
*   **树** — 通过递归（DFS）或逐层（BFS）遍历来计算或搜索分层数据。
*   **堆/优先队列** — 快速获取最小值/最大值，非常适合 Top-K 或“处理最紧急任务”类问题。
*   **图** — 通过 DFS/BFS 遍历节点/边，以探索路径、连通性或最短路线。

Please focus only on these topics. Topics like dynamic programming, tries, and bit manipulation are very tough to learn and don’t appear frequently in interviews. That time spent learning these advanced topics is better focussed in other areas of the process like system design or behavioural interview prep. To be honest, I have only ever practised 40 Leetcode problems and that allowed me to pass over 90% of my coding interviews, some of which were for senior machine learning roles.
请只关注这些主题。像动态规划、字典树和位运算等主题学习难度很大，且在面试中出现频率不高。将学习这些高级主题的时间花在面试流程的其他领域（如系统设计或行为面试准备）会更有价值。老实说，我总共只练习了 40 道 LeetCode 题目，这让我通过了 90% 以上的编程面试，其中一些还是高级机器学习岗位的面试。
